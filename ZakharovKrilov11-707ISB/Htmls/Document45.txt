	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" >Ward Christensen</h1>
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Computer pioneer, invented Xmodem and co-invented BBS</div>
<table class="infobox biography vcard" style="width:22em"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;font-size:125%;font-weight:bold"><div class="fn" style="display:inline">Ward Christensen</div></th></tr><tr><td colspan="2" style="text-align:center"><a href="/wiki/File:Ward_Christensen_and_the_First_BBS.jpg" class="image" title="Ward Christensen and the First BBS"><img alt="Ward Christensen and the First BBS" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Ward_Christensen_and_the_First_BBS.jpg/220px-Ward_Christensen_and_the_First_BBS.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="105" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Ward_Christensen_and_the_First_BBS.jpg/330px-Ward_Christensen_and_the_First_BBS.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Ward_Christensen_and_the_First_BBS.jpg/440px-Ward_Christensen_and_the_First_BBS.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="975" /></a><div>Ward Christensen and the First BBS</div></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Born</th><td><span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday">1945-10-23</span>) </span>October 23, 1945<span class="noprint ForceAgeToShow"> (age&#160;75)</span><br /><div style="display:inline" class="birthplace"><a href="/wiki/West_Bend,_Wisconsin" title="West Bend, Wisconsin">West Bend, Wisconsin</a>, United States</div></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Known&#160;for</th><td>first <a href="/wiki/Bulletin_board_system" title="Bulletin board system">bulletin board system</a> (BBS)<br /><a href="/wiki/XMODEM" title="XMODEM">XMODEM</a> Protocol</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Ward Christensen</b> (born 1945 in <a href="/wiki/West_Bend,_Wisconsin" title="West Bend, Wisconsin">West Bend, Wisconsin</a>, United States) is the co-founder of the <a href="/wiki/CBBS" title="CBBS">CBBS</a> bulletin board, the first <a href="/wiki/Bulletin_board_system" title="Bulletin board system">bulletin board system</a> (BBS) ever brought online. Christensen, along with partner <a href="/wiki/Randy_Suess" title="Randy Suess">Randy Suess</a>.,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> started development during a blizzard in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, and officially established CBBS four weeks later, on February 16, 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Christensen was noted for building <a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a> tools for his needs. He wrote a cassette-based <a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">operating system</a> before <a href="/wiki/Floppy_disk" title="Floppy disk">floppies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hard_disk_drive" title="Hard disk drive">hard disks</a> were common. When he lost track of the <a href="/wiki/Source_code" title="Source code">source code</a> for some <a href="/wiki/Computer_program" title="Computer program">programs</a> he wrote ReSource, an iterative <a href="/wiki/Disassembler" title="Disassembler">disassembler</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Intel_8080" title="Intel 8080">Intel 8080</a>, to help him regenerate the <a href="/wiki/Source_code" title="Source code">source code</a>. When he needed to send files to Randy Suess he wrote <a href="/wiki/XMODEM" title="XMODEM">XMODEM</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jerry_Pournelle" title="Jerry Pournelle">Jerry Pournelle</a> wrote in 1983 of a collection of <a href="/wiki/CP/M" title="CP/M">CP/M</a> <a href="/wiki/Public-domain_software" title="Public-domain software">public-domain software</a> that "probably 50 percent of the really good programs were written by Ward Christensen, a public benefactor."<sup id="cite_ref-pournelle198307_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pournelle198307-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Christensen received two 1992 <a href="/wiki/Dvorak_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Dvorak Awards">Dvorak Awards for Excellence in Telecommunications</a>, one with Randy Suess for developing the first BBS, and a lifetime achievement award "for outstanding contributions to PC telecommunications."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 1993, he received the <a href="/wiki/EFF_Pioneer_Award" title="EFF Pioneer Award">Pioneer Award</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Frontier_Foundation" title="Electronic Frontier Foundation">Electronic Frontier Foundation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Christensen worked at <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> from 1968<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> until his retirement in 2012.  His last position with IBM was field technical sales specialist.
</p><p>In May 2005, Christensen and Suess were both featured in <i><a href="/wiki/BBS:_The_Documentary" title="BBS: The Documentary">BBS: The Documentary</a></i>.
</p>
