<p><b>Dries Buytaert</b> (born 19 November 1978)<sup id="cite_ref-cv_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cv-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a Belgian <a href="/wiki/Open-source_software" title="Open-source software">open-source software</a> <a href="/wiki/Programmer" title="Programmer">programmer</a> notable as founder and lead developer of the <a href="/wiki/Drupal" title="Drupal">Drupal</a> content management system.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dries_Buytaert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Buytaert was born in <a href="/wiki/Wilrijk" title="Wilrijk">Wilrijk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antwerp" title="Antwerp">Antwerp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cv_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cv-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Buytaert defended his PhD dissertation in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> on 27 January 2008,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> at <a href="/wiki/Ghent_University" title="Ghent University">Ghent University</a> in Belgium.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 1999 to 2000 he was the maintainer of the <i><a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a>-<a href="/wiki/Wireless_LAN" title="Wireless LAN">WLAN</a> FAQ</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 December 2007, Dries announced,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> together with co-founder <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jay_Batson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jay Batson (page does not exist)">Jay Batson</a> the launch of a start-up called <a href="/wiki/Acquia" title="Acquia">Acquia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Acquia is a commercial open-source software company providing products, services, and technical support for Drupal. Acquia tries to be to Drupal what <a href="/wiki/Red_Hat" title="Red Hat">Red Hat</a> has been to <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, Acquia helped re-launch Whitehouse.gov on Drupal.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Dries serves as CTO of Acquia.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 31 March 2008, Dries launched <a href="/wiki/Mollom" title="Mollom">Mollom</a>, a service dedicated to stopping website spam: "Mollom's purpose is to dramatically reduce the effort of keeping your site clean and the quality of your content high. Currently, Mollom is a spam-killing one-two punch combination of a state-of-the-art spam filter and CAPTCHA server." Over 59,000 websites are protected by the Mollom service,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> including all of <a href="/wiki/Netlog" title="Netlog">Netlog</a>'s messages.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Mollom support ended 2 April 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2008, Buytaert was elected "Young Entrepreneurs of Tech" by <a href="/wiki/BusinessWeek" class="mw-redirect" title="BusinessWeek">BusinessWeek</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> He was also named to the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">MIT</a> <a href="/wiki/Technology_Review" class="mw-redirect" title="Technology Review">Technology Review</a> <a href="/wiki/TR35" class="mw-redirect" title="TR35">TR35</a> as one of the top 35 innovators in the world under the age of 35.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
