<p><b>Alain Colmerauer</b> (24 January 1941 – 12 May 2017) was a French <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a>. He was a professor at <a href="/wiki/Aix-Marseille_University" title="Aix-Marseille University">Aix-Marseille University</a>, and the creator of the <a href="/wiki/Logic_programming" title="Logic programming">logic programming</a> language <a href="/wiki/Prolog" title="Prolog">Prolog</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alain_Colmerauer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alain Colmerauer was born on 24 January 1941 in <a href="/wiki/Carcassonne" title="Carcassonne">Carcassonne</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-idrefcolmerauer_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-idrefcolmerauer-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Grenoble_Institute_of_Technology" title="Grenoble Institute of Technology">Grenoble Institute of Technology</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-cohen637_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cohen637-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and he earned a PhD from the <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_nationale_sup%C3%A9rieure_d%27informatique_et_de_math%C3%A9matiques_appliqu%C3%A9es_de_Grenoble" title="École nationale supérieure d&#39;informatique et de mathématiques appliquées de Grenoble">Ensimag</a> in <a href="/wiki/Grenoble" title="Grenoble">Grenoble</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-associationforlogicprogrammingobit_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-associationforlogicprogrammingobit-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alain_Colmerauer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Colmerauer spent 1967–1970 as assistant professor at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Montreal" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Montreal">University of Montreal</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-associationforlogicprogrammingobit_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-associationforlogicprogrammingobit-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> where he created <a href="/wiki/Q-systems" title="Q-systems">Q-Systems</a>, one of the earliest linguistic formalisms used in the development of the TAUM-METEO <a href="/wiki/Machine_translation" title="Machine translation">machine translation</a> prototype.<sup id="cite_ref-cohen637_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cohen637-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Developing Prolog III in 1984, he was one of the main founders of the field of <a href="/wiki/Constraint_logic_programming" title="Constraint logic programming">constraint logic programming</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cohen637_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cohen637-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Colmerauer became an associate professor at <a href="/wiki/Aix-Marseille_University" title="Aix-Marseille University">Aix-Marseille University</a> in Luminy in 1970. He was promoted to full professor in 1979. From 1993 to 1995, he was head of the Laboratoire d'Informatique de Marseille (LIM), a joint laboratory of the Centre National de la Recherche Scientifique, the Université de Provence and the Université de la Méditerranée.<sup id="cite_ref-associationforlogicprogrammingobit_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-associationforlogicprogrammingobit-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Despite retiring as emeritus professor in 2006,<sup id="cite_ref-associationforlogicprogrammingobit_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-associationforlogicprogrammingobit-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> he remained a member of the artificial intelligence taskforce in Luminy.<sup id="cite_ref-bnf_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bnf-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Colmerauer won an award from the <a href="/wiki/Regional_council_(France)" title="Regional council (France)">regional council</a> of <a href="/wiki/Provence-Alpes-C%C3%B4te_d%27Azur" title="Provence-Alpes-Côte d&#39;Azur">Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur</a>, and in 1985 the <a href="/wiki/Michel_Monpetit_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Michel Monpetit Award">Michel Monpetit Award</a>, from the <a href="/wiki/French_Academy_of_Sciences" title="French Academy of Sciences">French Academy of Sciences</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bnfprixdecommissions_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bnfprixdecommissions-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 1986, he was made a knight of the <a href="/wiki/Legion_of_Honour" title="Legion of Honour">Legion of Honour</a> by the French government.<sup id="cite_ref-associationforlogicprogrammingobit_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-associationforlogicprogrammingobit-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He became Fellow of the American Association for Artificial Intelligence in 1991,<sup id="cite_ref-aaaielected_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aaaielected-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and received the Association for Constraint Programming's Research Excellence Award in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-associationresearchexcellenceaward_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-associationresearchexcellenceaward-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He was also a correspondent of the <a href="/wiki/French_Academy_of_Sciences" title="French Academy of Sciences">French Academy of Sciences</a> in the area of mathematics.<sup id="cite_ref-academiedessciences_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-academiedessciences-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alain_Colmerauer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Colmerauer died on 12 May 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-associationforlogicprogrammingobit_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-associationforlogicprogrammingobit-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ACM_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ACM-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
