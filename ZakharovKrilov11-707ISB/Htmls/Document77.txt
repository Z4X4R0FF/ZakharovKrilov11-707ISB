	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" >Stuart Feldman</h1>
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		<div id="contentSub2"></div>
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#searchInput">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For the American lobbyist and social activist, see <a href="/wiki/Stuart_F._Feldman" title="Stuart F. Feldman">Stuart F. Feldman</a>.</div>
<table class="infobox biography vcard" style="width:22em"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;font-size:125%;font-weight:bold"><div class="fn" style="display:inline">Stuart Feldman</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Known&#160;for</th><td><a href="/wiki/Make_(software)" title="Make (software)">make</a>;<br />President of <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">ACM</a>, 2006–08<sup id="cite_ref-ACM_pp_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ACM_pp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Stuart Feldman</b> is the creator of the <a href="/wiki/Computer_software" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer software">computer software</a> program <a href="/wiki/Make_(software)" title="Make (software)">make</a> for <a href="/wiki/Unix" title="Unix">UNIX</a> systems. He was also an author of the first <a href="/wiki/Fortran_77" class="mw-redirect" title="Fortran 77">Fortran 77</a> <a href="/wiki/Compiler" title="Compiler">compiler</a>, and he was part of the original group at <a href="/wiki/Bell_Labs" title="Bell Labs">Bell Labs</a> that created the Unix <a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">operating system</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-reader_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reader-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Feldman is head of Schmidt Sciences at the foundation Schmidt Philanthropies, and a member of the dean's External Advisory Board at the University of Michigan's School of Information.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He was previously Vice President, Engineering, East Coast, at <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, and before that Vice President of Computer Science at <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> Research. Feldman has served on the board of the <a href="/wiki/Computing_Research_Association" title="Computing Research Association">Computing Research Association</a> (CRA) and of the <a href="/wiki/Association_to_Advance_Collegiate_Schools_of_Business" title="Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business">Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business</a> (AACSB International). He was chair of <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">ACM</a> <a href="/wiki/SIGPLAN" title="SIGPLAN">SIGPLAN</a> and founding chair of ACM SIGecom. He was elected the President of the ACM in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-ACM_pp_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ACM_pp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Feldman is also a member of the Editorial Advisory Board of <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20050106042350/http://acmqueue.com/">ACM Queue</a></i>, a magazine he helped found with <a href="/wiki/Stephen_R._Bourne" title="Stephen R. Bourne">Steve Bourne</a>.  He has also served on the editorial boards of <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Electrical_and_Electronics_Engineers" title="Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers">IEEE</a> Internet Computing and IEEE Transactions on Software Engineering.
He received an <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">A.B.</a> in <a href="/wiki/Astrophysical_sciences" class="mw-redirect" title="Astrophysical sciences">astrophysical sciences</a> from <a href="/wiki/Princeton_University" title="Princeton University">Princeton University</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Ph.D" class="mw-redirect" title="Ph.D">Ph.D</a> in <a href="/wiki/Applied_mathematics" title="Applied mathematics">applied mathematics</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a>. 
Feldman became a <a href="/wiki/Fellow" title="Fellow">Fellow</a> of the IEEE in 1991 and a Fellow of the ACM in 1995. In 2003, he was awarded ACM's <a href="/wiki/Software_System_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Software System Award">Software System Award</a> for his creation of make.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Feldman also participated in development of the <a href="/wiki/ALTRAN" title="ALTRAN">ALTRAN</a> programming language.
</p>
