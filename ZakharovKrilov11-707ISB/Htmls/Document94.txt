<p><b>Jagex Limited</b> is a British <a href="/wiki/Video_game_developer" title="Video game developer">video game developer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Video_game_publisher" title="Video game publisher">publisher</a> based at the <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Science_Park" title="Cambridge Science Park">Cambridge Science Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge" title="Cambridge">Cambridge</a>, England. It is best known for <i><a href="/wiki/RuneScape" title="RuneScape">RuneScape</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Old_School_RuneScape" title="Old School RuneScape">Old School RuneScape</a></i>, collectively known as the world's largest <a href="/wiki/Free-to-play" title="Free-to-play">free-to-play</a> <a href="/wiki/Massively_multiplayer_online_role-playing_game" title="Massively multiplayer online role-playing game">massively multiplayer online role-playing games</a>. The company's name is derived from the company's original slogan, "<b>Ja</b>va <b>G</b>aming <b>Ex</b>perts". In addition to <i>RuneScape</i>, Jagex has released multiple <a href="/wiki/Casual_game" title="Casual game">casual games</a> on its <a href="/wiki/FunOrb" title="FunOrb">FunOrb</a> <a href="/wiki/Game_portal" class="mw-redirect" title="Game portal">portal</a>, as well as other titles. Jagex was owned by U.S. investors between 2012 and 2016, by Chinese investors from 2016–2020, by Macarthur Fortune Holding LLC for about a year in 2020, and is presently owned by <a href="/wiki/The_Carlyle_Group" title="The Carlyle Group">The Carlyle Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jagex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jagex_Logo.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/fb/Jagex_Logo.png/220px-Jagex_Logo.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="75" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/f/fb/Jagex_Logo.png 1.5x" data-file-width="254" data-file-height="87" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jagex_Logo.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An early Jagex logo</div></div></div>
<p>After initially creating the Jagex name and logo for their projects, brothers and computer programmers <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Gower" title="Andrew Gower">Andrew Gower</a> and Paul Gower began trading under the Jagex name in 1999, describing Jagex Software as a "small software company based in England who specialise in producing top-quality Java-games for webpages." That same year they began work on the <a href="/wiki/MMORPG" class="mw-redirect" title="MMORPG">MMORPG</a> <i>RuneScape</i>, which was released in January 2001. In December 2001, <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Gower" title="Andrew Gower">Andrew Gower</a>, Paul Gower, and Constant Tedder launched Jagex in its current incarnation, with Tedder as its <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEO</a>. Jagex formally acquired the Jagex name from Andrew Gower in 2001.
</p><p><i>RuneScape</i> grew dramatically; one year after its release over a million free accounts had been registered. The game was originally supported by advertisements, however, the <a href="/wiki/Dot-com_bubble" title="Dot-com bubble">Dot-com bubble</a> meant that there were fewer advertisers willing or able to sign with Jagex. One of the first tasks of the new company was to create a paid version of the game with extra features, to support hosting costs and continued development. This was achieved on 27 February 2002 when the <a href="/wiki/Pay-to-play" class="mw-redirect" title="Pay-to-play">pay-to-play</a> version of <i>RuneScape</i> was released. It gained 5,000 subscribers in the first week, making it one of the largest Java pay-to-play games in the world at the time.
</p><p>Until the release of <i><a href="/wiki/War_of_Legends" title="War of Legends">War of Legends</a></i> in 2010, the company used the slogan "Java Gaming Experts" as it had only produced games written in <a href="/wiki/Java_(programming_language)" title="Java (programming language)">Java</a> up to that point. Following the launch of the <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Flash" title="Adobe Flash">Flash</a>-based <i>War of Legends</i>, the company name was said to stand for "Just About the Game Experience".<sup id="cite_ref-MCVUK_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCVUK-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Company_structure">Company structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jagex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Company structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As <i>RuneScape</i> gained users, Jagex grew its employee base. By 11 December 2003, <i>RuneScape</i> had 65,000 paying members, and Jagex had 29 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-RuneToMove_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RuneToMove-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On 4 May 2007, <i>RuneScape</i> had over 6,000,000 active free accounts and over 1,000,000 active pay-to-play subscribers. In July 2012, Jagex had over 500 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-Employees2012_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Employees2012-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 23 October 2007, Geoff Iddison, former European CEO of <a href="/wiki/PayPal" title="PayPal">PayPal</a>, replaced Constant Tedder as CEO in order to "accelerate international growth." Iddison resigned as CEO in January 2009 and was replaced by Mark Gerhard, who had been Jagex <a href="/wiki/Chief_technology_officer" title="Chief technology officer">CTO</a> before his appointment. Jagex became a member of <a href="/wiki/The_Independent_Games_Developers_Association" class="mw-redirect" title="The Independent Games Developers Association">TIGA</a>, the United Kingdom's game developer trade body, on 15 April 2009. Richard Wilson, TIGA's CEO, described Jagex as "one of the most successful game developers in the world, not just the UK. Jagex has developed extraordinarily popular games and is at the leading edge in terms of online safety and security."
</p><p>Jagex received an investment from <a href="/wiki/Insight_Venture_Partners" class="mw-redirect" title="Insight Venture Partners">Insight Venture Partners</a> in October 2005, before which the company had been self-funded. In December 2010 The Raine Group and <a href="/wiki/Spectrum_Equity_Investors" class="mw-redirect" title="Spectrum Equity Investors">Spectrum Equity Investors</a> invested in Jagex, while Insight increased their investment. Andrew Gower, Paul Gower, and Constant Tedder left the board of directors at that time.<sup id="cite_ref-PCGames_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PCGames-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In January 2012, Insight increased its stake in Jagex from 35% to 55%, giving it a controlling interest in the company.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> However, in an interview, Gerhard stated that Insight took their 55% stake 13 months prior in December 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On 11 September 2014, Mark Gerhard announced his resignation from Jagex by 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On 2 April 2015, Rod Cousens was appointed CEO of Jagex following his departure from Codemasters.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In July 2016 it was announced that Jagex was purchased by Hongtou, a Chinese investment company. Hongtou was subsequently acquired by Zhongji Holdings, which later changed its name to Fukong Interactive Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Jagex remained under Hongtou within Fukong's structure until it was sold to Macarthur Fortune Holding, a US-based asset management company, in April 2020. Macarthur Fortune Holding, through its Platinum Fortune fund, paid <span style="white-space: nowrap">$530&#160;million</span> for this acquisition.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Phil Mansell became Jagex' CEO in January 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/The_Carlyle_Group" title="The Carlyle Group">The Carlyle Group</a> acquired a majority stake in Jagex in January 2021 from Macarthur in a deal for an undisclosed amount, though <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i> estimated was greater than then <span style="white-space: nowrap">$530 million</span> Macarthur had paid for previously.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> However, after announcement of this, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Plutos_Sama_Holdings&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Plutos Sama Holdings (page does not exist)">Plutos Sama Holdings</a> filed a legal complaint asserting that the sale of Jagex from Shanghai Hongtou to Macarthur was invalid related to past issues over business dealings with Shanghai Hongtou. Plutos Sama stated in their complaint they had put in a sole bid to acquire Hongtou, including Jagex, around August 2020, but this deal never was complete, but as it should have gone through, they are the rightful owners of Jagex.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Charity_fundraising">Charity fundraising</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jagex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Charity fundraising">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since 2004 Jagex has made donations to a number of national and international charities, as well as running charity auctions for signed merchandise. In 2008 they donated artwork and prizes to the MMOCalendar, which raises funds for <a href="/wiki/St._Jude_Children%27s_Research_Hospital" title="St. Jude Children&#39;s Research Hospital">St. Jude Children's Research Hospital</a>.
</p><p>In 2011, Jagex donated a total of £150,076 to a number of local and US-based charities.
</p><p>In 2013, Jagex introduced the "Well of Goodwill" to <i>RuneScape</i>, which allowed players to gift in-game items or wealth to charity. The Well of Goodwill also featured a hi-scores page for the players who donated. For every 10 million gold pieces gifted by the community, Jagex donated $2 to a number of charitable causes. On the weekend of 21 to 24 February 2014, Jagex reopened the well of goodwill to donations. They also hosted livestreams for the then-active double experience weekend and released a limited-offer virtual purchase to raise money for SpecialEffect, a charity geared towards making games accessible to children with disabilities.
</p><p>In July 2014, Jagex helped raise awareness about the illegal poaching of <a href="/wiki/Black_rhino" class="mw-redirect" title="Black rhino">black</a> and <a href="/wiki/White_rhino" class="mw-redirect" title="White rhino">white rhinos</a> by adding rhinoceros pets and trivia questions to <i>RuneScape</i>. This campaign was partnershipped with United for Wildlife.
</p><p>In November 2014, Jagex reopened the Well of Goodwill to support 3 new charities: AbleGamers, DonateGames, and YoungMinds.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Games">Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jagex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Developed">Developed</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jagex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Developed">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="RuneScape"><i>RuneScape</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jagex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: RuneScape">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/RuneScape" title="RuneScape">RuneScape</a></div>
<p><i>RuneScape</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Fantasy" title="Fantasy">fantasy</a> <a href="/wiki/Massively_multiplayer_online_role-playing_game" title="Massively multiplayer online role-playing game">MMORPG</a> released in January 2001 by <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Gower" title="Andrew Gower">Andrew</a> and Paul Gower.<sup id="cite_ref-RuneToMove_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RuneToMove-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It is a <a href="/wiki/Graphical_game" class="mw-redirect" title="Graphical game">graphical</a> <a href="/wiki/Browser_game" title="Browser game">browser game</a> implemented on the <a href="/wiki/Client_(computing)" title="Client (computing)">client-side</a> in <a href="/wiki/Java_(programming_language)" title="Java (programming language)">Java</a>, and incorporates <a href="/wiki/3D_rendering" title="3D rendering">3D rendering</a>. The game has over 200&#160;million registered accounts,<sup id="cite_ref-200Million_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-200Million-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and is recognised by the <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Records</a> as the world's most popular <a href="/wiki/Free-to-play" title="Free-to-play">free-to-play</a> MMORPG.
</p><p><i>RuneScape</i> takes place in the world of Gielinor, a medieval fantasy realm divided into different kingdoms, regions, and cities. Each region offers different types of monsters, resources, and quests to challenge players. The game's <a href="/wiki/Fictional_universe" title="Fictional universe">fictional universe</a> has also been explored through a <a href="/wiki/Tie-in" title="Tie-in">tie-in</a> video game on its maker's other website, <i><a href="/wiki/FunOrb" title="FunOrb">FunOrb</a></i>, <i>Armies of Gielinor</i>, and the novels <i>Betrayal at Falador</i>, <i>Return to Canifis</i> and <i>Legacy of Blood</i>.
</p><p>Players are represented in the game with customisable <a href="/wiki/Avatar_(computing)" title="Avatar (computing)">avatars</a>. <i>RuneScape</i> does not follow a <a href="/wiki/Linear_gameplay" class="mw-redirect" title="Linear gameplay">linear</a> storyline; rather, players set their own goals and objectives. Players can choose to fight <a href="/wiki/Non-player_character" title="Non-player character">non-player character</a> (NPC) monsters, complete <a href="/wiki/Quest_(gaming)" class="mw-redirect" title="Quest (gaming)">quests</a>, or increase their <a href="/wiki/Experience_point" title="Experience point">experience</a> in the available skills.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJKnights_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJKnights-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Players interact with each other through trading, <a href="/wiki/Conversation" title="Conversation">chatting</a>, or by participating in <a href="/wiki/Mini-games" class="mw-redirect" title="Mini-games">mini-games</a> and activities, some of which are competitive or combative in nature, while others require cooperative or collaborative play.
</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Old_School_RuneScape"><i>Old School RuneScape</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jagex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Old School RuneScape">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Old_School_RuneScape" title="Old School RuneScape">Old School RuneScape</a></div>
<p><i>Old School RuneScape</i> is a separate incarnation of <i>RuneScape</i> released on 22 February 2013, based on a copy of the game from August 2007. It was opened to paying subscribers after a poll to determine the level of support for releasing this game passed 50,000 votes (totaling 449,351 votes<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>), followed by a free-to-play version on 19 February 2015. <i>Old School RuneScape</i> receives regular content updates, which must be voted on by its players before they can be added to the game.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> On 17 July 2017, Jagex announced the development of a mobile version of <i>Old School RuneScape</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="DarkScape"><i>DarkScape</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jagex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: DarkScape">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>On 16 September 2015 Jagex released <i>DarkScape</i>, a separate version of <i>RuneScape</i> which featured open-world player versus player combat. <i>DarkScape</i> was originally released with most of <i>RuneScape's</i> content, but received separate content updates. <i>DarkScape</i> was completely free to play, with some additional benefits reserved for paying subscribers. On 29 February 2016 it was announced that <i>DarkScape</i> would close on 28 March due to lack of interest.
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="FunOrb"><i>FunOrb</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jagex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: FunOrb">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="8Realms"><i>8Realms</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jagex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 8Realms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/8Realms" title="8Realms">8Realms</a></div>
<p><i>8Realms</i> was an <a href="/wiki/HTML_based_game" class="mw-redirect" title="HTML based game">HTML-based</a> empire-building <a href="/wiki/MMORTS" class="mw-redirect" title="MMORTS">massively multiplayer online strategy game</a> developed by Jagex.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> It was the company's first internally developed <a href="/wiki/MMORTS" class="mw-redirect" title="MMORTS">MMORTS</a>, and second published MMORTS after <i><a href="/wiki/War_of_Legends" title="War of Legends">War of Legends</a></i>.
</p><p>The <i>8Realms</i> <a href="/wiki/Software_release_life_cycle#Open_and_closed_beta" title="Software release life cycle">closed beta</a> was released on 5 May 2011; players were given advanced access to the closed beta by invitation and through the game's <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> page. On 28 May 2012, Jagex announced they were closing the game, stating that "it has become clear that the game doesn’t meet our high expectations for success."<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Stellar_Dawn"><i>Stellar Dawn</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jagex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Stellar Dawn">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Stellar_Dawn" title="Stellar Dawn">Stellar Dawn</a></div>
<p><i>Stellar Dawn</i> was an upcoming browser-based <a href="/wiki/Sci-fi" class="mw-redirect" title="Sci-fi">sci-fi</a> themed <a href="/wiki/MMORPG" class="mw-redirect" title="MMORPG">MMORPG</a> under development by Jagex. Originally known as <i>MechScape</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> the project was scrapped and renamed after it was decided that the completed project did not meet the standards of the original <a href="/wiki/Design_brief" title="Design brief">design brief</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The project was overhauled and formally announced as <i>Stellar Dawn</i> on 14 July 2010 when Jagex released the official <i>Stellar Dawn</i> website. On 10 August 2010 the first official <i>Stellar Dawn</i> teaser was released. The game was slated for a 2011 launch, but in March 2012 Jagex announced that development had been paused in favour of <i>Transformers Universe</i> and <i>RuneScape</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="RuneScape:_Idle_Adventures"><i>RuneScape: Idle Adventures</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jagex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: RuneScape: Idle Adventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><i>RuneScape: Idle Adventures</i> was an <a href="/wiki/Incremental_game" title="Incremental game">idle game</a> set in the <i>RuneScape</i> universe developed by <a href="/wiki/Hyper_Hippo_Productions" title="Hyper Hippo Productions">Hyper Hippo Productions</a>. A closed beta was opened on Steam on 23 April 2016,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> then was subsequently shutdown on 15 May 2017<sup id="cite_ref-RSIdleAdventuresClosure_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RSIdleAdventuresClosure-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> due to lack of users.
</p>
