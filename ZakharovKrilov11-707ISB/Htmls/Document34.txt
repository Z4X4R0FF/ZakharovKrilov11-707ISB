<p><b>Jack Elton Bresenham</b> (born 11 October 1937, <a href="/wiki/Clovis,_New_Mexico" title="Clovis, New Mexico">Clovis, New Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">US</a>) is a former professor of <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jack_Elton_Bresenham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bresenham retired from 27 years of service at <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> as a Senior Technical Staff Member in 1987. He taught for 16 years at <a href="/wiki/Winthrop_University" title="Winthrop University">Winthrop University</a> and has nine <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patents</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He has four children: Janet, Craig, Linda, and David.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Bresenham%27s_line_algorithm" title="Bresenham&#39;s line algorithm">Bresenham's line algorithm</a>, developed in 1962, is his most well-known innovation. It determines which points on a 2-dimensional <a href="/wiki/Raster_graphics" title="Raster graphics">raster</a> should be plotted in order to form a straight line between two given points, and is commonly used to draw lines on a computer screen.  It is one of the earliest algorithms discovered in the field of <a href="/wiki/Computer_graphics" title="Computer graphics">computer graphics</a>.  The <a href="/wiki/Midpoint_circle_algorithm" title="Midpoint circle algorithm">midpoint circle algorithm</a> shares some similarities to his line algorithm and is known as <i>Bresenham's circle algorithm</i>.
</p>
