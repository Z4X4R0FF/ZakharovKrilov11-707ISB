<p><b>Stephen Richard</b> "<b>Steve</b>" <b>Bourne</b> (born 7 January 1944) is an English <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a> based in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> for most of his career. He is well known as the author of the <a href="/wiki/Bourne_shell" title="Bourne shell">Bourne shell</a> (<code>sh</code>), which is the foundation for the standard <a href="/wiki/Command-line_interface" title="Command-line interface">command-line interfaces</a> to <a href="/wiki/Unix" title="Unix">Unix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stephen_R._Bourne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bourne has a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">Bachelor of Science</a> (B.S.) degree in <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a> from <a href="/wiki/King%27s_College_London" title="King&#39;s College London">King's College London</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. He has a <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Diploma_in_Computer_Science" title="Cambridge Diploma in Computer Science">Diploma in Computer Science</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Doctor of Philosophy</a> (Ph.D.) in mathematics from <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Cambridge" title="Trinity College, Cambridge">Trinity College, Cambridge</a>. Subsequently, he worked on an <a href="/wiki/ALGOL_68" title="ALGOL 68">ALGOL 68</a> <a href="/wiki/Compiler" title="Compiler">compiler</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge_Computer_Laboratory" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Cambridge Computer Laboratory">University of Cambridge Computer Laboratory</a> (see <a href="/wiki/ALGOL_68C" title="ALGOL 68C">ALGOL 68C</a>). He also worked on <a href="/wiki/CAMAL" class="mw-redirect" title="CAMAL">CAMAL</a>, a system for algebraic manipulation used for <a href="/wiki/Lunar_theory" title="Lunar theory">lunar theory</a> calculations.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a>, Bourne spent nine years at <a href="/wiki/Bell_Labs" title="Bell Labs">Bell Labs</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Seventh_Edition_Unix" class="mw-redirect" title="Seventh Edition Unix">Seventh Edition Unix</a> team.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Besides the Bourne shell, he wrote the <code><a href="/wiki/Advanced_Debugger" title="Advanced Debugger">adb</a></code> <a href="/wiki/Debugger" title="Debugger">debugger</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Unix_System" title="The Unix System">The Unix System</a></i>, the second book on the topic, intended for general readers.
</p><p>After Bell Labs, Bourne worked in senior <a href="/wiki/Engineering_management" title="Engineering management">engineering management</a> positions at <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Graphics" title="Silicon Graphics">Silicon Graphics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Digital_Equipment_Corporation" title="Digital Equipment Corporation">Digital Equipment Corporation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sun_Microsystems" title="Sun Microsystems">Sun Microsystems</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cisco_Systems" title="Cisco Systems">Cisco Systems</a>.
</p><p>He was involved with developing <a href="/wiki/International_standard" title="International standard">international standards</a> in programming and informatics, as a member of the <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_for_Information_Processing" title="International Federation for Information Processing">International Federation for Information Processing</a> (IFIP) <a href="/wiki/IFIP_Working_Group_2.1" title="IFIP Working Group 2.1">IFIP Working Group 2.1</a> on Algorithmic Languages and Calculi,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> which <a href="/wiki/Specification_(technical_standard)" title="Specification (technical standard)">specified</a>, maintains, and supports the <a href="/wiki/Programming_language" title="Programming language">programming languages</a> <a href="/wiki/ALGOL_60" title="ALGOL 60">ALGOL 60</a> and <a href="/wiki/ALGOL_68" title="ALGOL 68">ALGOL 68</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 2000 to 2002 he was president of the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a> (ACM).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> For his work on computing, Bourne was awarded the ACM's Presidential Award in 2008 and was made a <a href="/wiki/Fellow" title="Fellow">Fellow</a> of the organization in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He is also a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Astronomical_Society" title="Royal Astronomical Society">Royal Astronomical Society</a>.
</p><p>Bourne was chief technology officer at Icon Venture Partners, a venture capital firm based in <a href="/wiki/Menlo_Park,_California" title="Menlo Park, California">Menlo Park, California</a> through 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He is also <a href="/wiki/Chairperson" title="Chairperson">chairperson</a> of the editorial advisory board for <i><a href="/wiki/ACM_Queue" title="ACM Queue">ACM Queue</a></i>, a magazine he helped found when he was president of the ACM.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
