<p><b>Mike Cowlishaw</b> is a Visiting Professor at the Department of Computer Science at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Warwick" title="University of Warwick">University of Warwick</a>, and a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Engineering" title="Royal Academy of Engineering">Royal Academy of Engineering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He is a retired <a href="/wiki/IBM_Fellow" title="IBM Fellow">IBM Fellow</a>, and was a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Engineering_and_Technology" title="Institute of Engineering and Technology">Institute of Engineering and Technology</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/British_Computer_Society" title="British Computer Society">British Computer Society</a>. He was educated at <a href="/wiki/Monkton_Combe_School" title="Monkton Combe School">Monkton Combe School</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_University_of_Birmingham" class="mw-redirect" title="The University of Birmingham">The University of Birmingham</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_at_IBM">Career at IBM</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Cowlishaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career at IBM">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cowlishaw joined IBM in 1974 as an electronic engineer but is best known as a programmer and writer. He is known for designing and implementing the <a href="/wiki/REXX" class="mw-redirect" title="REXX">REXX</a> programming language (1984),<sup id="cite_ref-rexx_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rexx-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> his work on colour perception and image processing that led to the formation of <a href="/wiki/JPEG" title="JPEG">JPEG</a> (1985),<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/STET_(text_editor)" title="STET (text editor)">STET</a> <a href="/wiki/Folding_editor" class="mw-redirect" title="Folding editor">folding editor</a> (1977), the <a href="/wiki/LEXX_(text_editor)" title="LEXX (text editor)">LEXX</a> live parsing editor with <a href="/wiki/Syntax_highlighting" title="Syntax highlighting">colour highlighting</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Oxford_English_Dictionary" title="Oxford English Dictionary">Oxford English Dictionary</a> (1985),<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> electronic publishing, <a href="/wiki/Standard_Generalized_Markup_Language" title="Standard Generalized Markup Language">SGML</a> applications, the IBM <a href="/wiki/Jargon_File" title="Jargon File">Jargon File</a> <i>IBMJARG</i> (1990),<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> a programmable <a href="/wiki/OS/2" title="OS/2">OS/2</a> world globe <i>PMGlobe</i> (1993),<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <i>MemoWiki</i> based on his <i>GoServe</i> <a href="/wiki/Gopher_(protocol)" title="Gopher (protocol)">Gopher</a>/<a href="/wiki/Hypertext_Transfer_Protocol" title="Hypertext Transfer Protocol">http</a> server,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Java_(programming_language)" title="Java (programming language)">Java</a>-related <a href="/wiki/NetRexx" title="NetRexx">NetRexx</a> programming language (1997).
</p><p>He has contributed to and/or edited various computing standards, including <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">ISO</a> (<a href="/wiki/SGML" class="mw-redirect" title="SGML">SGML</a>, <a href="/wiki/COBOL" title="COBOL">COBOL</a>, <a href="/wiki/C_(programming_language)" title="C (programming language)">C</a>, <a href="/wiki/C%2B%2B" title="C++">C++</a>), <a href="/wiki/British_Standards_Institution" class="mw-redirect" title="British Standards Institution">BSI</a> (SGML, C), <a href="/wiki/American_National_Standards_Institute" title="American National Standards Institute">ANSI</a> (REXX), <a href="/wiki/IETF" class="mw-redirect" title="IETF">IETF</a> (<a href="/wiki/HTTP" class="mw-redirect" title="HTTP">HTTP</a> 1.0/<a class="external mw-magiclink-rfc" rel="nofollow" href="https://tools.ietf.org/html/rfc1945">RFC 1945</a>), <a href="/wiki/W3C" class="mw-redirect" title="W3C">W3C</a> (<a href="/wiki/XML_Schema_(W3C)" title="XML Schema (W3C)">XML Schema</a>), <a href="/wiki/ECMAScript" title="ECMAScript">ECMA</a> (<a href="/wiki/JavaScript" title="JavaScript">JavaScript</a>/<a href="/wiki/ECMAScript" title="ECMAScript">ECMAScript</a>, <a href="/wiki/C_Sharp_(programming_language)" title="C Sharp (programming language)">C#</a>, <a href="/wiki/Common_Language_Infrastructure" title="Common Language Infrastructure">CLI</a>), and <a href="/wiki/IEEE" class="mw-redirect" title="IEEE">IEEE</a> (<a href="/wiki/IEEE_754-2008" class="mw-redirect" title="IEEE 754-2008">754 decimal floating-point</a>). He retired from IBM in March 2010.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Decimal_arithmetic">Decimal arithmetic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Cowlishaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Decimal arithmetic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cowlishaw has worked on aspects of <a href="/wiki/Decimal" title="Decimal">decimal</a> arithmetic; his proposal for an improved Java BigDecimal class (<a href="/wiki/Java_Specification_Request" class="mw-redirect" title="Java Specification Request">JSR</a>&#160;13) is now included in Java&#160;5.0, and in 2002, he invented a refinement of <a href="/wiki/Chen%E2%80%93Ho_encoding" title="Chen–Ho encoding">Chen–Ho encoding</a> known as <a href="/wiki/Densely_packed_decimal" title="Densely packed decimal">densely packed decimal</a> encoding. Cowlishaw's decimal arithmetic specification formed the proposal for the decimal parts of the <a href="/wiki/IEEE_754" title="IEEE 754">IEEE 754 standard</a>, as well as being followed by many implementations, such as <a href="/wiki/Python_(programming_language)" title="Python (programming language)">Python</a> and <a href="/wiki/SAP_AG" class="mw-redirect" title="SAP AG">SAP</a> <a href="/wiki/Netweaver" class="mw-redirect" title="Netweaver">Netweaver</a>. His decNumber decimal package is also available as open source under several licenses and is now part of <a href="/wiki/GNU_Compiler_Collection" title="GNU Compiler Collection">GCC</a>, and his proposals for decimal hardware have been adopted by IBM and others.  They are integrated into the <a href="/wiki/POWER6" title="POWER6">IBM POWER6</a> and <a href="/wiki/IBM_System_z10" title="IBM System z10">IBM System z10</a> processor cores, and in numerous IBM software products such as <a href="/wiki/IBM_DB2" class="mw-redirect" title="IBM DB2">DB2</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transaction_Processing_Facility" title="Transaction Processing Facility">TPF</a> (in <a href="/wiki/Sabre_(computer_system)" title="Sabre (computer system)">Sabre</a>), <a href="/wiki/IBM_WebSphere_MQ" class="mw-redirect" title="IBM WebSphere MQ">WebSphere MQ</a>, operating systems, and C and PL/I compilers.<sup id="cite_ref-Cowlishaw_GDA_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cowlishaw_GDA-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_activities">Other activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Cowlishaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cowlishaw wrote an emulator for the <a href="/wiki/Acorn_System_1" title="Acorn System 1">Acorn System 1</a>, and collected related documentation.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Outside computing, he caved in the UK, New England, Spain,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and Mexico
<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and continues to cave and hike in Spain. He is a life member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Speleological_Society" title="National Speleological Society">National Speleological Society</a> (NSS), wrote articles in the 1970s and 1980s on battery technology and on the shock strength of caving ropes, and designed <a href="/wiki/LED" class="mw-redirect" title="LED">LED</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Caving_equipment" title="Caving equipment">caving lamps</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
His current programming projects include MapGazer.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and PanGazer<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
