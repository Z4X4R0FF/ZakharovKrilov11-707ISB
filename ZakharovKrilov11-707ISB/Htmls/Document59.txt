	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" >James Duncan Davidson</h1>

<p><b>James Duncan Davidson</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> photographer and a <a href="/wiki/Software_developer" class="mw-redirect" title="Software developer">software developer</a>. While a software engineer at <a href="/wiki/Sun_Microsystems" title="Sun Microsystems">Sun Microsystems</a> (1997–2001), Davidson created <a href="/wiki/Apache_Tomcat" title="Apache Tomcat">Tomcat</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Java_(programming_language)" title="Java (programming language)">Java</a>-based webserver application and the <a href="/wiki/Apache_Ant" title="Apache Ant">Ant</a> Java-based build tool. Davidson is the author or co-author of several books on both using and writing software, including <i>Learning Cocoa with Objective-C</i>, <i>Cocoa in a Nutshell</i>, <i>Running Mac OS X Panther</i>, and <i>Mac OS X Panther Hacks</i>, all published by <a href="/wiki/O%E2%80%99Reilly_Media" class="mw-redirect" title="O’Reilly Media">O’Reilly Media</a>. He also contributed to <i>Agile Web Development with Rails</i>, published by The Pragmatic Programmers.
</p><p>He was raised in <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a> and <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, and is now<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (March 2020)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> a resident of <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>. He is currently the Startup Advocate and CTO in Residence at <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://startups.microsoft.com">Microsoft for Startups</a>. His work is to provide advice for startup founders and CTOs and help them prioritize, focus, and think through their work.
</p><p>Starting in 2005, Duncan added photography to his other professional focuses.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
He has served as the primary event photographer at several high-profile technology conferences. Since 2009 he has been the main stage photographer for <a href="/wiki/TED_(conference)" title="TED (conference)">TED conferences</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> photographing every TED and <a href="/wiki/TEDGlobal" class="mw-redirect" title="TEDGlobal">TEDGlobal</a> event. In 2010, Duncan was the photographer for the Mission Blue Voyage in the <a href="/wiki/Galapagos_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Galapagos Islands">Galapagos Islands</a> and led the TEDxOilSpill Expedition in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>. Davidson is also known for his artistic travel photos, often with an eye for architectural details, a clear reference to his university studies in architecture.
</p><p>He is co-founder and co-owner (along with Greg Koenig) of Luma Labs, a small company based in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland, OR</a> focused on making high quality camera accessories.
</p>
