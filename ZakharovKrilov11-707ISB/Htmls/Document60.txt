<p><b>Terrence Andrew Davis</b> (December 15, 1969 – August 11, 2018) was an American <a href="/wiki/Programmer" title="Programmer">programmer</a> who created and designed the operating system <a href="/wiki/TempleOS" title="TempleOS">TempleOS</a>. Its development was an extremely complex, time-consuming and unusual undertaking for one person.<sup id="cite_ref-dalles_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dalles-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nosmallfeat_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nosmallfeat-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> During his final years, he amassed an online following and regularly posted video blogs to social media. He often referred to himself as "the smartest programmer that's ever lived".<sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a teenager, Davis learned <a href="/wiki/Assembly_language" title="Assembly language">assembly language</a> on a <a href="/wiki/Commodore_64" title="Commodore 64">Commodore 64</a>. He later earned a <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master&#39;s degree">master's degree</a> in <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a> from <a href="/wiki/Arizona_State_University" title="Arizona State University">Arizona State University</a> and worked for several years at <a href="/wiki/Ticketmaster" title="Ticketmaster">Ticketmaster</a> as a programmer for <a href="/wiki/VAX" title="VAX">VAX</a> machines. In 1996, he began experiencing regular manic episodes, one of which led him to hospitalization. Initially diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Bipolar_disorder" title="Bipolar disorder">bipolar disorder</a>, he was later declared to have <a href="/wiki/Schizophrenia" title="Schizophrenia">schizophrenia</a>. He collected disability payments and resided in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a> with his parents until the year before his death.
</p><p>Davis grew up as a <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a> and was an <a href="/wiki/Atheism" title="Atheism">atheist</a> for some of his adult life. After experiencing a self-described "revelation", he proclaimed that he was in direct communication with <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">God</a> and that God commanded him to build a <a href="/wiki/Third_Temple" title="Third Temple">successor</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Second_Temple" title="Second Temple">Second Temple</a>. He committed a decade of his life to creating an <a href="/wiki/Operating_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Operating System">OS</a> modeled after the <a href="/wiki/DOS" title="DOS">DOS</a>-based interfaces of his youth, first calling it the "J Operating System" and later "LoseThos". In 2013, Davis announced that he had completed the project, which was now called "TempleOS". It was released to public derision<sup id="cite_ref-derision_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-derision-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> contrasted by mostly favorable reviews from members of the tech community.<sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although Davis remained lucid when discussing computer-related subjects, his communication skills were largely affected by his schizophrenia. He was controversial for his regular use of racial epithets, which he explained was his way of combating actors of <a href="/wiki/Psychological_warfare" title="Psychological warfare">psychological warfare</a>. During his final months, he struggled with periods of homelessness and incarceration. In 2018, he was struck by a train and died at the age of 48. Investigators could not determine whether his death was suicide or accidental.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Terry_A._Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Terry_Davis_1990.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Terry_Davis_1990.jpg/220px-Terry_Davis_1990.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Terry_Davis_1990.jpg/330px-Terry_Davis_1990.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Terry_Davis_1990.jpg/440px-Terry_Davis_1990.jpg 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Terry_Davis_1990.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Davis with his parents, circa 1994</div></div></div>
<p>Terrence Andrew Davis was born in <a href="/wiki/West_Allis,_Wisconsin" title="West Allis, Wisconsin">West Allis, Wisconsin</a>, later moving to <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a>. He was the seventh of eight children, and his father was an <a href="/wiki/Industrial_engineering" title="Industrial engineering">industrial engineer</a>. As a child, Davis used an <a href="/wiki/Apple_II" title="Apple II">Apple II</a> at his elementary school, and as a teenager, learned <a href="/wiki/Assembly_language" title="Assembly language">assembly language</a> on a <a href="/wiki/Commodore_64" title="Commodore 64">Commodore 64</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He earned a master's degree in <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a> from <a href="/wiki/Arizona_State_University" title="Arizona State University">Arizona State University</a> in 1994 and worked for several years at <a href="/wiki/Ticketmaster" title="Ticketmaster">Ticketmaster</a><sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> as a programmer for <a href="/wiki/VAX" title="VAX">VAX</a> machines.<sup id="cite_ref-tech_Temp_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tech_Temp-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On the subject of his certifications, he wrote in 2011: "Everybody knows electrical is higher in the engineering pecking order than CS because it requires real math&#160;;-) I'm a rocket scientist, though, not a very good one".<sup id="cite_ref-LT2011_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LT2011-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Onset_of_illness_and_spiritual_awakening">Onset of illness and spiritual awakening</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Terry_A._Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Onset of illness and spiritual awakening">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Davis grew up <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a>, but was an atheist for some of his adult life before experiencing what he called a "revelation from God".<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In March 1996, Davis began experiencing regular manic episodes and developed delusions centering around <a href="/wiki/Extraterrestrial_life" title="Extraterrestrial life">space aliens</a> and government <a href="/wiki/Special_agent" title="Special agent">agents</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-slat_Temp_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-slat_Temp-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> According to Davis, he attributed a profound quality to the <a href="/wiki/Rage_Against_the_Machine" title="Rage Against the Machine">Rage Against the Machine</a> lyric "<a href="/wiki/Killing_in_the_Name" title="Killing in the Name">some of those that work forces are the same that burn crosses</a>" and recalled "I started seeing people following me around in suits and stuff. It just seemed something was strange."<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He started donating large sums of money to charity organizations, something he had never done before. Later, he surmised, "that act [probably] caused God to reveal Himself to me and saved me."<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Soon afterward, out of fear of the suited figures he believed to be following him, Davis left town and began driving hundreds of miles south with no destination. After becoming convinced that his car radio was communicating with him, he dismantled his vehicle (apparently in a search for tracking devices he believed were hidden on it) and threw his keys into the desert. He began to walk aimlessly along the side of the highway, where he was then picked up by an officer. Davis escaped from the patrol vehicle, broke his collarbone, and was then taken to a hospital. Distressed about a conversation over artifacts found on his X-ray scans, interpreted by him as "alien artifacts", he ran from the hospital and attempted to carjack a nearby truck before being arrested. In jail, he stripped himself, broke his glasses and jammed the frames into a nearby electrical outlet, trying to open his cell door by switching the breaker. This failed, as he had been wearing non-conductive frames. He was then admitted to a mental hospital for two weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 1996 to 2003, Davis was admitted to a psychiatric ward every six months due to reoccurring manic episodes. In 2014, he said that he "was genuinely pretty crazy in a way. Now I'm not. I'm crazy in a different way maybe."<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Davis acknowledged that the sequence of events leading to his spiritual awakening might give the impression of "mental illness, as opposed to some glorious revelation from God. ... It would sound polite if you said I scared myself thinking about <a href="/wiki/Quantum_computing" title="Quantum computing">quantum computers</a>. And then I guess you just throw in your ordinary mental illness."<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Reflecting on the initial episode, he said, "I'm not especially proud of the logic and thinking. It looks very young and childish and pathetic. ... In the Bible it says if you seek God, He will be found of you. I was really seeking, and I was looking everywhere to see what he might be saying to me."<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Davis was initially diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Bipolar_disorder" title="Bipolar disorder">bipolar disorder</a> and later declared to have <a href="/wiki/Schizophrenia" title="Schizophrenia">schizophrenia</a>. Afterward, he felt "guilty for being such a technology-advocate atheist" and sought to emulate <a href="/wiki/Jesus" title="Jesus">Jesus</a> by giving away all his possessions and living a nomadic lifestyle.<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In July 1996, he returned to Arizona and started formulating plans for a new business. He designed a three-axis <a href="/wiki/Milling_machine" class="mw-redirect" title="Milling machine">milling machine</a>, as he recalled having <a href="/wiki/3D_printing" title="3D printing">3D printing</a> in mind as an obvious pursuit. An incident involving a Dremel tool nearly set his apartment on fire, which caused him to abandon the idea.<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> From then onward, he lived with his parents in Las Vegas and collected <a href="/wiki/Social_Security_(United_States)" title="Social Security (United States)">Social Security</a> disability payments.<sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;nb 1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>
His schizophrenia affected his communication skills, and his online comments were usually incomprehensible. However, he was reported as "always lucid" if the topic was about computers.<sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <i>Vice</i> noted that, in 2012, he had a productive conversation with the contributors at <a href="/wiki/MetaFilter" title="MetaFilter">MetaFilter</a>, where his work was introduced as "an operating system written by a schizophrenic programmer".<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> </p><div style="clear:left;"></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="TempleOS">TempleOS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Terry_A._Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: TempleOS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/TempleOS" title="TempleOS">TempleOS</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:TempleOS_4.05_session.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/TempleOS_4.05_session.png/220px-TempleOS_4.05_session.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/TempleOS_4.05_session.png/330px-TempleOS_4.05_session.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/TempleOS_4.05_session.png/440px-TempleOS_4.05_session.png 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:TempleOS_4.05_session.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Screenshot of the TempleOS interface</div></div></div>
<p>TempleOS (known as "J Operating System" from 2004 to 2005, "LoseThos" from 2006 to early 2012, and "SparrowOS" in late 2012) is an operating system similar to the Commodore 64, <a href="/wiki/DESQview" title="DESQview">DESQview</a> and other early <a href="/wiki/DOS" title="DOS">DOS</a>-based interfaces.<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It was conceived by Davis in the early 2000s<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and developed alone over the course of a decade. This included the design of its original <a href="/wiki/Programming_language" title="Programming language">programming language</a>, <a href="/wiki/Source_code_editor" class="mw-redirect" title="Source code editor">editor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Compiler" title="Compiler">compiler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kernel_(operating_system)" title="Kernel (operating system)">kernel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was ultimately composed of over 100,000 lines of code.<sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005, Davis stated that his ambition for the J Operating System was "to recreate the dynamic environment that used to exist when the Commodore 64 was around and everyone was creating odd-ball software".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He envisioned the system as a Commodore 64 with a "thousand times" more powerful processing speed.<sup id="cite_ref-LT2011_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LT2011-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Three years later, he wrote that the primary purpose of LoseThos was "for making video games. It has no networking or Internet support. As far as I'm concerned, that would be reinventing the wheel".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Davis later proclaimed that he was in direct communication with God, and that God told him to build a <a href="/wiki/Third_Temple" title="Third Temple">successor</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Second_Temple" title="Second Temple">Second Temple</a> as an operating system.<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> As such, references to Biblical tropes are ubiquitous in the OS. One bundled program, "After Egypt", is a game in which the player travels to a <a href="/wiki/Burning_bush" title="Burning bush">burning bush</a> to use a "high-speed stopwatch". The stopwatch is meant to act as an <a href="/wiki/Oracle" title="Oracle">oracle</a> that generates pseudo-random text, something Davis believed to be coded messages from God. He likened the process to a <a href="/wiki/Ouija" title="Ouija">Ouija</a> board and <a href="/wiki/Glossolalia" class="mw-redirect" title="Glossolalia">speaking in tongues</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tech_Temp_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tech_Temp-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> An example of generated text follows:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>among consigned penally result perverseness checked stated held sensation reasonings skies adversity Dakota lip Suffer approached enact displacing feast Canst pearl doing alms comprehendeth nought<sup id="cite_ref-tech_Temp_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tech_Temp-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>According to Davis, many of the system's features, such as its 640x480 resolution and 16-color display, were also explicit instructions from God. The <a href="/wiki/Charter" title="Charter">charter</a> on his website stated that TempleOS was "God's official temple. Just like <a href="/wiki/Solomon%27s_Temple" title="Solomon&#39;s Temple">Solomon's Temple</a>, this is a community focal point where offerings are made and God's oracle is consulted".<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He used the oracle to ask God about war ("servicemen competing"), death ("awful"), dinosaurs ("Brontosaurs' feet hurt when stepped"), favorite video game (<i><a href="/wiki/Donkey_Kong_(video_game)" title="Donkey Kong (video game)">Donkey Kong</a></i>), favorite car (<a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a>), favorite national anthem (<a href="/wiki/Dievs,_sv%C4%93t%C4%AB_Latviju!" title="Dievs, svētī Latviju!">Latvia's</a>), favorite band (<a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">the Beatles</a>), and the <a href="/wiki/Ten_Commandments" title="Ten Commandments">11th commandment</a> ("Thou shall not litter").<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Davis stated that LoseThos was downloaded 10,000 times since 2009, and that there was "no evidence anyone has installed it. I am in a <a href="/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency" title="Central Intelligence Agency">CIA</a> prison."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Later in the year, he renamed LoseThos to "SparrowOS", and in early 2013, rebranded again as "TempleOS".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> A few weeks later, his website announced: "God's temple is finished. Now, God kills CIA until it spreads".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Recognition.2C_controversy_and_following"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Recognition,_controversy_and_following">Recognition, controversy and following</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Terry_A._Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Recognition, controversy and following">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:25em;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">What people are going to read is, "It's about a pathetic schizophrenic who made a crappy operating system." My perspective is, "God said I made His temple."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">—Terry A. Davis in regards to an article about his life, from an email to a <i>Vice</i> journalist<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Davis was controversial for his regular use of racist and homophobic slurs,<sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> sometimes rebuking his critics as "<a href="/wiki/CIA" class="mw-redirect" title="CIA">CIA</a> <a href="/wiki/Niggers" class="mw-redirect" title="Niggers">niggers</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Online, he would frequently communicate in randomly-generated blocks of text and off-topic declarations about God, which led to bans from websites including <a href="/wiki/Something_Awful" title="Something Awful">Something Awful</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reddit" title="Reddit">Reddit</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hacker_News" title="Hacker News">Hacker News</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> However, the critical reception to TempleOS was mostly favorable, as tech journalist David Cassel wrote, "programming websites tried to find the necessary patience and understanding to accommodate Davis".<sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/TechRepublic" title="TechRepublic">TechRepublic</a> and <a href="/wiki/OSNews" title="OSNews">OSNews</a> published positive articles on Davis' work, even though he was banned from the latter for hostile comments targeting its readers and staff.<sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>
Such outbursts, along with the system's "amateurish" presentation, ultimately caused TempleOS to become a frequent object of derision.<sup id="cite_ref-derision_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-derision-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Davis explained that his use of the word "nigger" was a reaction to being subject to <a href="/wiki/Psychological_warfare" title="Psychological warfare">psychological warfare</a> tactics by media agencies such as the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>. He addressed concerns about his language on his website: <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r996844942"/></p><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Jesus used a racist slur, calling Canaanites "dogs" in <a href="/wiki/Exorcism_of_the_Syrophoenician_woman%27s_daughter" title="Exorcism of the Syrophoenician woman&#39;s daughter">Matthew 15:21-28</a> ... I asked God about racism? He said, "sports". ...  White people indirectly call each other "nigger" all the time. After the BBC kept indirectly calling me a nigger I said, "Fine!  I ain't a pussy! I'll use the real word on my enemies". ... when I fight Satan, I use the sharpest knives I can find.  I ain't shedding no tears cause you don't like "nigger". ... Just remember that <a href="/wiki/Men_Are_from_Mars,_Women_Are_from_Venus" title="Men Are from Mars, Women Are from Venus">men are from Mars, women are from Venus</a> &#32;&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Sic" title="Sic">sic</a></i>&#93;, and our dear God is from planet Earth.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Once TempleOS was completed, most of his time was spent "coding, web surfing, or using the output from the <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Standards_and_Technology" title="National Institute of Standards and Technology">National Institute of Standards and Technology</a> randomness beacon to talk to God".<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He posted hours of video blogs and would refer to himself as "the smartest programmer that's ever lived" while showing his creations.<sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> A small fan following proceeded to form around Davis online.<sup id="cite_ref-dalles_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dalles-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, the OS was shown as a part of an <a href="/wiki/Outsider_art" title="Outsider art">outsider art</a> exhibition in <a href="/wiki/Bourogne" title="Bourogne">Bourogne</a>, France.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Davis said he was happy to receive the attention but was disappointed that few of his fans actually used the OS to speak to God.<sup id="cite_ref-VICE_5-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VICE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His YouTube channels were repeatedly banned due to his vulgarities.<sup id="cite_ref-dalles_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dalles-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2018, OSNews editor Thom Holwerda wrote: "Davis was clearly a gifted programmer – writing an entire operating system is no small feat – and it was sad to see him affected by his mental illness".<sup id="cite_ref-nosmallfeat_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nosmallfeat-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> One fan described him as a "programming legend", while another, a computer engineer, compared the development of TempleOS to a one-man-built skyscraper.<sup id="cite_ref-dalles_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dalles-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The engineer had previously spoken to Davis at length and believed that Davis could have been a "<a href="/wiki/Steve_Jobs" title="Steve Jobs">Steve Jobs</a>" or a "<a href="/wiki/Steve_Wozniak" title="Steve Wozniak">Steve Wozniak</a>" were it not for his illness.<sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He added that it "actually boggles my mind that one man wrote all that" and surmised that it may be difficult for a <a href="/wiki/Layperson" class="mw-redirect" title="Layperson">layperson</a> to understand how extraordinary it is to write an entire operating system alone.<sup id="cite_ref-dalles_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dalles-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Terry_A._Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Terry_A._Davis_in_front_of_Richland_WA_library.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Terry_A._Davis_in_front_of_Richland_WA_library.jpg/220px-Terry_A._Davis_in_front_of_Richland_WA_library.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Terry_A._Davis_in_front_of_Richland_WA_library.jpg/330px-Terry_A._Davis_in_front_of_Richland_WA_library.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Terry_A._Davis_in_front_of_Richland_WA_library.jpg/440px-Terry_A._Davis_in_front_of_Richland_WA_library.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4608" data-file-height="3456" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Terry_A._Davis_in_front_of_Richland_WA_library.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Davis during his phase of homelessness, June 2018</div></div></div>
<p>During his final months, Davis struggled with periods of homelessness and incarceration. He stopped taking medication because he believed that it limited his creativity. Some fans helped him by bringing him supplies, but he refused their housing offers. After living with his sister in Arizona, Davis traveled to California, and in April 2018, stopped in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland, Oregon</a>. Local police were informed that Davis may be a threat, since he had stated a willingness to kill if asked by God. In June, Portland police informed officials in the nearby town of <a href="/wiki/The_Dalles,_Oregon" title="The Dalles, Oregon">The Dalles</a> that they had received word that Davis may be headed there. No further complaints were received about Davis.<sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In his final video, recorded on a bench at the Dalles Wasco County Library and uploaded hours before his death, he explained that he had removed most of his videos because he did not wish to "litter" the Internet, and that he had learned how to "purify" himself. At the very end, he states: "It's good to be king. Wait, maybe. I think maybe I'm just like a little bizarre little person who walks back and forth. Whatever, you know, but..."<sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the evening of August 11, 2018, while walking alongside railroad tracks in The Dalles, Oregon, Davis was struck and killed by a <a href="/wiki/Union_Pacific" class="mw-redirect" title="Union Pacific">Union Pacific</a> train. Investigators could not determine whether his death was suicide or accidental, although the train engineer believed his death to be a suicide. The police report stated that Davis was walking with his back toward the train and that he turned around before the moment of impact.<sup id="cite_ref-dalles_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dalles-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> When <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dalles_Chronicle" title="The Dalles Chronicle">The Dalles Chronicle</a></i> ran a story about an unnamed homeless man who was struck by a train, the newspaper was inundated with phone calls inquiring whether it was Davis, which the paper later confirmed in a follow-up piece.<sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As reports of his death surfaced online, he was memorialized by fans in a number of tributes posted to social media.<sup id="cite_ref-newsstack_2-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsstack-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Through the TempleOS website, his family asked people to donate to "organizations working to ease the pain and suffering caused by mental illness".<sup id="cite_ref-dalles_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dalles-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In December 2018, Linux.org (an unofficial community for <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a> users) was modified by hackers to include a reference to his death.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In November 2019, Davis was the subject of a 30-minute documentary on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_4" title="BBC Radio 4">BBC Radio 4</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
