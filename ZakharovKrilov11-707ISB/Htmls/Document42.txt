	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" >Steve Capps</h1>
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		<div id="contentSub2"></div>
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#searchInput">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">American computer programmer</div>
<p><b>Steve Capps</b> is an American computer programmer, who was one of the designers of the original <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a> <a href="/wiki/Macintosh" title="Macintosh">Macintosh</a> computer.<sup id="cite_ref-lemmons198402_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lemmons198402-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Capps started working at the <a href="/wiki/Xerox" title="Xerox">Xerox Corporation</a> while still a <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> student at the <a href="/wiki/Rochester_Institute_of_Technology" title="Rochester Institute of Technology">Rochester Institute of Technology</a>. In 1981, Capps started working for Apple on the <a href="/wiki/Apple_Lisa" title="Apple Lisa">Lisa</a> project and he continued his work on the Macintosh, principally writing the <a href="/wiki/Macintosh_Finder" class="mw-redirect" title="Macintosh Finder">Finder</a> (along with <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Horn" title="Bruce Horn">Bruce Horn</a>) and Macintosh system utilities—such as <a href="/wiki/ResEdit" title="ResEdit">ResEdit</a>.
</p><p>During a break in 1986, he wrote three music programs including <a href="/wiki/Jam_Session_(software)" title="Jam Session (software)">Jam Session</a>, <a href="/wiki/SoundEdit" title="SoundEdit">SoundEdit</a>, and Super Studio Session; SoundEdit was eventually sold to <a href="/wiki/Macromedia" title="Macromedia">Macromedia</a>. From 1987 to 1996, he was the <a href="/wiki/Software_architect" title="Software architect">chief architect</a> and Apple Fellow for the <a href="/wiki/Apple_Newton" title="Apple Newton">Apple Newton</a>, where he led the specification and development of the user interface of Newton, shepherded the team of software developers, and wrote many portions of the built-in application software. From 1996 until 2001, he was a user interface architect at <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a>. His early work at Microsoft resulted in the <a href="/wiki/Internet_Explorer" title="Internet Explorer">Internet Explorer</a> Search, History, and Favorites panes. He was also a co-founder of the <a href="/wiki/MSN_Explorer" class="mw-redirect" title="MSN Explorer">MSN Explorer</a> project.
</p><p>As of 2010, he was working at Silicon Valley start-up <a href="/wiki/Kwedit" title="Kwedit">Kwedit</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> now called PayNearMe,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> focusing on online payment systems. At that time, he was married to lawyer Marie D'Amico and had one child, Emma T Capps, who was born in 1997.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
