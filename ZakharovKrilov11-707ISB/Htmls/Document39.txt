<p><b>Andries Evert Brouwer</b> (born 1951) is a Dutch mathematician and computer programmer, Professor Emeritus at <a href="/wiki/Eindhoven_University_of_Technology" title="Eindhoven University of Technology">Eindhoven University of Technology</a> (TU/e). He is known as the creator of the greatly expanded 1984 to 1985 versions of the <a href="/wiki/Roguelike" title="Roguelike">roguelike</a> computer game <i><a href="/wiki/Hack_(Unix_video_game)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hack (Unix video game)">Hack</a></i> that formed the basis for <i><a href="/wiki/NetHack" title="NetHack">NetHack</a></i>. He is also a <a href="/wiki/Linux_kernel" title="Linux kernel">Linux kernel</a> <a href="/wiki/Hacker_(programmer_subculture)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hacker (programmer subculture)">hacker</a>. He is sometimes referred to by the handle <i>aeb</i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andries_Brouwer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in Amsterdam, Brouwer attended the <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(school)" title="Gymnasium (school)">gymnasium</a>, and obtained his MSc in mathematics at the University of Amsterdam in 1971. In 1976 he received his Ph.D. in mathematics from <a href="/wiki/Vrije_Universiteit" class="mw-redirect" title="Vrije Universiteit">Vrije Universiteit</a> with a thesis entitled "Treelike Spaces and Related Topological Spaces", under the supervision of Maarten Maurice and Pieter Baayen, both of whom were in turn students of <a href="/wiki/Johannes_de_Groot" title="Johannes de Groot">Johannes de Groot</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gen_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gen-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2004 he received an honorary doctorate from <a href="/wiki/Aalborg_University" title="Aalborg University">Aalborg University</a>.
</p><p>After graduation Brouwer started his academic career at the Mathematisch Centrum, later <a href="/wiki/Centrum_Wiskunde_%26_Informatica" title="Centrum Wiskunde &amp; Informatica">Centrum Wiskunde &amp; Informatica</a>. From 1986 to 2012 he was Professor at <a href="/wiki/Eindhoven_University_of_Technology" title="Eindhoven University of Technology">Eindhoven University of Technology</a> (TU/e).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work">Work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andries_Brouwer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brouwer's varied research interests include several branches of <a href="/wiki/Discrete_mathematics" title="Discrete mathematics">discrete mathematics</a>, particularly <a href="/wiki/Graph_theory" title="Graph theory">graph theory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finite_geometry" title="Finite geometry">finite geometry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coding_theory" title="Coding theory">coding theory</a>.
</p><p>He has published dozens of papers in graph theory and other areas of <a href="/wiki/Combinatorics" title="Combinatorics">combinatorics</a>, many of them in collaboration with other researchers. His co-authors include at least 9 of the co-authors of <a href="/wiki/Paul_Erd%C5%91s" title="Paul Erdős">Paul Erdős</a>, giving him an <a href="/wiki/Erd%C5%91s_number" title="Erdős number">Erdős number</a> of 2.<sup id="cite_ref-erdos_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-erdos-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hack">Hack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andries_Brouwer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 1984, while at the <a href="/wiki/Centrum_Wiskunde_%26_Informatica" title="Centrum Wiskunde &amp; Informatica">Centrum Wiskunde &amp; Informatica</a> (CWI), he made the first public release of <a href="/wiki/Hack_(Unix_video_game)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hack (Unix video game)"><i>Hack</i></a> on <a href="/wiki/Usenet" title="Usenet">Usenet</a>. <i>Hack</i> was an implementation of <i><a href="/wiki/Rogue_(computer_game)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rogue (computer game)">Rogue</a></i> originally written in 1982 by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jay_Fenlason&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jay Fenlason (page does not exist)">Jay Fenlason</a> and a few others, but Brouwer heavily modified and expanded it. He distributed a total of four versions of <i>Hack</i> between December 1984 and July 1985. 
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Source_code" title="Source code">source code</a> was released as <a href="/wiki/Free_software" title="Free software">free software</a>, and it was widely copied, played, and <a href="/wiki/Porting" title="Porting">ported</a> to multiple computer platforms. When Mike Stephenson brought together a large development team via Usenet to produce an enhanced version in 1987 incorporating changes from many of the <i>Hack</i> derivatives, they respected Brouwer's wishes by renaming their game <i><a href="/wiki/NetHack" title="NetHack">NetHack</a></i>, as Brouwer might "...eventually release a new version of his own."<sup id="cite_ref-nethack_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nethack-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Linux_kernel">Linux kernel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andries_Brouwer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Linux kernel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Brouwer has also been involved with the development of <a href="/wiki/Unix-like" title="Unix-like">Unix-like</a> computer <a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">operating systems</a> based on the Linux kernel. He was previously the <a href="/wiki/Software_maintainer" title="Software maintainer">maintainer</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Man_page" title="Man page">man pager</a> program <code>man</code><sup id="cite_ref-man_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-man-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and the maintainer of the Linux man-pages project (from 1995 to 2004),<sup id="cite_ref-mpm_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mpm-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and he is a kernel maintainer in the areas of disk geometry and <a href="/wiki/Disk_partitioning" title="Disk partitioning">partition handling</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-lxr_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lxr-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Brouwer also serves as specialist in security aspects of Unix and Linux for EiPSI (Eindhoven Institute for the Protection of Systems and Information), TU/e's <a href="/wiki/Information_security" title="Information security">information security</a> research institute.<sup id="cite_ref-eipsi_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eipsi-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
