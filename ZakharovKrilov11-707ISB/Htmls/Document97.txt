	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" >Ralph Griswold</h1>
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		<div id="contentSub2"></div>
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#searchInput">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>
<b>Ralph E. Griswold</b> (May 19, 1934, <a href="/wiki/Modesto,_California" title="Modesto, California">Modesto</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">CA</a> – October 4, 2006, <a href="/wiki/Tucson" class="mw-redirect" title="Tucson">Tucson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">AZ</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a> known for his research into high-level <a href="/wiki/Programming_language" title="Programming language">programming languages</a> and <a href="/wiki/Symbolic_computation" class="mw-redirect" title="Symbolic computation">symbolic computation</a>. His language credits include the <a href="/wiki/String_(computer_science)" title="String (computer science)">string processing</a> language  <a href="/wiki/SNOBOL" title="SNOBOL">SNOBOL</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/w/index.php?title=SL5&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="SL5 (page does not exist)">SL5</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Icon_programming_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Icon programming language">Icon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He attended <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a>, receiving a bachelor's degree in physics, then an M.S. and Ph.D. in <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a>. Griswold went to <a href="/wiki/Bell_Labs" title="Bell Labs">Bell Labs</a> in 1962, where he studied ideas for non-numerical computation. SNOBOL was the outcome; it was a radically different language in its time and still is. He became the head of the Labs' Programming Research and Development department in 1967.
</p><p>In 1971, he was hired by the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Arizona" title="University of Arizona">University of Arizona</a> to be its first professor of computer science, subsequently organized the department, and was its head until 1981. While at Arizona, Griswold developed <a href="/wiki/Icon_programming_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Icon programming language">Icon</a>.  The earlier <a href="/wiki/Ratfor" title="Ratfor">Ratfor</a> implementation of Icon was discarded and the language rewritten from scratch in C and UNIX.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1990 Griswold was appointed Regents' Professor, and he retired in 1995. "As one of the founders of the Bell Labs software culture that spawned UNIX, C, and many other essential contributions to modern software, Ralph Griswold brought to his academic research not only brilliance, but also experience and a value system that demanded that research ideas be tested by fire and proven useful and usable by real users, not just good-looking diagrams in academic papers."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After his retirement, his interests turned to the mathematical aspects of <a href="/wiki/Weaving" title="Weaving">weaving</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Griswold died  on October 4, 2006, from cancer.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Griswold's son, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Griswold" title="Bill Griswold">Bill Griswold</a>, is also a computer scientist.
</p>
