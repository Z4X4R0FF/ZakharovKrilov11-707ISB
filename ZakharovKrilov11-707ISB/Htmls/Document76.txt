<p><b>Steve Fawkner</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> video game designer, programmer, and composer. He created the <i><a href="/wiki/Warlords_(game_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Warlords (game series)">Warlords</a></i> game series in 1989 and more recently the <i><a href="/wiki/Puzzle_Quest" title="Puzzle Quest">Puzzle Quest</a></i> series.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Fawkner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2003, after a long alliance with <a href="/wiki/Strategic_Studies_Group" title="Strategic Studies Group">SSG</a>, he split off to form his own game development company <a href="/wiki/Infinite_Interactive" class="mw-redirect" title="Infinite Interactive">Infinite Interactive</a>.
</p><p>In 2007, <a href="/wiki/Infinite_Interactive" class="mw-redirect" title="Infinite Interactive">Infinite Interactive</a> put <i><a href="/wiki/Puzzle_Quest:_Challenge_of_the_Warlords" title="Puzzle Quest: Challenge of the Warlords">Puzzle Quest: Challenge of the Warlords</a></i> on the market for the DS and PSP. It was a sleeper hit that received critical acclaim from gamers and game developers alike. Infinite Interactive has since ported <i>Puzzle Quest</i> to the Wii, Windows, Xbox 360, PS2, Mac, mobile, and iPhone.
</p><p>In 2008, a <i>Puzzle Quest</i> spin-off game was developed on a number of platforms for <a href="/wiki/D3_Publisher" title="D3 Publisher">D3 Publisher</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/Puzzle_Quest:_Galactrix" title="Puzzle Quest: Galactrix">Puzzle Quest: Galactrix</a></i> was released on 24 February 2009. While critics praised the variety of the gameplay, it wasn't as well received as <i>Puzzle Quest: Challenge of the Warlords</i>. A full sequel to <i>Puzzle Quest</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Puzzle_Quest_2" title="Puzzle Quest 2">Puzzle Quest 2</a></i>, was released on 22 June 2010 and was seen as a return to form for the <i>Puzzle Quest</i> series.
</p><p>Fawkner and Infinite Interactive licensed the game design to Boston-based <a href="/wiki/Demiurge_Studios" title="Demiurge Studios">Demiurge Studios</a> to create <i><a href="/wiki/Marvel_Puzzle_Quest:_Dark_Reign" class="mw-redirect" title="Marvel Puzzle Quest: Dark Reign">Marvel Puzzle Quest: Dark Reign</a></i>, while he worked on another project he called "Puzzle Quest and Warlords thrown together in a blender." <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gems_of_War&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gems of War (page does not exist)">Gems of War</a></i> was released on 20 November 2014 and is still being supported with updates and events.
</p><p>In 2016 as director of Infinity Plus Two, Steve designed and, the company self published another mixed mechanic game called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tiny_Quest&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tiny Quest (page does not exist)">Tiny Quest</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The game was released on iOS and Android in 2017, it was well received with plans to build on the game with a sequel.
</p>
