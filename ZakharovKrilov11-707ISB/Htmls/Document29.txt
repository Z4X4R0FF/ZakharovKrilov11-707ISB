<p><b>Gijsbert (Bert) Bos</b> (born 1963<sup id="cite_ref-dis_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dis-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) is a computer scientist known for the development of <a href="/wiki/Argo_(web_browser)" title="Argo (web browser)">Argo</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Web_browser" title="Web browser">web browser</a> he developed as test application for his <a href="/wiki/Style_sheet_language" title="Style sheet language">style sheet</a> proposal.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_work">Life and work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bert_Bos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">The Hague</a>, Bos studied <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Groningen" title="University of Groningen">University of Groningen</a>, and wrote his PhD <a href="/wiki/Thesis" title="Thesis">thesis</a> on <i>Rapid user interface development with the script language Gist</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-dis_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dis-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1996, he joined the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web_Consortium" title="World Wide Web Consortium">World Wide Web Consortium</a> (W3C) to work on <a href="/wiki/Cascading_Style_Sheets" class="mw-redirect" title="Cascading Style Sheets">Cascading Style Sheets</a> (CSS). He is a former chairman and the current W3C Staff Contact of the <a href="/wiki/CSS_Working_Group" title="CSS Working Group">CSS Working Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is based in <a href="/wiki/Sophia_Antipolis" title="Sophia Antipolis">Sophia Antipolis</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.
</p>
