<p><b>Roland Carl Backhouse</b> (born 18 August 1948) is a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mathematician" title="Mathematician">mathematician</a>. As of 2020<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Roland_Carl_Backhouse&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, he is <a href="/wiki/Emeritus" title="Emeritus">Emeritus</a> <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> of Computing Science at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Nottingham" title="University of Nottingham">University of Nottingham</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roland_Carl_Backhouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Backhouse was born and raised in the <a href="/wiki/Thorntree" title="Thorntree">Thorntree</a> district of Middlesbrough, an industrial town in the north-east of England. In 1959, he won a place at the then all-male Acklam Hall Grammar School before going on to <a href="/wiki/Churchill_College,_Cambridge" title="Churchill College, Cambridge">Churchill College, Cambridge</a>, in 1966. His <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">doctorate</a> (Ph.D.) was completed under the supervision of Jim Cunningham at <a href="/wiki/Imperial_College_London" title="Imperial College London">Imperial College London</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roland_Carl_Backhouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Backhouse's career has included <a href="/wiki/Royal_Aircraft_Establishment" title="Royal Aircraft Establishment">Royal Aircraft Establishment</a> (1969–1970), <a href="/wiki/Heriot-Watt_University" title="Heriot-Watt University">Heriot-Watt University</a> (1973–1982), <a href="/wiki/University_of_Essex" title="University of Essex">University of Essex</a> (1982–1986). He was formerly Professor of Computer Science at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Groningen" title="University of Groningen">University of Groningen</a> (1986–1990) and <a href="/wiki/Eindhoven_University_of_Technology" title="Eindhoven University of Technology">Eindhoven University of Technology</a> (1990–1999) in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, before his position at the University of Nottingham.
</p><p>He was a member of the <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_for_Information_Processing" title="International Federation for Information Processing">International Federation for Information Processing</a> (IFIP) <a href="/wiki/IFIP_Working_Group_2.1" title="IFIP Working Group 2.1">IFIP Working Group 2.1</a> on Algorithmic Languages and Calculi,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> which <a href="/wiki/Specification_(technical_standard)" title="Specification (technical standard)">specified</a>, maintains, and supports the <a href="/wiki/Programming_language" title="Programming language">programming languages</a> <a href="/wiki/ALGOL_60" title="ALGOL 60">ALGOL 60</a> and <a href="/wiki/ALGOL_68" title="ALGOL 68">ALGOL 68</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roland_Carl_Backhouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Backhouse is married to Hilary, née Mitchell. They have three sons, Kevin, Andrew, and <a href="/wiki/David_Backhouse" title="David Backhouse">David</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_interests">Academic interests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roland_Carl_Backhouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Academic interests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His research interests lie in the mathematics of program construction and <a href="/wiki/Algorithm" title="Algorithm">algorithmic</a> problem solving. Together with <a href="/wiki/Jan_L._A._van_de_Snepscheut" title="Jan L. A. van de Snepscheut">Jan L. A. van de Snepscheut</a> (1953—1994), he began the biennial series of conferences on the Mathematics of Program Construction, the first of which was held in 1989.
</p>
