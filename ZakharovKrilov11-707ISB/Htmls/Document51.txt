<p><b>Alan Cox</b> (born 22 July 1968) is a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Programmer" title="Programmer">computer programmer</a> who has been a key figure in the development of <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a>. He maintained the 2.2 branch of the <a href="/wiki/Linux_kernel" title="Linux kernel">Linux kernel</a> and continues to be heavily involved in the development of the Linux kernel, an association that dates back to 1991. He lives in <a href="/wiki/Swansea" title="Swansea">Swansea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, where he lived with his wife Telsa Gwynne, who died in 2015<sup id="cite_ref-jonas-2015_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jonas-2015-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gardiner-2015_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gardiner-2015-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and now lives with author Tara Neale,<sup id="cite_ref-neale-2019_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-neale-2019-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> who he married in 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-neale-2020_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-neale-2020-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He graduated with a <a href="/wiki/BSc" class="mw-redirect" title="BSc">BSc</a> in <a href="/wiki/Computer_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer Science">Computer Science</a> from <a href="/wiki/Swansea_University" title="Swansea University">Swansea University</a> in 1991 and received an <a href="/wiki/MBA" class="mw-redirect" title="MBA">MBA</a> from the same university in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-SwanseaHD_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SwanseaHD-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Involvement_in_the_Linux_kernel">Involvement in the Linux kernel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Cox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Involvement in the Linux kernel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:With-alan-cox.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/With-alan-cox.jpg/220px-With-alan-cox.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/With-alan-cox.jpg/330px-With-alan-cox.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/With-alan-cox.jpg/440px-With-alan-cox.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:With-alan-cox.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alan Cox at the LinuxWorldExpo</div></div></div>
<p>While employed on the campus of <a href="/wiki/Swansea_University" title="Swansea University">Swansea University</a>, Cox installed a very early version of Linux on one of the machines belonging to the university computer society. <sup id="cite_ref-sucs-2014_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sucs-2014-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> This was one of the first Linux installations on a busy <a href="/wiki/Computer_network" title="Computer network">network</a> and revealed many bugs in the networking code. Cox fixed many of these bugs and went on to rewrite much of the networking subsystem. He then became one of the main developers and maintainers of the whole kernel.
</p><p>He maintained the 2.2 branch, and his own versions of the 2.4 branch (signified by an "ac" in the version, for example 2.4.13-ac1). This branch was very stable and contained bugfixes that went directly into the vendor kernels.
</p><p>Cox was once commonly regarded as being the "second in command" after <a href="/wiki/Linus_Torvalds" title="Linus Torvalds">Linus Torvalds</a> himself, before reducing his involvement with Linux to study for an <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Business_Administration" title="Master of Business Administration">MBA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 28 July 2009, Cox quit his role as the <a href="/wiki/Tty_(Unix)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tty (Unix)">TTY</a> layer maintainer, after disagreement with Torvalds about the scope of work required to fix an error in that subsystem.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alan was employed by the Linux distributor <a href="/wiki/Red_Hat" title="Red Hat">Red Hat</a> during 1999–2009.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Starting from 2011 he was employed by <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel Corporation</a> but left both Intel and Linux kernel development in January 2013 to care full-time for his wife during a critical period of medical treatment, and returned to both later that year,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> until taking early retirement at the end of 2019.
</p><p>He has also been involved in the <a href="/wiki/GNOME" title="GNOME">GNOME</a> and <a href="/wiki/X.Org_Server" title="X.Org Server">X.Org</a> projects, and was the main developer of <a href="/wiki/AberMUD" title="AberMUD">AberMUD</a>, which he wrote whilst a student at the <a href="/wiki/Aberystwyth_University" title="Aberystwyth University">University of Wales, Aberystwyth</a>.
</p>

</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Model_trains">Model trains</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Cox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Model trains">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alan Cox runs Etched Pixels, a model train company producing <a href="/wiki/N_gauge" class="mw-redirect" title="N gauge">N gauge</a> kits.<sup id="cite_ref-urlEtched_Pixels:_N_scale_etched_models_and_details_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urlEtched_Pixels:_N_scale_etched_models_and_details-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activism">Activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Cox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cox is an ardent supporter of programming freedom, and an outspoken opponent of <a href="/wiki/Software_patent" title="Software patent">software patents</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Digital_Millennium_Copyright_Act" title="Digital Millennium Copyright Act">DMCA</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Consumer_Broadband_and_Digital_Television_Promotion_Act" title="Consumer Broadband and Digital Television Promotion Act">CBDTPA</a>. He resigned from a subgroup of <a href="/wiki/Usenix" class="mw-redirect" title="Usenix">Usenix</a> in protest, and said he would not visit the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> for fear of being imprisoned after the arrest of <a href="/wiki/United_States_v._Elcom_Ltd." title="United States v. Elcom Ltd.">Dmitry Sklyarov</a> for <a href="/wiki/Digital_Millennium_Copyright_Act" title="Digital Millennium Copyright Act">DMCA</a> violations.
</p><p>In January 2007, he applied for a series of patents on "RMS", or "rights management systems".<sup id="cite_ref-Patents_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Patents-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It is said<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (December 2013)">according to whom?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> that he has filed a patent for <a href="/wiki/Digital_rights_management" title="Digital rights management">digital rights management</a>. Red Hat Inc., Cox's former employer, has stated (in a document drafted by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webbink" title="Mark Webbink">Mark Webbink</a> and Cox himself)<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> that it will not use patents against <a href="/wiki/Free_software" title="Free software">free software</a> projects.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cox is also an adviser to the <a href="/wiki/Foundation_for_Information_Policy_Research" title="Foundation for Information Policy Research">Foundation for Information Policy Research</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Open_Rights_Group" title="Open Rights Group">Open Rights Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Cox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cox was the recipient of the <a href="/wiki/Free_Software_Foundation" title="Free Software Foundation">Free Software Foundation</a>'s 2003 <a href="/wiki/Award_for_the_Advancement_of_Free_Software" class="mw-redirect" title="Award for the Advancement of Free Software">Award for the Advancement of Free Software</a> at the <a href="/wiki/FOSDEM" title="FOSDEM">FOSDEM</a> conference in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 5 October 2005, Cox received a lifetime achievement award at the <a href="/wiki/LinuxWorld_Conference_and_Expo" title="LinuxWorld Conference and Expo">LinuxWorld</a> awards in London.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wales_Trinity_Saint_David" title="University of Wales Trinity Saint David">University of Wales Trinity Saint David</a> Awarded Cox an Honorary Fellowship on 18 July 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He received an honorary doctorate from the Swansea University, his alma mater, on 20 July 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-SwanseaHD_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SwanseaHD-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
