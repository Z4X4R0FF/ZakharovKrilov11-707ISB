<p><b>Lawrence (Larry) Moser Breed</b> (born July 17, 1940) is a <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Artist" title="Artist">artist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Inventor" title="Inventor">inventor</a>, best known for his involvement in the programming language <a href="/wiki/APL_(programming_language)" title="APL (programming language)">APL</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawrence_M._Breed&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As an undergraduate at <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a> in 1961, he created the first computer animation language and system and used it at Stanford football half-times to coordinate images produced by a 100&#160;ft-by-100&#160;ft array of rooters holding up colored cards.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a graduate student at Stanford, he corresponded with APL's inventor, <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_E._Iverson" title="Kenneth E. Iverson">Ken Iverson</a>, to correct the formal description of the <a href="/wiki/IBM_System/360" title="IBM System/360">IBM System/360</a> which used Iverson's notation.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> After receiving his M.S. from Stanford in 1965, under academic supervisor <a href="/wiki/Niklaus_Wirth" title="Niklaus Wirth">Niklaus Wirth</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> he joined Iverson's group at IBM's <a href="/wiki/Thomas_J._Watson_Research_Center" title="Thomas J. Watson Research Center">Thomas J. Watson Research Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yorktown_Heights,_New_York" title="Yorktown Heights, New York">Yorktown Heights, New York</a>, where he created the first implementation of APL, with <a href="/wiki/Philip_S._Abrams" title="Philip S. Abrams">Philip S. Abrams</a>, on a <a href="/wiki/Mainframe_computer" title="Mainframe computer">mainframe computer</a>, an <a href="/wiki/IBM_7090" title="IBM 7090">IBM 7090</a>, in 1965.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He later created APL implementations for an experimental IBM <i>Little Computer</i>, and the IBM 360 in 1966, and for the <a href="/wiki/IBM_1130" title="IBM 1130">IBM 1130</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Breed was the 1973 recipient (with <a href="/wiki/Richard_H._Lathwell" title="Richard H. Lathwell">Dick Lathwell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roger_Moore_(computer_scientist)" title="Roger Moore (computer scientist)">Roger Moore</a>) of the <a href="/wiki/Grace_Murray_Hopper_Award" title="Grace Murray Hopper Award">Grace Murray Hopper Award</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a> "for their work in the design and implementation of APL\360, setting new standards in simplicity, efficiency, reliability and response time for interactive systems."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With Dan Dyer and others he co-founded <a href="/wiki/Scientific_Time_Sharing_Corporation" title="Scientific Time Sharing Corporation">Scientific Time Sharing Corporation</a> in 1969, where he led the development of the APL PLUS time-sharing system. While there, in 1972, he and Francis Bates III wrote <a href="/wiki/History_of_email" title="History of email">one of the world's first</a> worldwide <a href="/wiki/Email" title="Email">email</a> systems, named <i>Mailbox</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Breed rejoined IBM in 1977.  He helped develop the <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">International Organization for Standardization</a> (ISO) APL standard, then joined IBM efforts to port <a href="/wiki/Berkeley_Software_Distribution" title="Berkeley Software Distribution">Berkeley Software Distribution</a> (BSD) <a href="/wiki/Unix" title="Unix">Unix</a> onto IBM platforms. He worked on <a href="/wiki/Compiler" title="Compiler">compilers</a> for the programming language <a href="/wiki/C_(programming_language)" title="C (programming language)">C</a>, <a href="/wiki/Floating-point_arithmetic" title="Floating-point arithmetic">floating-point arithmetic</a> standardization, and <a href="/wiki/Radix" title="Radix">radix</a> conversion, until retiring in 1992.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawrence_M._Breed&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Breed became a significant contributor to the <a href="/wiki/Burning_Man" title="Burning Man">Burning Man</a> festival, under the <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">playa name</a> of Ember. He coined the term "MOOP" (matter out of place), and conceived and built the first <i>trash fence</i> to capture windborne debris.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bmj2015_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bmj2015-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He created the spiraling, flaming sculpture "Chaotick", the playa’s longest-running art piece besides the Man himself,<sup id="cite_ref-bmj2015_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bmj2015-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and built artistic bicycle light effects.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>  He edited and proofread the Black Rock Gazette newspaper, a role in which he continued as a co-founder and director of its successor the <a href="/wiki/Black_Rock_Beacon" title="Black Rock Beacon">Black Rock Beacon</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and edited other Burning Man materials.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>  As co-founder of the Earth Guardians, Breed promoted the "Leave No Trace" ethos, particularly in post-event cleanup.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bmj2015_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bmj2015-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1973 and 1974 he took first place, with co-solver Donna Breed, in the Dictionary Rally.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gray-B-Gone_and_Evapotrons">Gray-B-Gone and Evapotrons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawrence_M._Breed&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Gray-B-Gone and Evapotrons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Associated with his Burning Man activities, Breed devised the <i>Gray-B-Gon</i> and the <i>Evapotron</i> evaporators for <a href="/wiki/Graywater" class="mw-redirect" title="Graywater">graywater</a> disposal, and through Bay Area workshops directed construction, by Burning Man campers, of over 100 units, as of 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-bmj2015_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bmj2015-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
