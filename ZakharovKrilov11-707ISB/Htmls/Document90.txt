	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" >Ryan C. Gordon</h1>

<p><b>Ryan C. Gordon</b> (also known as <b>icculus</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Computer_programmer" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer programmer">computer programmer</a> and former <a href="/wiki/Loki_Software" class="mw-redirect" title="Loki Software">Loki Software</a> employee who is now responsible for <i>icculus.org</i>, which hosts many Loki Software projects as well as several new projects created by himself and others. Gordon's site hosts projects with the <a href="/wiki/Category:Commercial_video_games_with_freely_available_source_code" title="Category:Commercial video games with freely available source code">code from such commercial games</a> as <i><a href="/wiki/Duke_Nukem_3D" title="Duke Nukem 3D">Duke Nukem 3D</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Shadow_Warrior" title="Shadow Warrior">Shadow Warrior</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Quake_III_Arena" title="Quake III Arena">Quake III Arena</a></i> and many other <a href="/wiki/Free_and_open_source" class="mw-redirect" title="Free and open source">free and open source</a> projects for multiple platforms.
</p><p>Gordon has also created ports of proprietary software products to the <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mac_OS_X" class="mw-redirect" title="Mac OS X">Mac OS X</a> platforms. These include being hired to port most of the <i><a href="/wiki/Unreal_(video_game_series)" title="Unreal (video game series)">Unreal Tournament</a></i> series, some of the <i><a href="/wiki/Serious_Sam" title="Serious Sam">Serious Sam series</a></i>, the official <i><a href="/wiki/Postal_(video_game_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Postal (video game series)">Postal series</a></i>, some <a href="/wiki/Tripwire_Interactive" title="Tripwire Interactive">Tripwire Interactive</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coffee_Stain_Studios" title="Coffee Stain Studios">Coffee Stain Studios</a> titles,  and <i><a href="/wiki/Prey_(2006_video_game)" title="Prey (2006 video game)">Prey (2006)</a></i>. He has also been involved in porting several non-gaming products such as <i><a href="/wiki/Second_Life" title="Second Life">Second Life</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Google_Earth" title="Google Earth">Google Earth</a></i>. Some, such as <i>Candy Cruncher</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Postal_2" title="Postal 2">Postal 2</a></i>, have been published by <a href="/wiki/Linux_Game_Publishing" title="Linux Game Publishing">Linux Game Publishing</a>. He has also been described alongside Edward Rudd as being "instrumental" in ensuring the multi-platform nature of the <a href="/wiki/Humble_Indie_Bundle" class="mw-redirect" title="Humble Indie Bundle">Humble Indie Bundle</a> initiatives.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_C._Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_C._Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gordon grew up just outside <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, going to college in <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">Charlotte, North Carolina</a> where he currently resides.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On January 3, 2010 he became engaged to his <a href="/wiki/Girlfriend" title="Girlfriend">girlfriend</a> Carrie and they were married later that year.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On January 31, 2012 his wife gave birth to their first child, Olive Finch Gordon. He is also a founding member of the BareBones Theatre Group in Charlotte.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Loki_Software">Loki Software</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_C._Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Loki Software">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the summer of 1999 <a href="/wiki/Loki_Software" class="mw-redirect" title="Loki Software">Loki Software</a> ran a contest called "Loki Hack" at the <i>Atlanta Linux Showcase</i>, with the goal of improving the Linux port of <i><a href="/wiki/Civilization:_Call_To_Power" class="mw-redirect" title="Civilization: Call To Power">Civilization: Call To Power</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Gordon decided to give it a try, driving the four-hour trip from his home to <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Loki was impressed with his work and decided to offer him a job.<sup id="cite_ref-hardware.no_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hardware.no-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Gordon quickly rose to prominence at Loki, working as a developer on the game ports of <i><a href="/wiki/Kohan:_Immortal_Sovereigns" title="Kohan: Immortal Sovereigns">Kohan: Immortal Sovereigns</a></i>, <i>Quake III Arena</i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Eric%27s_Ultimate_Solitaire" title="Eric&#39;s Ultimate Solitaire">Eric's Ultimate Solitaire</a></i>, as well as being the lead developer for <i><a href="/wiki/Descent_3" title="Descent 3">Descent 3</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Heavy_Metal:_F.A.K.K.%C2%B2" class="mw-redirect" title="Heavy Metal: F.A.K.K.²">Heavy Metal: F.A.K.K.²</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2000 he also wrote several articles for the online Linux news and information website <a href="/wiki/Linux.com" title="Linux.com">Linux.com</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Around this time he founded <i>icculus.org</i>, made to provide hosting for various <a href="/wiki/Free_and_open_source" class="mw-redirect" title="Free and open source">free and open source</a> projects, and would later be used to support and continue some of Loki's free software projects and tools. While at Loki Software, Gordon picked his <a href="/wiki/Nickname" title="Nickname">nickname</a> icculus as a reference to the <a href="/wiki/Fiction" title="Fiction">fictional</a> <a href="/wiki/Gamehendge#Characters_from_Gamehendge" title="Gamehendge">character from Gamehendge</a> in the song <a href="/wiki/Junta_(album)" title="Junta (album)">"Icculus"</a> by <a href="/wiki/Phish" title="Phish">Phish</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SteamLUG_Cast_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SteamLUG_Cast-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Independent_contracts">Independent contracts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_C._Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Independent contracts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The upcoming closure of Loki forced him to seek outside employment. A friend offered him a job at his <a href="/wiki/Cybercafe" class="mw-redirect" title="Cybercafe">cybercafe</a>, and he was forced to move in with his parents. Desperate to escape working a <a href="/wiki/Cash_register" title="Cash register">cash register</a> for a living, he found the <a href="/wiki/E-mail" class="mw-redirect" title="E-mail">e-mail</a> address of an artist working for the developer <a href="/wiki/Croteam" title="Croteam">Croteam</a>. They had recently released <a href="/wiki/Serious_Sam:_The_First_Encounter" title="Serious Sam: The First Encounter">the first</a> of their highly successful <i><a href="/wiki/Serious_Sam" title="Serious Sam">Serious Sam</a></i> series, and Gordon asked if they would be interested in him building a Linux port.<sup id="cite_ref-hardware.no_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hardware.no-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> They agreed, and the port's first <a href="/wiki/Software_release_life_cycle#Beta" title="Software release life cycle">beta</a> was released on December 5, 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> From there he gained other contracts such as being hired to port <i><a href="/wiki/Devastation_(video_game)" title="Devastation (video game)">Devastation</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Medal_of_Honor:_Allied_Assault" title="Medal of Honor: Allied Assault">Medal of Honor: Allied Assault</a></i> for Linux and <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Army" title="America&#39;s Army">America's Army</a></i> for Linux and Mac OS X.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He was also contracted by <a href="/wiki/Epic_Games" title="Epic Games">Epic Games</a> to port their <i><a href="/wiki/Unreal_Tournament_2003" title="Unreal Tournament 2003">Unreal Tournament 2003</a></i> title to <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a> and Mac OS X, with the port being included with the packaged <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Windows</a> version.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> This business relationship continued with the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Unreal_Tournament_2004" title="Unreal Tournament 2004">Unreal Tournament 2004</a></i>, and followed from the original <i>Unreal Tournament</i> which was ported to Linux internally by Epic but was published by Loki.
</p><p>Upon learning about the release of <i>Postal 2</i> in 2003, Gordon decided to contact the developer behind it wondering if they would be interested in him making a port of the game to Linux and Mac OS X. Loki had previously ported the original <i><a href="/wiki/Postal_(video_game)" title="Postal (video game)">Postal</a></i> to Linux, and he was interested in keeping the franchise compatible.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Running_with_Scissors_(company)" title="Running with Scissors (company)">Running with Scissors</a> agreed, and the finished port was shipped on February 14, 2005, with Linux Game Publishing initially handling the publishing of the Linux version.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Soon after he was contracted to make a Linux port for the <a href="/wiki/Wolfire_Games" title="Wolfire Games">Wolfire Games</a> title <i><a href="/wiki/Lugaru:_The_Rabbit%27s_Foot" class="mw-redirect" title="Lugaru: The Rabbit&#39;s Foot">Lugaru: The Rabbit's Foot</a></i>, a business relationship that would later prove fruitful for Gordon.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Around this time he was also contracted to build a Linux client for the online virtual world <i>Second Life</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> also utilized his services, with him being contracted to make a native Linux port of their <i>Google Earth</i> application, with Linux functionality finally becoming available starting with the release of the  version 4 beta on June 12, 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007 he was once again contracted by Epic Games to port their new <i><a href="/wiki/Unreal_Tournament_3" title="Unreal Tournament 3">Unreal Tournament 3</a></i> title to both Linux and Mac OS X. The Linux game server was released on December 18, 2007,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> but work was eventually abandoned on the Linux and Mac OS X clients for reasons which remain undisclosed.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In October 2008 he made the surprise announcement that he had been working on a Linux client for the <a href="/wiki/First-person_shooter" title="First-person shooter">first-person shooter</a> game <i><a href="/wiki/Prey_(2006_video_game)" title="Prey (2006 video game)">Prey</a></i>, after previously porting the game's Linux server in 2006. Although there had been some speculation about a potential Linux release, it had been denied by the developer of the title in the past.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The finished port was released on December 7, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="FatELF.2C_Humble_Bundle.2C_and_Steam"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="FatELF,_Humble_Bundle,_and_Steam">FatELF, Humble Bundle, and Steam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_C._Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: FatELF, Humble Bundle, and Steam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 23, 2009 he announced his plans to make a <a href="/wiki/Universal_binary" title="Universal binary">universal binary</a> system similar to the one used on Mac OS X for Linux systems called <i><a href="/wiki/Executable_and_Linkable_Format#FatELF:_universal_binaries_for_Linux" title="Executable and Linkable Format">FatELF</a></i>. The project generated considerable controversy, with several <a href="/wiki/Linux_Kernel" class="mw-redirect" title="Linux Kernel">Linux Kernel</a> developers decrying the effort.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Gordon announced that the project was on hold in early November 2009,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> later stating that he would be willing to work on it again if he receives help from an interested party.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Around this time he also started to port the game <i><a href="/wiki/Aquaria_(video_game)" title="Aquaria (video game)">Aquaria</a></i> to Linux for <a href="/wiki/Bit_Blot" class="mw-redirect" title="Bit Blot">Bit Blot</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>  On May 11, 2010 he and Wolfire Games released the source code of his code branch of <i><a href="/wiki/Lugaru" title="Lugaru">Lugaru</a></i> under the <a href="/wiki/GNU_General_Public_License" title="GNU General Public License">GNU General Public License</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He was also involved with the source code release of Aquaria, both of which were sold as part of the first <a href="/wiki/Humble_Bundle#Humble_Indie_Bundle" title="Humble Bundle">Humble Indie Bundle</a>, which was also hosted by Wolfire. He was then contracted to port <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Orchestra:_Ostfront_41-45" title="Red Orchestra: Ostfront 41-45">Red Orchestra: Ostfront 41-45</a></i> to Mac OS X, stating that he could potentially port it to Linux if <a href="/wiki/Steam_(service)#Linux" title="Steam (service)">Steam</a> ever released a Linux client.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Gordon also generated some controversy that summer for his talk at the 2010 <a href="/wiki/SouthEast_LinuxFest" title="SouthEast LinuxFest">SouthEast LinuxFest</a> titled "Anatomy of a Failure", where he criticized some aspects of the Kernel development process.<sup id="cite_ref-selfanatomy_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-selfanatomy-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He then ported <i><a href="/wiki/Braid_(video_game)" title="Braid (video game)">Braid</a></i> to both Linux and Mac OS X to allow it to be a part of <a href="/wiki/Humble_Indie_Bundle_2" class="mw-redirect" title="Humble Indie Bundle 2">Humble Indie Bundle 2</a>, as well as porting the games <i><a href="/wiki/Cogs_(video_game)" title="Cogs (video game)">Cogs</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Hammerfight" title="Hammerfight">Hammerfight</a></i> for later bundles.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> As part of this he ported the "<a href="/wiki/Haaf%27s_Game_Engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Haaf&#39;s Game Engine">Haaf's Game Engine</a>" to Linux and Mac OS X, and released his port under a <a href="/wiki/Free_software_license" class="mw-redirect" title="Free software license">free software license</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He also ported <i><a href="/wiki/Frozen_Synapse" title="Frozen Synapse">Frozen Synapse</a></i> to Linux to be included as the primary part of the Frozen Synapse Bundle, as well as <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Meat_Boy" title="Super Meat Boy">Super Meat Boy</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Shank_(video_game)" title="Shank (video game)">Shank</a></i> for Humble Indie Bundle 4. During the rest of 2012 Gordon would work on several more ports for the Humble Bundles, including <i><a href="/wiki/Avadon:_The_Black_Fortress" title="Avadon: The Black Fortress">Avadon: The Black Fortress</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Space_Pirates_and_Zombies" title="Space Pirates and Zombies">Space Pirates and Zombies</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Psychonauts" title="Psychonauts">Psychonauts</a></i>. He would also be involved in updating old releases as well as porting several new titles to Linux to be included as part of <a href="/wiki/Valve_Corporation" title="Valve Corporation">Valve</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Steam_(service)" title="Steam (service)">Steam</a> Linux launch, including creating Linux ports of the games <i>Red Orchestra: Ostfront 41-45</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Killing_Floor_(2009_video_game)" class="mw-redirect" title="Killing Floor (2009 video game)">Killing Floor</a></i> for Tripwire Interactive. As part of the <a href="/wiki/Humble_Indie_Bundle_7" class="mw-redirect" title="Humble Indie Bundle 7">Humble Indie Bundle 7</a> promotion Gordon ported the title <i><a href="/wiki/Dungeon_Defenders" title="Dungeon Defenders">Dungeon Defenders</a></i> to Linux, making it the first Linux native title to utilize <a href="/wiki/Unreal_Engine_3" class="mw-redirect" title="Unreal Engine 3">Unreal Engine 3</a>, the engine that debuted with the release of <i>Unreal Tournament 3</i> back in 2007 which Gordon had unsuccessfully tried to port to the platform.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013 he ported <i><a href="/wiki/Dear_Esther" title="Dear Esther">Dear Esther</a></i> to Linux to be included as part of <a href="/wiki/Humble_Indie_Bundle_8" class="mw-redirect" title="Humble Indie Bundle 8">Humble Indie Bundle 8</a>. Gordon was one of the speakers at the first ever <i>Steam Dev Days</i>, where he outlined how to port a game to Linux and to <a href="/wiki/SteamOS" title="SteamOS">SteamOS</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> In 2014 he ported the games <i><a href="/wiki/Goat_Simulator" title="Goat Simulator">Goat Simulator</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Sanctum_2" title="Sanctum 2">Sanctum 2</a></i> to Linux and Mac OS X for Coffee Stain Studios. On June 19, 2014 he released a new Linux port of <i><a href="/wiki/Limbo_(video_game)" title="Limbo (video game)">LIMBO</a></i>, a game that had previously been released for Linux using a <a href="/wiki/CrossOver_(software)" title="CrossOver (software)">CrossOver</a> wrapper as part of <a href="/wiki/Humble_Indie_Bundle_V" class="mw-redirect" title="Humble Indie Bundle V">Humble Indie Bundle V</a>, something that proved controversial at the time due to it not being a fully native release.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> In order to accomplish this Gordon also had to port <a href="/wiki/Audiokinetic_Wwise" title="Audiokinetic Wwise">Wwise</a>, the audio middleware that previously prevented a native port from being possible.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="icculus.org">icculus.org</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_C._Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: icculus.org">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>icculus.org is Gordon's personal website and a project incubator similar to <a href="/wiki/Sourceforge.net" class="mw-redirect" title="Sourceforge.net">Sourceforge.net</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ourproject.org" title="Ourproject.org">Ourproject.org</a> or <a href="/wiki/GNU_Savannah" title="GNU Savannah">GNU Savannah</a>. Gordon is often involved in the projects hosted on his site but not universally. The site hosts port or enhancement projects for games such as <i><a href="/wiki/Aliens_versus_Predator_(video_game)" class="mw-redirect" title="Aliens versus Predator (video game)">Aliens versus Predator</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Jump_%27n_Bump" title="Jump &#39;n Bump">Jump 'n Bump</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/DONKEY.BAS#DONKEY.GB" title="DONKEY.BAS">DONKEY.BAS</a></i>, <i>Duke Nukem 3D</i>, <i>Shadow Warrior</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Freespace_2" class="mw-redirect" title="Freespace 2">Freespace 2</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Descent_2" class="mw-redirect" title="Descent 2">Descent 2</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Shades" title="Black Shades">Black Shades</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ken%27s_Labyrinth" title="Ken&#39;s Labyrinth">Ken's Labyrinth</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rise_of_the_Triad" title="Rise of the Triad">Rise of the Triad</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Wolfenstein_3D" title="Wolfenstein 3D">Wolfenstein 3D</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Heretic_(video_game)" title="Heretic (video game)">Heretic</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hexen:_Beyond_Heretic" title="Hexen: Beyond Heretic">Hexen</a></i>, cooperative <i><a href="/wiki/Rune_(video_game)" title="Rune (video game)">Rune</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Quake_II" title="Quake II">Quake II</a></i>, <i>the <a href="/wiki/Build_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Build engine">Build engine</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Unreal_(1998_video_game)" title="Unreal (1998 video game)">Unreal</a></i> (as an <i>Unreal Tournament</i> mod) and the <i><a href="/wiki/Chzo_Mythos" class="mw-redirect" title="Chzo Mythos">Chzo Mythos</a></i>.  The site also hosts free software projects such as <a href="/wiki/Neverball" class="mw-redirect" title="Neverball">Neverball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ioquake3" class="mw-redirect" title="Ioquake3">ioquake3</a> and related projects, an attempted <a href="/wiki/Visual_Basic" title="Visual Basic">Visual Basic</a> re-implementation, <a href="/wiki/OpenAL" title="OpenAL">OpenAL</a> for Mac OS X, and a <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Messenger" title="Yahoo! Messenger">Yahoo! Messenger</a> client. It is also the host of the freeware <a href="/wiki/CodeRED:_Alien_Arena" class="mw-redirect" title="CodeRED: Alien Arena">CodeRED: Alien Arena</a> project and has hosted code from the developers Blazing Games,<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Piga Software,<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Wolfire Games, and Bit Blot.
</p>
