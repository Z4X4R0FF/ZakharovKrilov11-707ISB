	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" >Marc Blank</h1>
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		<div id="contentSub2"></div>
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#searchInput">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Marc Blank</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_game_developer" title="Video game developer">game developer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Software_engineer" title="Software engineer">software engineer</a>.  He is best known as part of the team that created one of the first commercially successful <a href="/wiki/Text_adventure" class="mw-redirect" title="Text adventure">text adventure</a> <a href="/wiki/Computer_games" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer games">computer games</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Zork" title="Zork">Zork</a></i>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:MBlank2018.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/MBlank2018.jpg/220px-MBlank2018.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/MBlank2018.jpg/330px-MBlank2018.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/MBlank2018.jpg/440px-MBlank2018.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3672" data-file-height="4896" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:MBlank2018.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Marc Blank photographed in 2018</div></div></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Blank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blank first encountered <a href="/wiki/Don_Woods_(programmer)" title="Don Woods (programmer)">Don Woods</a> and <a href="/wiki/Will_Crowther" class="mw-redirect" title="Will Crowther">Will Crowther</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Colossal_Cave_Adventure" title="Colossal Cave Adventure">Adventure</a></i> game while he was studying at <a href="/wiki/MIT" class="mw-redirect" title="MIT">MIT</a> in the mid-1970s, where the game was played on <a href="/wiki/Mainframe_computer" title="Mainframe computer">mainframe</a> computers.
</p><p>Blank was frustrated by the computer's tiny vocabulary; when it parsed user inputs very few words were recognized. After thinking about the problem during his undergraduate years, he started work on his own adventure game using <a href="/wiki/MDL_(programming_language)" title="MDL (programming language)">MDL</a>, a computer language invented at MIT. Blank and a handful of friends wrote the original version of <i>Zork</i> on a <a href="/wiki/PDP-10" title="PDP-10">PDP-10</a> while he was attending medical school at <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein_College_of_Medicine" title="Albert Einstein College of Medicine">Albert Einstein College of Medicine</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> (he received his MD degree in 1979).
</p><p>The free-play university version of <i>Zork</i> first became available on the MIT-DM PDP-10 in June 1977. It was then distributed by the <a href="/wiki/Digital_Equipment_Corporation" title="Digital Equipment Corporation">Digital Equipment Corporation</a> <a href="/wiki/DECUS" title="DECUS">DECUS</a> program and spread to many colleges in the United States and Canada.
</p><p>Blank graduated from medical school in 1979 but the call of <i>Zork</i> was irresistible. He and several friends spent the next year developing a specialized <a href="/wiki/Computer_language" title="Computer language">computer language</a> that they could use to program text adventures like <i>Zork</i> on the new <a href="/wiki/Microcomputers" class="mw-redirect" title="Microcomputers">microcomputers</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Apple_II" title="Apple II">Apple II</a>'s limited <a href="/wiki/RAM" class="mw-redirect" title="RAM">RAM</a> required them to cut half of the original version of <i>Zork</i>. The new <i>Zork</i> for the Apple and the <a href="/wiki/Radio_Shack" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio Shack">Radio Shack</a> <a href="/wiki/TRS-80" title="TRS-80">TRS-80</a> had a 600-word vocabulary. They founded the new company, <a href="/wiki/Infocom" title="Infocom">Infocom</a>, to publish the game and more like it. Aside from <i>Zork I</i>, <i>II</i>, and <i>III</i>, he designed <i><a href="/wiki/Deadline_(video_game)" title="Deadline (video game)">Deadline</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Enchanter_(video_game)" title="Enchanter (video game)">Enchanter</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Fooblitzky" title="Fooblitzky">Fooblitzky</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Border_Zone_(video_game)" title="Border Zone (video game)">Border Zone</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Journey_(1989_video_game)" title="Journey (1989 video game)">Journey: The Quest Begins</a></i>
</p><p>In 1993 he teamed up with former Infocom writer <a href="/wiki/Michael_Berlyn" title="Michael Berlyn">Michael Berlyn</a> to found Blank, Berlyn and Co. The company's name was later changed to <a href="/wiki/Bend_Studio" title="Bend Studio">Eidetic</a> after former Apple Employee [Russ Wetmore] joined as the third partner, to help program Notion, for the Apple Newton. Eidetic initially published <a href="/wiki/Productivity_software" title="Productivity software">productivity software</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Apple_Newton" title="Apple Newton">Apple Newton</a>. Eidetic's <i>Notion: The Newton List Manager</i> became a hit and was ultimately bundled in all Newtons.
</p><p>Blank returned to text adventures in 1997 when <a href="/wiki/Activision" title="Activision">Activision</a> producer <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Dombrower" title="Eddie Dombrower">Eddie Dombrower</a> asked Blank and Berlyn to create a small promotional game, <i><a href="/wiki/Zork:_The_Undiscovered_Underground" title="Zork: The Undiscovered Underground">Zork: The Undiscovered Underground</a></i>, to promote the release of Activision's graphical game <i><a href="/wiki/Zork:_Grand_Inquisitor" title="Zork: Grand Inquisitor">Zork: Grand Inquisitor</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As Newton sales faded, Eidetic changed gears to focus on <a href="/wiki/IBM_PC_clone" class="mw-redirect" title="IBM PC clone">PC</a> and <a href="/wiki/PlayStation" title="PlayStation">PlayStation</a> games, producing <i><a href="/wiki/Bubsy_3D" title="Bubsy 3D">Bubsy 3D</a></i> in 1996 and <i><a href="/wiki/Syphon_Filter" title="Syphon Filter">Syphon Filter</a></i> in 1999. In 2000 <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a> acquired Eidetic for an undisclosed sum.
</p><p>Blank left Sony in 2004, where he focused on his email client for the <a href="/wiki/Palm_Treo" title="Palm Treo">Treo</a> smartphone, ChatterEmail. On February 22, 2007, Blank announced he would no longer be "actively" working on ChatterEmail. Blank subsequently joined Palm, Inc. (who acquired ChatterEmail) and led the design and implementation of the Palm Pre's Email application. He worked in the <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a> group at <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> from 2010 to 2012, and as a Principal Engineer at <a href="/wiki/Lab126" class="mw-redirect" title="Lab126">Lab126</a> (<a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a>) from 2012 to 2018.
</p><p>In 2013 Blank and <a href="/wiki/Dave_Lebling" title="Dave Lebling">Dave Lebling</a> were awarded the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Interactive_Arts_%26_Sciences" title="Academy of Interactive Arts &amp; Sciences">Academy of Interactive Arts &amp; Sciences</a> Pioneer Award for their work on <i>Zork</i>.
