	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" >Joshua Bloch</h1>

<p><b>Joshua J. Bloch</b> (born August 28, 1961) is an American <a href="/wiki/Software_engineer" title="Software engineer">software engineer</a> and a technology author, formerly employed at <a href="/wiki/Sun_Microsystems" title="Sun Microsystems">Sun Microsystems</a> and <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>. He led the design and implementation of numerous <a href="/wiki/Java_platform" class="mw-redirect" title="Java platform">Java platform</a> features, including the <a href="/wiki/Java_Collections" class="mw-redirect" title="Java Collections">Java Collections</a> Framework, the <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886049734">.mw-parser-output .monospaced{font-family:monospace,monospace}</style><span class="monospaced">java.math</span> package, and the <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r886049734"/><span class="monospaced">assert</span> mechanism.<sup id="cite_ref-about_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-about-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He is the author of the programming guide <i>Effective Java</i> (2001), which won the 2001 <a href="/wiki/Jolt_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Jolt Awards">Jolt Award</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and is a co-author of two other Java books, <i>Java Puzzlers</i> (2005) and <i>Java Concurrency In Practice</i> (2006).
</p><p>Bloch holds a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">B.S.</a> in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> from <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Ph.D." class="mw-redirect" title="Ph.D.">Ph.D.</a> in computer science from <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Mellon_University" title="Carnegie Mellon University">Carnegie Mellon University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-about_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-about-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His 1990 thesis was titled <i>A Practical Approach to Replication of Abstract Data Objects</i><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">ACM</a> Distinguished Doctoral Dissertation Award.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bloch has worked as a Senior Systems Designer at <a href="/wiki/Transarc" title="Transarc">Transarc</a>, and later as a Distinguished Engineer at <a href="/wiki/Sun_Microsystems" title="Sun Microsystems">Sun Microsystems</a>. In June 2004, he left Sun and became Chief Java Architect at <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On August 3, 2012, Bloch announced that he would be leaving Google.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2004, <i>Java Developer's Journal</i> included Bloch in its list of the "Top 40 Software People in the World".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bloch has proposed the extension of the Java programming language with two features: Concise Instance Creation Expressions (CICE) (coproposed with Bob Lee and <a href="/wiki/Doug_Lea" title="Doug Lea">Doug Lea</a>) and Automatic Resource Management (ARM) blocks. The combination of CICE and ARM formed one of the three early proposals for adding support for <a href="/wiki/Closure_(computer_science)" class="mw-redirect" title="Closure (computer science)">closures</a> to Java.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> ARM blocks were added to the language in JDK7.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bloch is currently an affiliated faculty member of the Institute for Software Research at Carnegie Mellon University, where he holds the title "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Adjunct_Professor_of_the_Practice&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Adjunct Professor of the Practice (page does not exist)">Adjunct Professor of the Practice</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
