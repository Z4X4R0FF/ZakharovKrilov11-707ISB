<p><b>Adam Dunkels</b> (born 1978) is a Swedish <a href="/wiki/Entrepreneur" class="mw-redirect" title="Entrepreneur">entrepreneur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Programmer" title="Programmer">programmer</a> and founder of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thingsquare&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Thingsquare (page does not exist)">Thingsquare</a>.
</p><p>His father was Andrejs Dunkels, professor in Mathematics. His mother was Kerstin Vännman also professor. His work is mainly focused on networking technology and distributed communication for small <a href="/wiki/Embedded_system" title="Embedded system">embedded</a> devices and wireless <a href="/wiki/Sensor_network" class="mw-redirect" title="Sensor network">sensor networks</a> on the Internet. Dunkels is best known to the embedded community as the author of the <a href="/wiki/UIP_(micro_IP)" title="UIP (micro IP)">uIP</a> (micro-IP) and <a href="/wiki/LwIP" title="LwIP">lwIP</a> TCP/IP protocol stacks. He is also the creator of <a href="/wiki/Protothread" title="Protothread">protothreads</a> and author of the <a href="/wiki/Contiki" title="Contiki">Contiki</a> operating system. The <i>MIT Technology Review</i> placed him on the <a href="/wiki/TR35" class="mw-redirect" title="TR35">TR35</a> list of world's top 35 innovators under 35, in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His book <i>Interconnecting Smart Objects with IP - the Next Internet</i>, co-authored with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jean-Philippe_Vasseur&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jean-Philippe Vasseur (page does not exist)">Jean-Philippe Vasseur</a> and with a foreword by <a href="/wiki/Vint_Cerf" title="Vint Cerf">Vint Cerf</a>, was published in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is a founder of the <a href="/wiki/IPSO_Alliance" title="IPSO Alliance">IPSO Alliance</a>, who promotes IP networking for smart objects such as embedded systems and wireless sensors, and author of the alliance's white paper.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dunkels received the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Roger_Needham#EuroSys_Roger_Needham_PhD_Award" title="Roger Needham">EuroSys Roger Needham PhD Award</a> for his PhD thesis "Programming Memory-Constrained Networked Embedded Systems".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
