<p><b>Lars Bak</b> (born 1962 or 1963)<sup id="cite_ref-Schenker_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Schenker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a Danish <a href="/wiki/Computer_programmer" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer programmer">computer programmer</a>. He is known as a <a href="/wiki/JavaScript" title="JavaScript">JavaScript</a> expert and for his work on <a href="/wiki/Virtual_machines" class="mw-redirect" title="Virtual machines">virtual machines</a>. He previously worked for <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, having contributed to the <a href="/wiki/Google_Chrome" title="Google Chrome">Chrome browser</a> by developing the <a href="/wiki/V8_JavaScript_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="V8 JavaScript engine">V8 JavaScript engine</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_life">Professional life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_Bak_(computer_programmer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Professional life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bak studied at <a href="/wiki/Aarhus_University" title="Aarhus University">Aarhus University</a> in Denmark, receiving an MS degree in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> in 1988 after which he became active in designing and implementing object-oriented <a href="/wiki/Process_virtual_machine" class="mw-redirect" title="Process virtual machine">virtual machines</a>. He has been awarded the 2018 Senior <a href="/wiki/Dahl%E2%80%93Nygaard_Prize" title="Dahl–Nygaard Prize">Dahl–Nygaard Prize</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Virtual_machines">Virtual machines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_Bak_(computer_programmer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Virtual machines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After participating in the design and implementation of the <a href="/wiki/BETA_(programming_language)" title="BETA (programming language)">BETA</a> Mjølner System, in 1991 he joined the Self group at <a href="/wiki/Sun_Microsystems_Laboratories" title="Sun Microsystems Laboratories">Sun Microsystems Laboratories</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cupertino" class="mw-redirect" title="Cupertino">Cupertino</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. During his time there, he developed a programming environment for <a href="/wiki/Self_(programming_language)" title="Self (programming language)">Self</a> and added several enhancements to the virtual machine.
</p><p>In 1994, he joined <a href="/wiki/Strongtalk" title="Strongtalk">LongView Technologies</a> LLC, where he designed and implemented high performance virtual machines for both <a href="/wiki/Smalltalk" title="Smalltalk">Smalltalk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Java_(programming_language)" title="Java (programming language)">Java</a>. After Sun Microsystems acquired LongView in 1997, Bak became engineering manager and technical lead in the <a href="/wiki/HotSpot_(virtual_machine)" title="HotSpot (virtual machine)">HotSpot</a> team at Sun's Java Software Division where he developed a high-performance <a href="/wiki/Java_virtual_machine" title="Java virtual machine">Java virtual machine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2002, after returning to Aarhus, Denmark, Bak founded OOVM, a company which developed software for mobile phones. In 2004, he sold it to a Swiss company, <a href="/wiki/Myriad_Group" title="Myriad Group">Esmertec</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2004, Bak joined Google to work on the <a href="/wiki/Google_Chrome" title="Google Chrome">Chrome browser</a>. He did not return to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, preferring to work in Denmark where his daughters were also receiving their education.<sup id="cite_ref-Schenker_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Schenker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> With a team of 12 engineers, Bak has been coordinating the development of the V8 JavaScript interpreter for Chrome, named after <a href="/wiki/V8_engine" title="V8 engine">the V8 automobile engine design</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bak co-developed the <a href="/wiki/Dart_(programming_language)" title="Dart (programming language)">Dart</a> programming language presented at the 2011 Goto conference in <a href="/wiki/Aarhus" title="Aarhus">Aarhus</a>, Denmark.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2018, Bak is working on a startup, building a new programming language for <a href="/wiki/Internet_of_Things" class="mw-redirect" title="Internet of Things">Internet of Things</a> systems.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Patents">Patents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_Bak_(computer_programmer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Patents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bak holds 18 <a href="/wiki/U.S._Patent" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Patent">U.S. software patents</a> in the field of <a href="/wiki/Virtual_machine" title="Virtual machine">virtual machines</a> programming.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, after <a href="/wiki/Oracle_Corporation" title="Oracle Corporation">Oracle</a> bought <a href="/wiki/Sun_Microsystems" title="Sun Microsystems">Sun</a> and with Lars Bak working for Google, Oracle sued Google for infringing on several software patents and amongst them was the "Interpreting Functions Utilizing a Hybrid of Virtual and Native Machine Instructions" patent<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> filed by Lars Bak et al.
</p>
