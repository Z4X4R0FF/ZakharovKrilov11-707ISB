	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" >Marc Ewing</h1>
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		<div id="contentSub2"></div>
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#searchInput">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Marc Ewing</b> (born May 9, 1969) is the creator and originator of the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hat" title="Red Hat">Red Hat</a> brand of software, most notably the Red Hat range of <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a> operating system distributions.<sup id="cite_ref-cenet_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cenet-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was involved in the <a href="/wiki/86open" class="mw-redirect" title="86open">86open</a> project in the mid-1990s.
</p><p>Ewing graduated from <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Mellon_University" title="Carnegie Mellon University">Carnegie Mellon University</a> in 1992. While at CMU, he was known to wear a red hat as he walked between classes. Because of his computer expertise, people would ask for help from the "man in the red hat". Ewing and co-founder <a href="/wiki/Bob_Young_(businessman)" title="Bob Young (businessman)">Bob Young</a> named their initial software after the hat, and the name <a href="/wiki/Red_Hat" title="Red Hat">Red Hat</a> stuck.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the height of the <a href="/wiki/Dot_com_bubble" class="mw-redirect" title="Dot com bubble">dot com bubble</a> in 1999, Ewing was briefly worth over 900 million dollars.<sup id="cite_ref-cenet_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cenet-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
