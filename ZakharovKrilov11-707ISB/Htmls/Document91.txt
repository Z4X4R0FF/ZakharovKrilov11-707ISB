<p><b>James Arthur Gosling, </b>often referred to as "Dr. Java", <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Canada" title="Order of Canada">OC</a> (born May 19, 1955) is a Canadian <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a>, best known as the founder and lead designer behind the <a href="/wiki/Java_(programming_language)" title="Java (programming language)">Java programming language</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Gosling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>James Gosling received a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">Bachelor of Science</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Calgary" title="University of Calgary">University of Calgary</a> <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and his M.A. and Ph.D. from <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Mellon_University" title="Carnegie Mellon University">Carnegie Mellon University</a>, all in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mathgene_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mathgene-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-goslingphd_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-goslingphd-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He wrote a version of <a href="/wiki/Emacs" title="Emacs">Emacs</a> called <a href="/wiki/Gosling_Emacs" title="Gosling Emacs">Gosling Emacs</a> (Gosmacs) while working toward his doctorate. He built a multi-processor version of Unix for a 16-way computer system<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> while at Carnegie Mellon University, before joining <a href="/wiki/Sun_Microsystems" title="Sun Microsystems">Sun Microsystems</a>. He also developed several <a href="/wiki/Compiler" title="Compiler">compilers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mail_transfer_agent" class="mw-redirect" title="Mail transfer agent">mail systems</a> there.
</p>
<h2><span id="Career_.26_contributions"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_&amp;_contributions">Career &amp; contributions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Gosling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career &amp; contributions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gosling was with Sun Microsystems between 1984 and 2010 (26 years). At Sun he invented an early Unix windowing system called <a href="/wiki/NeWS" title="NeWS">NeWS</a>, which became a lesser-used alternative to the still used <a href="/wiki/X_Window" class="mw-redirect" title="X Window">X Window</a>, because Sun did not give it an open source license.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>He is known as the father of the <a href="/wiki/Java_programming_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Java programming language">Java programming language</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LeavesSun_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LeavesSun-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-joinGoogle_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-joinGoogle-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He got the idea for the <a href="/wiki/Java_virtual_machine" title="Java virtual machine">Java VM</a> while writing a program to port software from a <a href="/wiki/PERQ" title="PERQ">PERQ</a> by translating Perq Q-Code to VAX assembler and emulating the hardware. He is generally credited with having invented the <a href="/wiki/Java_(programming_language)" title="Java (programming language)">Java</a> programming language in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
He created the original design of Java and implemented the language's original compiler and <a href="/wiki/Virtual_machine" title="Virtual machine">virtual machine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Gosling traces the origins of the approach to his early graduate student days, when he created a <a href="/wiki/P-code_machine" title="P-code machine">p-code virtual machine</a> for the lab's <a href="/wiki/DEC_VAX" class="mw-redirect" title="DEC VAX">DEC VAX</a> computer, so that his professor could run programs written in <a href="/wiki/UCSD_Pascal" title="UCSD Pascal">UCSD Pascal</a>.  In the work leading to Java at Sun, he saw that architecture-neutral execution for widely distributed programs could be achieved by implementing a similar philosophy:  always program for the same virtual machine.<sup id="cite_ref-McMillan2011_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McMillan2011-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Another contribution of Gosling's was co-writing the "<a href="/wiki/Shar" title="Shar">bundle</a>" program, known as "shar", a utility thoroughly detailed in <a href="/wiki/Brian_Kernighan" title="Brian Kernighan">Brian Kernighan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rob_Pike" title="Rob Pike">Rob Pike</a>'s book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Unix_Programming_Environment" title="The Unix Programming Environment">The Unix Programming Environment</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He left Sun Microsystems on April 2, 2010, after it was acquired by the <a href="/wiki/Oracle_Corporation" title="Oracle Corporation">Oracle Corporation</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-LeavesSun_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LeavesSun-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> citing reductions in pay, status, and decision-making ability, along with change of role and ethical challenges.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He has since taken a very critical stance towards Oracle in interviews, noting that "during the integration meetings between Sun and Oracle, where we were being grilled about the patent situation between Sun and Google, we could see the Oracle lawyer's eyes sparkle."<sup id="cite_ref-joinGoogle_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-joinGoogle-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He clarified his position during the <i><a href="/wiki/Oracle_v._Google" class="mw-redirect" title="Oracle v. Google">Oracle v. Google</a></i> trial over Android: "While I have differences with Oracle, in this case they are in the right. Google totally slimed Sun. We were all really disturbed, even <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_I._Schwartz" title="Jonathan I. Schwartz">Jonathan</a> [Schwartz]: he just decided to put on a happy face and tried to turn lemons into lemonade, which annoyed a lot of folks at Sun."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> However, he approved of the court's ruling that APIs should not be copyrightable.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2011, Gosling joined <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Six months later, he followed his colleague <a href="/wiki/Bill_Vass" title="Bill Vass">Bill Vass</a> and joined a startup called <a href="/wiki/Liquid_Robotics" title="Liquid Robotics">Liquid Robotics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nighthacks1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nighthacks1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In late 2016, Liquid Robotics was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Boeing" title="Boeing">Boeing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Following the acquisition, Gosling left Liquid Robotics to work at <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Web_Services" title="Amazon Web Services">Amazon Web Services</a> as Distinguished Engineer in May 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-aws_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aws-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is an advisor at the <a href="/wiki/Scala_(programming_language)" title="Scala (programming language)">Scala</a> company <a href="/wiki/Lightbend" title="Lightbend">Lightbend</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Independent Director at <a href="/wiki/Jelastic" title="Jelastic">Jelastic</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> and Strategic Advisor for <a href="/wiki/Eucalyptus_(computing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Eucalyptus (computing)">Eucalyptus</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> and is a board member of DIRTT Environmental Solutions.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is known for his love of proving "the unknown"<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (May 2020)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and has noted but later clarified to be untrue that his favorite <a href="/wiki/Irrational_number" title="Irrational number">irrational number</a> is <a href="/wiki/Square_root_of_2" title="Square root of 2"><span class="nowrap">&#8730;<span style="border-top:1px solid; padding:0 0.1em;">2</span></span></a>.  He has a framed picture of the first 1,000 digits of <a href="/wiki/Square_root_of_2" title="Square root of 2"><span class="nowrap">&#8730;<span style="border-top:1px solid; padding:0 0.1em;">2</span></span></a> in his office.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Gosling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For his achievement, the <a href="/wiki/National_Academy_of_Engineering" title="National Academy of Engineering">National Academy of Engineering</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> elected him as a Foreign Associate member.<sup id="cite_ref-NAE_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NAE-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<ul><li>2002: awarded <i><a href="/wiki/The_Economist" title="The Economist">The Economist</a></i> Innovation Award.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>2002: awarded <i><a href="/wiki/USENIX#USENIX_Lifetime_Achievement_Award" title="USENIX">The Flame Award</a></i> USENIX Lifetime Achievement Award.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>2007: made an Officer of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Canada" title="Order of Canada">Order of Canada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The Order is Canada's second highest civilian honor. Officers are the second highest grade within the Order.</li>
<li>2013: became a <a href="/wiki/Fellow" title="Fellow">fellow</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup></li>
