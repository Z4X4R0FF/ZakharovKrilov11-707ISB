<p><b>Richard Reeves Brodie</b> (born November 10, 1959) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Computer_programmer" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer programmer">computer programmer</a> and author. He was the original creator of <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Word" title="Microsoft Word">Microsoft Word</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Tsang_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tsang-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> After leaving Microsoft, Brodie became a <a href="/wiki/Motivational_speaker" title="Motivational speaker">motivational speaker</a> and authored two bestselling books.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Brodie_(programmer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Brodie_(programmer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Brodie was born in <a href="/wiki/Newton,_Massachusetts" title="Newton, Massachusetts">Newton, Massachusetts</a>, the older son of Mary Ann Brodie and Richard Brodie, a child psychologist. He graduated from <a href="/wiki/Newton_South_High_School" title="Newton South High School">Newton South High School</a> and entered <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a> in the fall of 1977 concentrating in applied mathematics with an emphasis on computer science. Brodie left Harvard after his sophomore year and moved to <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto,_CA" class="mw-redirect" title="Palo Alto, CA">Palo Alto, CA</a> to work for <a href="/wiki/Xerox" title="Xerox">Xerox Corporation's</a> Advanced Systems Division (ASD), where he met <a href="/wiki/Charles_Simonyi" title="Charles Simonyi">Charles Simonyi</a> and helped develop the Bravo X word processor for the Alto computer.<sup id="cite_ref-Manes_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Manes-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Simonyi became a mentor to Brodie while at Xerox and took Brodie with him when he moved to <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> in 1981.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Microsoft">Microsoft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Brodie_(programmer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Microsoft">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Simonyi hired Brodie in 1981 as Microsoft's 77th employee, and a founding member of the Microsoft Application Division.
</p><p>Brodie distinguished himself at Microsoft by creating the first version of <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Word" title="Microsoft Word">Microsoft Word</a> in less than seven months.<sup id="cite_ref-Tsang_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tsang-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In addition to primary authorship of Microsoft Word, Brodie wrote Microsoft's first C compiler, the original version of <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Notepad" title="Microsoft Notepad">Notepad</a>, and Word for the IBM PC Jr.
</p><p>Brodie's success as a programmer brought him to the attention of <a href="/wiki/Bill_Gates" title="Bill Gates">Bill Gates</a>, who made Brodie his technical assistant in 1983.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Brodie's primary accomplishment as Gates's assistant was the management of the Cashmere project, which would be released as Word for Windows.
Brodie left Microsoft after the company went public in 1986, but returned in 1991 as Chief Software Designer and Lead Developer of the Omega project, which would be released as <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Access" title="Microsoft Access">Microsoft Access</a> in 1992. He left Microsoft again in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_leaving_Microsoft">After leaving Microsoft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Brodie_(programmer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: After leaving Microsoft">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Between his stints at Microsoft, Brodie embarked upon a personal self-improvement quest. He took numerous courses and participated in retreats, looking for an answer to the question of "why money and success didn’t make me happy."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He wrote about his experience in his first book, <i>Getting Past OK: The Self-Help Book for People Who Don’t Need Help</i>, first published in 1993. The book became a regional bestseller and was republished by <a href="/wiki/Warner_Books" class="mw-redirect" title="Warner Books">Warner Books</a>. Brodie followed <i>Getting Past OK</i> with <i>Virus of the Mind</i> in 1995. <i>Virus of the Mind</i> explored the new field of <a href="/wiki/Memetics" title="Memetics">memetics</a>, but from a practical point of view. <a href="/wiki/Hay_House" title="Hay House">Hay House</a> bought the rights to both books and currently publishes them. Both books are in print in many languages worldwide.
</p><p>Brodie's books and the subsequent book tours landed him interviews on both <i><a href="/wiki/The_Oprah_Winfrey_Show" title="The Oprah Winfrey Show">The Oprah Winfrey Show</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Phil_Donahue_Show" title="The Phil Donahue Show">Phil Donahue</a></i>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Poker_player">Poker player</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Brodie_(programmer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Poker player">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Apart from his careers as a programmer and author, Brodie has found creative ways to integrate his love of sports and games into his professional life. In 2003 he joined the professional <a href="/wiki/Poker" title="Poker">poker</a> circuit. He has finished in the money in five <a href="/wiki/World_Series_of_Poker" title="World Series of Poker">World Series of Poker</a> events and six World Poker Tours. Until <a href="/wiki/Black_Friday_(2011)" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Friday (2011)">Black Friday</a>, he played as a <a href="/wiki/Full_Tilt_Poker" title="Full Tilt Poker">Full Tilt Poker</a> pro under the screen name Quiet Lion.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He appeared on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC's</a> game show <i><a href="/wiki/Identity_(game_show)" title="Identity (game show)">Identity</a></i> and played himself in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Grand_(film)" title="The Grand (film)">The Grand</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Brodie currently resides in <a href="/wiki/Kirkland,_Washington" title="Kirkland, Washington">Kirkland, Washington</a>.
</p>
