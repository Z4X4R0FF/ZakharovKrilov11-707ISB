<p><b>Jim Gettys</b> (born 15 October 1953) is an American <a href="/wiki/Computer_programmer" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer programmer">computer programmer</a>. He was involved in multiple computer related projects.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activity">Activity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jim_Gettys&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Activity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gettys worked at <a href="/wiki/Digital_Equipment_Corporation" title="Digital Equipment Corporation">DEC</a>'s Cambridge Research Laboratory.
</p><p>Until January 2009,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> he was the Vice President of Software at the <a href="/wiki/One_Laptop_per_Child" title="One Laptop per Child">One Laptop per Child</a> project,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> working on the software for the <a href="/wiki/OLPC_XO-1" class="mw-redirect" title="OLPC XO-1">OLPC XO-1</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 2009 through 2014, he worked at <a href="/wiki/Alcatel-Lucent" title="Alcatel-Lucent">Alcatel-Lucent</a> <a href="/wiki/Bell_Labs" title="Bell Labs">Bell Labs</a>.
</p><p>Gettys was the co-founder of the group investigating <a href="/wiki/Bufferbloat" title="Bufferbloat">Bufferbloat</a> and the effect it has on the performance of the Internet.,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and was a core member of the group from 2010-2017, concluding with his publication of "The Blind Man and the Elephant",<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> calling for the wide adoption of <a href="/wiki/Fair_Queuing" class="mw-redirect" title="Fair Queuing">Fair Queuing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Active_queue_management" title="Active queue management">AQM</a> techniques across the Internet, particularly RFC8290
.<sup id="cite_ref-:8290_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8290-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 2017 he has been investigating the potential for <a href="/wiki/Reproducible_builds" title="Reproducible builds">reproducible builds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blockchain" title="Blockchain">blockchain</a> technology to make possible a more securely updatable and maintainable IoT infrastructure.
</p><p>He is one of the original developers of the <a href="/wiki/X_Window_System" title="X Window System">X Window System</a> at <a href="/wiki/MIT" class="mw-redirect" title="MIT">MIT</a> and worked on it again with <a href="/wiki/X.Org_Server" title="X.Org Server">X.Org</a>, where he served on the board of directors.
</p><p>He served on the <a href="/wiki/GNOME" title="GNOME">GNOME</a> foundation board of directors.
</p><p>He worked at the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web_Consortium" title="World Wide Web Consortium">World Wide Web Consortium</a> (W3C)<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and was the editor of the <a href="/wiki/HTTP" class="mw-redirect" title="HTTP">HTTP</a>/1.1 specification in the <a href="/wiki/Internet_Engineering_Task_Force" title="Internet Engineering Task Force">Internet Engineering Task Force</a> through draft standard.
</p><p>Gettys helped establish the handhelds.org community, from which the development of <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a> on <a href="/wiki/Handheld" class="mw-redirect" title="Handheld">handheld</a> devices can be traced.
</p><p>One of his main goals at OLPC was to review and overhaul much of standard Linux software, in order to make it run faster and consume less memory and power. In this context, he has pointed out a common fallacy among programmers today: that storing computed values in memory is preferable to recomputing those values later. This, he claims, is often false on current hardware, given fast <a href="/wiki/CPU" class="mw-redirect" title="CPU">CPUs</a> and the long time it takes to recover from a potential <a href="/wiki/Cache_miss" class="mw-redirect" title="Cache miss">cache miss</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>He holds a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">BSc</a> degree from <a href="/wiki/MIT" class="mw-redirect" title="MIT">MIT</a> in <i>Earth and Planetary Sciences</i> (course 12 — EAPS).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jim_Gettys&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He won the 1997 <i>Internet Plumber of the Year</i> award on behalf of the group who worked on HTTP/1.1.
</p><p>Gettys is one of the keepers of the Flame (<a href="/wiki/USENIX" title="USENIX">USENIX</a>'s 1999 Lifetime Achievement Award) on behalf of The X Window System Community at Large.
</p>
