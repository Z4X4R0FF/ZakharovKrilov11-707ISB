<p><b>Andrew Christopher Gower</b> (born c. 1978) is a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> computer programmer and businessman. He is the co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Cambridge" title="Cambridge">Cambridge</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Jagex" title="Jagex">Jagex</a>, the company he founded with his brothers Paul and Ian Gower and with Constant Tedder. He created the <a href="/wiki/MMORPG" class="mw-redirect" title="MMORPG">MMORPG</a> <i><a href="/wiki/RuneScape" title="RuneScape">RuneScape</a></i> with the assistance of his two brothers, Ian and Paul Gower.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In December 2010 he left the Jagex board of directors.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He no longer has holdings in the company.<sup id="cite_ref-SDCP_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SDCP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> As of November 2011, he is listed under the credits for <i>RuneScape</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Since leaving Jagex, Gower has founded a new gaming development and consulting company, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fen_Research&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fen Research (page does not exist)">Fen Research</a>, of which he holds 90% of the shares.<sup id="cite_ref-SDCP_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SDCP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Currently, he is developing a futuristic sci-fi strategy game named <i>Solstrike</i>.  Gower has designed a <a href="/wiki/Type_system#Static_type_checking" title="Type system">statically-typed</a> <a href="/wiki/Programming_language" title="Programming language">programming language</a> to aid in the project's development.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Gower&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gower was born in <a href="/wiki/Nottingham,_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Nottingham, England">Nottingham, England</a>. He said in the <i>Runescape Documentary</i> (2017) that he first got into programming when he was seven years old.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/The_Becket_School" title="The Becket School">The Becket School</a> and went on to study at <a href="/wiki/Fitzwilliam_College,_Cambridge" title="Fitzwilliam College, Cambridge">Fitzwilliam College, Cambridge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cambridge_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cambridge-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Sunday_Times_Rich_List_2007" title="Sunday Times Rich List 2007">2007 Sunday Times Rich List</a> listed Andrew and Paul Gower as the 654th richest entrepreneurs in the UK, worth Â£113 million.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RetireAt21_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RetireAt21-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, the <i>Sunday Times</i> <a href="/wiki/Sunday_Times_Rich_List_2009" title="Sunday Times Rich List 2009">listed</a> them as the 566th richest men, worth an estimated Â£99 million.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Sunday_Times_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Sunday_Times-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i> also listed Andrew and Paul Gower as the 11th richest young <a href="/wiki/Entrepreneur" class="mw-redirect" title="Entrepreneur">entrepreneurs</a> in the <a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">UK</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
