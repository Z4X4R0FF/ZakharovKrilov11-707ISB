<p><b>Grady Booch</b> (born February 27, 1955) is an American <a href="/wiki/Software_engineer" title="Software engineer">software engineer</a>, best known for developing the <a href="/wiki/Unified_Modeling_Language" title="Unified Modeling Language">Unified Modeling Language</a> (UML) with <a href="/wiki/Ivar_Jacobson" title="Ivar Jacobson">Ivar Jacobson</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Rumbaugh" title="James Rumbaugh">James Rumbaugh</a>. He is recognized internationally for his innovative work in software architecture, software engineering, and <a href="/wiki/Collaborative_development_environment" title="Collaborative development environment">collaborative development environments</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grady_Booch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Booch earned his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor&#39;s degree">bachelor's degree</a> in 1977 from the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force_Academy" title="United States Air Force Academy">United States Air Force Academy</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master&#39;s degree">master's degree</a> in electrical engineering in 1979 from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Santa_Barbara" title="University of California, Santa Barbara">University of California, Santa Barbara</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_and_research">Career and research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grady_Booch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career and research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Booch worked at Vandenberg Air Force Base after he graduated. He started as a project engineer and later managed ground-support missions for the space shuttle and other projects. After he gained his master's degree he became an instructor at the Airforce Academy.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Booch served as Chief Scientist of <a href="/wiki/Rational_Software" title="Rational Software">Rational Software Corporation</a> from its founding in 1981 through its acquisition by IBM in 2003, where he continued to work until March 2008.  After this he became Chief Scientist, Software Engineering in IBM Research and series editor for <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Cummings" title="Benjamin Cummings">Benjamin Cummings</a>.
</p><p>Booch has devoted his life's work to improving the art and the science of software development. In the 1980s, he wrote one of the more popular books on programming in <a href="/wiki/Ada_(programming_language)" title="Ada (programming language)">Ada</a>. He is best known for developing the <a href="/wiki/Unified_Modeling_Language" title="Unified Modeling Language">Unified Modeling Language</a> with <a href="/wiki/Ivar_Jacobson" title="Ivar Jacobson">Ivar Jacobson</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Rumbaugh" title="James Rumbaugh">James Rumbaugh</a> in the 1990s.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="IBM_1130">IBM 1130</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grady_Booch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: IBM 1130">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Booch got his first exposure to programming on an <a href="/wiki/IBM_1130" title="IBM 1130">IBM 1130</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<blockquote><p>...  I pounded the doors at the local IBM sales office until a salesman took pity on me. After we chatted for a while, he handed me a Fortran [manual]. I'm sure he gave it to me thinking, "I'll never hear from this kid again." I returned the following week saying, "This is really cool. I've read the whole thing and have written a small program. Where can I find a computer?" The fellow, to my delight, found me programming time on an IBM 1130 on weekends and late-evening hours. That was my first programming experience, and I must thank that anonymous IBM salesman for launching my career. Thank you, IBM.</p></blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Booch_method">Booch method</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grady_Booch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Booch method">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Booch-diagram.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Booch-diagram.png/250px-Booch-diagram.png" decoding="async" width="250" height="161" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Booch-diagram.png/375px-Booch-diagram.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Booch-diagram.png/500px-Booch-diagram.png 2x" data-file-width="569" data-file-height="367" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Booch-diagram.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Class diagram</div></div></div>
<p>Booch developed the <a href="/wiki/Booch_method" title="Booch method">Booch method</a> of software development, which he presents in his 1991/94 book, <i>Object Oriented Analysis and Design With Applications</i>. He advises adding more classes to simplify complex code. The <a href="/wiki/Booch_method" title="Booch method">Booch method</a> is a technique used in <a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">software engineering</a>. It is an <a href="/wiki/Object_modeling_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Object modeling language">object modeling language</a> and methodology that was widely used in <a href="/wiki/Object-oriented_analysis_and_design" title="Object-oriented analysis and design">object-oriented analysis and design</a>. It was developed by Booch while at <a href="/wiki/Rational_Software" title="Rational Software">Rational Software</a>.
</p><p>The notation aspect of the Booch method has now been superseded by the <a href="/wiki/Unified_Modeling_Language" title="Unified Modeling Language">Unified Modeling Language</a> (UML), which features graphical elements from the Booch method along with elements from the <a href="/wiki/Object-modeling_technique" title="Object-modeling technique">object-modeling technique</a> (OMT) and <a href="/wiki/Object-oriented_software_engineering" class="mw-redirect" title="Object-oriented software engineering">object-oriented software engineering</a> (OOSE).
</p><p>Methodological aspects of the Booch method have been incorporated into several methodologies and processes, the primary such methodology being the <a href="/wiki/Rational_Unified_Process" title="Rational Unified Process">Rational Unified Process</a> (RUP).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_patterns">Design patterns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grady_Booch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Design patterns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Booch is also an advocate of <a href="/wiki/Design_pattern_(computer_science)" class="mw-redirect" title="Design pattern (computer science)">design patterns</a>. For instance, he wrote the <a href="/wiki/Foreword" title="Foreword">foreword</a> to  <i><a href="/wiki/Design_Patterns_(book)" class="mw-redirect" title="Design Patterns (book)">Design Patterns</a></i>, an early and highly influential book in the field.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="IBM_Research_-_Almaden">IBM Research - Almaden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grady_Booch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: IBM Research - Almaden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He now is part of <a href="/wiki/IBM_Research_-_Almaden" class="mw-redirect" title="IBM Research - Almaden">IBM Research - Almaden</a>, serving as Chief Scientist for Software Engineering, where he continues his work on the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://handbookofsoftwarearchitecture.com/">Handbook of Software Architecture</a> and also leads several projects in software engineering that are beyond the constraints of immediate product horizons. Grady continues to engage with customers working on real problems and maintains deep relationships with academia and other research organizations around the world. Grady has served as architect and architectural mentor for numerous complex software-intensive systems around the world in just about every domain imaginable.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_honors">Awards and honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grady_Booch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Awards and honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:GradyBoochAtIEEETechIgnite2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/GradyBoochAtIEEETechIgnite2017.jpg/220px-GradyBoochAtIEEETechIgnite2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/GradyBoochAtIEEETechIgnite2017.jpg/330px-GradyBoochAtIEEETechIgnite2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/GradyBoochAtIEEETechIgnite2017.jpg/440px-GradyBoochAtIEEETechIgnite2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4128" data-file-height="2322" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:GradyBoochAtIEEETechIgnite2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Booch at the <a href="/wiki/IEEE_Computer_Society" title="IEEE Computer Society">IEEE Computer Society</a>'s first TechIgnite conference</div></div></div>
<p>In 1995, Booch was inducted as a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He was named an <a href="/wiki/IBM_Fellow" title="IBM Fellow">IBM Fellow</a> in 2003, soon after his entry into IBM, and assumed his current role on March 18, 2008.  He was recognized as an <a href="/wiki/IEEE" class="mw-redirect" title="IEEE">IEEE</a> Fellow in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>  In 2012, the British Computer Society announced Booch would receive the Lovelace Medal and give the 2013 Lovelace Lecture.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He gave the <a href="/wiki/Turing_Lecture" class="mw-redirect" title="Turing Lecture">Turing Lecture</a> in 2007. He was awarded the IEEE Computer Society Computer Pioneer award in 2016 for his pioneering work in Object Modeling that led to the creation of the Unified Modeling Language (UML).<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
