<p><b>David Robert Filo</b> (born April 20, 1966) is an American billionaire businessman and the co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a> with <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Yang" title="Jerry Yang">Jerry Yang</a>. His Filo Server Program, written in the <a href="/wiki/C_(programming_language)" title="C (programming language)">C</a> programming language, was the server-side software used to dynamically serve variable web pages, called Filo Server Pages, on visits to early versions of the Yahoo! website.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Filo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Filo was born in <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a>. At age six, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Moss_Bluff,_Louisiana" title="Moss Bluff, Louisiana">Moss Bluff</a>, a suburb of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Charles,_Louisiana" title="Lake Charles, Louisiana">Lake Charles, Louisiana</a>.  He graduated from Sam Houston High School and then earned a B.S. degree in <a href="/wiki/Computer_engineering" title="Computer engineering">computer engineering</a> at <a href="/wiki/Tulane_University" title="Tulane University">Tulane University</a> (through the <a href="/wiki/Dean%27s_Honor_Scholarship" class="mw-redirect" title="Dean&#39;s Honor Scholarship">Dean's Honor Scholarship</a>) and an M.S. degree in 1990 at <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_profile_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes_profile-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Filo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February 1994, he co-created with <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Yang_(entrepreneur)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jerry Yang (entrepreneur)">Jerry Yang</a> an <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a> website called "Jerry and David's Guide to the World Wide Web", consisting of a directory of other websites. It was renamed "Yahoo!" (an exclamation). Yahoo! became very popular, and Filo and Yang realized the business potential and co-founded <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo! Inc.</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Yahoo! started off as a <a href="/wiki/Web_portal" title="Web portal">web portal</a> with a web directory providing an extensive range of products and services for various online activities. Yahoo was one of the pioneers of the early Internet era in the 1990s. It is still one of the leading internet brands and, due to partnerships with telecommunications firms, is one of the most visited <a href="/wiki/Website" title="Website">websites</a> on the internet.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Filo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Filo is married to photographer and teacher Angela Buenning.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> They have one child, and live in Palo Alto, California.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_profile_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes_profile-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005 he gave $30 million to his alma mater, <a href="/wiki/Tulane_University" title="Tulane University">Tulane University</a>, for use in its <a href="/wiki/Tulane_University_School_of_Science_and_Engineering" title="Tulane University School of Science and Engineering">School of Engineering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_profile_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes_profile-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of September 2019, <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> estimated Filo to be worth $4.3 billion, ranking him the 379th-richest person in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-Forbes_profile_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes_profile-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
