	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" >Andrew Braybrook</h1>
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<b>Andrew Braybrook</b> (b. 1960) is a <a href="/wiki/Software_engineer" title="Software engineer">software engineer</a>, and former <a href="/wiki/Game_programmer" class="mw-redirect" title="Game programmer">game programmer</a>. He created <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video games</a> such as <i><a href="/wiki/Paradroid" title="Paradroid">Paradroid</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gribbly%27s_Day_Out" title="Gribbly&#39;s Day Out">Gribbly's Day Out</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Fire_and_Ice_(video_game)" title="Fire and Ice (video game)">Fire and Ice</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Uridium" title="Uridium">Uridium</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Morpheus_(1987_video_game)" title="Morpheus (1987 video game)">Morpheus</a></i>. He also programmed the <a href="/wiki/Amiga" title="Amiga">Commodore Amiga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atari_ST" title="Atari ST">Atari ST</a> conversion of the <a href="/wiki/Arcade_game" title="Arcade game">arcade game</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Rainbow Islands">Rainbow Islands</a></i>.
<sup id="cite_ref-theone_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theone-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Braybrook&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Braybrook started out writing accounting programs for <a href="/wiki/GEC_Marconi" class="mw-redirect" title="GEC Marconi">GEC Marconi</a> using <a href="/wiki/COBOL" title="COBOL">COBOL</a> in 1979.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In his spare time he wrote games in <a href="/wiki/BASIC" title="BASIC">BASIC</a> for the ZX80, ZX81 and Dragon 32.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His first foray into professional games came while he was playing <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bass guitar</a> in a rock band with <a href="/wiki/Steve_Turner_(game_programmer)" title="Steve Turner (game programmer)">Steve Turner</a>. Steve was writing games for the <a href="/wiki/ZX_Spectrum" title="ZX Spectrum">ZX Spectrum</a> in his spare time and decided to make his break into full-time games production by starting the company which went on to become <a href="/wiki/Graftgold" title="Graftgold">Graftgold</a>. A few months after its inception, Turner asked Braybrook to join him in September 1983. Braybrook was commissioned by the magazine <a href="/wiki/Zzap!64" title="Zzap!64">Zzap!64</a> to write a diary detailing the making of the video game <i>Paradroid</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-diary_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-diary-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> This was followed by a subsequent diary published in the magazine about his game <i>Morpheus</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Braybrook&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1986 Braybrook was voted Best Programmer of the Year at the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Joystick_Awards" title="Golden Joystick Awards">Golden Joystick Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 1998 to July 2016 Braybrook worked as a senior <a href="/wiki/Software_developer" class="mw-redirect" title="Software developer">software developer</a> for Eurobase International. Since then he has worked as a freelance writer, programmer and game designer.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Several games have been created by taking inspiration from <i><a href="/wiki/Uridium" title="Uridium">Uridium</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
