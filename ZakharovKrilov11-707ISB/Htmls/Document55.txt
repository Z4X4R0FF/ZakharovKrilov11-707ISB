<p><b>Howard G. Cunningham</b> (born May 26, 1949) is an American <a href="/wiki/Computer_programmer" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer programmer">computer programmer</a> who developed the first <a href="/wiki/Wiki" title="Wiki">wiki</a> and was a co-author of the <i><a href="/wiki/Agile_software_development#The_Agile_Manifesto" title="Agile software development">Manifesto for Agile Software Development</a></i>. A pioneer in both <a href="/wiki/Design_pattern_(computer_science)" class="mw-redirect" title="Design pattern (computer science)">design patterns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Extreme_programming" title="Extreme programming">extreme programming</a>, he started coding the <a href="/wiki/WikiWikiWeb" title="WikiWikiWeb">WikiWikiWeb</a> in 1994, and installed it on c2.com (the website of his software consultancy) on March 25, 1995, as an add-on to the <a href="/wiki/Portland_Pattern_Repository" title="Portland Pattern Repository">Portland Pattern Repository</a>. He co-authored (with <a href="/wiki/Bo_Leuf" title="Bo Leuf">Bo Leuf</a>) a book about wikis, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wiki_Way" title="The Wiki Way">The Wiki Way</a></i>, and invented the <a href="/wiki/Framework_for_Integrated_Tests" class="mw-redirect" title="Framework for Integrated Tests">Framework for Integrated Tests</a>.
</p><p>Cunningham was a keynote speaker at the first three instances of the <a href="/wiki/WikiSym" class="mw-redirect" title="WikiSym">WikiSym</a> conference series on wiki research and practice, and also at the Wikimedia Developer Summit 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_employment">Early life and employment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ward_Cunningham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and employment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Howard G. Cunningham was born in <a href="/wiki/Michigan_City,_Indiana" title="Michigan City, Indiana">Michigan City, Indiana</a>, on May 26, 1949.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He grew up in <a href="/wiki/Highland,_Lake_County,_Indiana" title="Highland, Lake County, Indiana">Highland, Indiana</a>, staying there through high school.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He received his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor&#39;s degree">bachelor's degree</a> in interdisciplinary engineering (<a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>) and his <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master&#39;s degree">master's degree</a> in computer science from <a href="/wiki/Purdue_University" title="Purdue University">Purdue University</a>, graduating in 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He is a co-founder of Cunningham &amp; Cunningham, a software consultancy he started with his wife.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He has also served as Director of R&amp;D at Wyatt Software and as Principal Engineer in the <a href="/wiki/Tektronix" title="Tektronix">Tektronix</a> Computer Research Laboratory. He is founder of <a href="/wiki/The_Hillside_Group" title="The Hillside Group">The Hillside Group</a> and has served as program chair of the <a href="/wiki/Pattern_Languages_of_Programming" class="mw-redirect" title="Pattern Languages of Programming">Pattern Languages of Programming</a> conference which it sponsors. Cunningham was part of the <a href="/wiki/Smalltalk" title="Smalltalk">Smalltalk</a> community.
</p><p>From December 2003 until October 2005, Cunningham worked for <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Microsoft Corporation">Microsoft</a> in the "Patterns &amp; Practices" group. From October 2005 to May 2007, he held the position of Director of Committer Community Development at the <a href="/wiki/Eclipse_Foundation" title="Eclipse Foundation">Eclipse Foundation</a>. In May 2009, he joined <a href="/wiki/AboutUs.org" class="mw-redirect" title="AboutUs.org">AboutUs</a> as its <a href="/wiki/Chief_technology_officer" title="Chief technology officer">chief technology officer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On March 24, 2011 <i><a href="/wiki/The_Oregonian" title="The Oregonian">The Oregonian</a></i> reported that Cunningham had quietly departed AboutUs to join the <a href="/wiki/Venice_Beach" class="mw-redirect" title="Venice Beach">Venice Beach</a>-based CitizenGlobal, a startup working on crowd-sourced video content, as their chief technology officer and the Co-Creation Czar.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He remains "an adviser" with AboutUs.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Cunningham left CitizenGlobal and is now a programmer at <a href="/wiki/New_Relic" title="New Relic">New Relic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-relic_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-relic-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ideas_and_inventions">Ideas and inventions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ward_Cunningham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ideas and inventions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ward_Cunningham_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Ward_Cunningham_1.jpg/220px-Ward_Cunningham_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="169" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Ward_Cunningham_1.jpg/330px-Ward_Cunningham_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Ward_Cunningham_1.jpg/440px-Ward_Cunningham_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3161" data-file-height="2432" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ward_Cunningham_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Cunningham in October 2011</div></div></div>
<p>Cunningham is well known for a few widely disseminated ideas which he originated and developed. The most famous among these are the <a href="/wiki/Wiki" title="Wiki">wiki</a> and many ideas in the field of <a href="/wiki/Software_design_pattern" title="Software design pattern">software design patterns</a>, made popular by the <i><a href="/wiki/Gang_of_Four_(software)" class="mw-redirect" title="Gang of Four (software)">Gang of Four</a></i> (GoF). He owns the company Cunningham &amp; Cunningham Inc., a consultancy that has specialized in <a href="/wiki/Object-oriented_programming" title="Object-oriented programming">object-oriented programming</a>. He also created the site (and software) <a href="/wiki/WikiWikiWeb" title="WikiWikiWeb">WikiWikiWeb</a>, the first internet wiki.
</p><p>When asked in a 2006 interview with internetnews.com whether he considered patenting the wiki concept, he explained that he thought the idea "just sounded like something that no one would want to pay money for."<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Cunningham is interested in tracking the number and location of wiki page edits as a sociological experiment and may even consider the degradation of a wiki page as part of its process to stability. "There are those who give and those who take. You can tell by reading what they write."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, Cunningham created <a href="/wiki/Smallest_Federated_Wiki" title="Smallest Federated Wiki">Smallest Federated Wiki</a>, a tool for wiki federation, which applies aspects of software development such as <a href="/wiki/Fork_(software_development)" title="Fork (software development)">forking</a> to wiki pages. He signed the Manifesto for Agile Software Development <sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cunningham has contributed to the practice of <a href="/wiki/Object-oriented_programming" title="Object-oriented programming">object-oriented programming</a>, in particular the use of <a href="/wiki/Pattern_language" title="Pattern language">pattern languages</a> and (with <a href="/wiki/Kent_Beck" title="Kent Beck">Kent Beck</a>) the <a href="/wiki/Class-responsibility-collaboration_card" title="Class-responsibility-collaboration card">class-responsibility-collaboration cards</a>. He also contributes to the <a href="/wiki/Extreme_programming" title="Extreme programming">extreme programming</a> <a href="/wiki/Software_development_methodology" class="mw-redirect" title="Software development methodology">software development methodology</a>. Much of this work was done collaboratively on the first wiki site.
</p><p>Cunningham is credited with the idea: "The best way to get the right answer on the Internet is not to ask a question; it's to post the wrong answer."<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> This refers to the observation that people are quicker to correct a wrong answer than to answer a question. According to <a href="/wiki/Steven_McGeady" title="Steven McGeady">Steven McGeady</a>, Cunningham advised him of this on a whim in the early 1980s, and McGeady dubbed this <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Cunningham%27s_Law" class="extiw" title="meta:Cunningham&#39;s Law">Cunningham's law</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Although originally referring to interactions on <a href="/wiki/Usenet" title="Usenet">Usenet</a>, the law has been used to describe how other online communities work, such as Wikipedia.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Cunningham himself denies ownership of the law, calling it a "misquote that disproves itself by propagating through the internet."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ward_Cunningham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cunningham lives in <a href="/wiki/Beaverton,_Oregon" title="Beaverton, Oregon">Beaverton, Oregon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-relic_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-relic-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He holds an <a href="/wiki/Amateur_Radio" class="mw-redirect" title="Amateur Radio">Amateur Radio</a> Extra Class license issued by the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Communications_Commission" title="Federal Communications Commission">Federal Communications Commission</a>, and his <a href="/wiki/Call_sign" title="Call sign">call sign</a> is Kilo Nine Oscar X-ray, K9OX.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cunningham is <a href="/wiki/Nike,_Inc." title="Nike, Inc.">Nike</a>'s first Code for a Better World Fellow.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
