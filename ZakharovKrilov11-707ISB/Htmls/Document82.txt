<p><b>Brian Jhan Fox</b> (born 1959) is an American <a href="/wiki/Computer_programmer" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer programmer">computer programmer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Free_software" title="Free software">free software</a> advocate. He is the original author of the <a href="/wiki/GNU" title="GNU">GNU</a> <a href="/wiki/Bash_(Unix_shell)" title="Bash (Unix shell)">Bash</a> <a href="/wiki/Operating_system_shell" class="mw-redirect" title="Operating system shell">shell</a>, which he announced as a beta in June 1989.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He continued as the primary maintainer of bash until at least early 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Fox also built the first interactive <a href="/wiki/Online_banking" title="Online banking">online banking</a> software in the U.S. for <a href="/wiki/Wells_Fargo" title="Wells Fargo">Wells Fargo</a> in 1995,<sup id="cite_ref-UCSB_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UCSB-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and he created an open source election system in 2008.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_Software_Foundation">Free Software Foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brian_Fox_(computer_programmer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Free Software Foundation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1985 Fox worked with <a href="/wiki/Richard_Stallman" title="Richard Stallman">Richard Stallman</a> at Stallman's newly created <a href="/wiki/Free_Software_Foundation" title="Free Software Foundation">Free Software Foundation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  At the FSF, Fox authored <a href="/wiki/Bash_(Unix_shell)" title="Bash (Unix shell)">GNU Bash</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Makeinfo" class="mw-redirect" title="Makeinfo">GNU Makeinfo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Info_(Unix)" title="Info (Unix)">GNU Info</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finger_protocol" title="Finger protocol">GNU Finger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Echo_(command)" title="Echo (command)">GNU Echo</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Readline" class="mw-redirect" title="Readline">readline</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and history libraries.
</p><p>He was also the maintainer of <a href="/wiki/GNU_Emacs" title="GNU Emacs">GNU Emacs</a> for a time, and made many contributions to the software that was created for the GNU Project between 1986 and 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-UCSB_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UCSB-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Open_source_election_systems">Open source election systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brian_Fox_(computer_programmer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Open source election systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2008, Fox collaborated with Alan Dechert and Brent Turner to create a completely open source election system.  The system was coded together with Parker Abercrombie, and demonstrated at the LinuxWorld conference in Moscone Center in San Francisco, August 5–7, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fox also is a founding member of both the <a href="/wiki/California_Association_of_Voting_Officials" title="California Association of Voting Officials">California Association of Voting Officials</a> (CAVO)<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and the National Association of Voting Officials (NAVO).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> These not-for-profit organizations promote open source voting systems for use in public elections. Fox co-wrote a <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">New York Times</a></i> piece in 2017 with former <a href="/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency" title="Central Intelligence Agency">CIA</a> head <a href="/wiki/R._James_Woolsey_Jr." title="R. James Woolsey Jr.">R. James Woolsey</a> advocating open source election systems as a means of securing US elections against Russian interference.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_software">Other software</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brian_Fox_(computer_programmer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other software">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fox also wrote AMACS, a cut-down implementation of <a href="/wiki/Emacs" title="Emacs">Emacs</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Apple_II_series" title="Apple II series">Apple II series</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relatives">Relatives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brian_Fox_(computer_programmer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Relatives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He is the fourth born in a family of six siblings, composer and musician <a href="/wiki/Donal_Fox" title="Donal Fox">Donal Fox</a>, Thaddeus Fox, sister Ena Fox, Daniel Fox and sister Sara Fox-Ray. He lives in Santa Barbara with longtime partner Lissa Liggett and their three children.
</p><p>He is the son of physicist and educator Herbert Fox<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and grandson of artist Daniel Fox, creator of the <a href="/wiki/Rich_Uncle_Pennybags" title="Rich Uncle Pennybags">Monopoly Man</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
