<p><b>Donald Becker</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Programmer" title="Programmer">computer programmer</a> who wrote <a href="/wiki/Ethernet" title="Ethernet">Ethernet</a> drivers for the <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a> operating system.<sup id="cite_ref-Yaghmour2003_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Yaghmour2003-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Becker, in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Sterling_(computing)" title="Thomas Sterling (computing)">Thomas Sterling</a>, created the  <a href="/wiki/Beowulf_cluster" title="Beowulf cluster">Beowulf clustering</a> software while at <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>, to connect many inexpensive PCs to solve complex math problems typically reserved for classic <a href="/wiki/Supercomputer" title="Supercomputer">supercomputers</a>. For this work, Becker received the <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Bell_Prize" title="Gordon Bell Prize">Gordon Bell Prize</a> in 1997. 
</p><p>Becker became the Chief Technology Officer (CTO) of Scyld Computer Corporation, a wholly owned subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Penguin_Computing" title="Penguin Computing">Penguin Computing</a>, a developer and supplier of Beowulf clusters.
</p>
