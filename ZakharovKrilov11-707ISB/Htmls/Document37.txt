<p><b>Sergey Mikhaylovich Brin</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Серге́й Миха́йлович Брин</span>, <a href="/wiki/Scientific_transliteration_of_Cyrillic" title="Scientific transliteration of Cyrillic"><small>tr.</small></a> <i>Sergéj Mixájlovič Brin</i>; born August 21, 1973) is an American <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a> and Internet entrepreneur. Together with <a href="/wiki/Larry_Page" title="Larry Page">Larry Page</a>, he co-founded <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>. Brin was the president of Google's parent company, <a href="/wiki/Alphabet_Inc." title="Alphabet Inc.">Alphabet Inc.</a>, until stepping down from the role on December 3, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He and Page remain at Alphabet as co-founders, <a href="/wiki/Controlling_interest" title="Controlling interest">controlling shareholders</a>, board members, and employees. As of January 2021, Brin is the 9th-richest person in the world, with an estimated net worth of US$75 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Profile_Sergey_Brin_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Profile_Sergey_Brin-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Brin immigrated to the United States with his family from the Soviet Union at the age of six. He earned his bachelor's degree at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Maryland,_College_Park" title="University of Maryland, College Park">University of Maryland, College Park</a>, following in his father's and grandfather's footsteps by studying mathematics, as well as computer science. After graduation, he enrolled in <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a> to acquire a PhD in computer science. There he met Page, with whom he built a <a href="/wiki/Web_search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Web search engine">web search engine</a>. The program became popular at Stanford, and they suspended their PhD studies to start up Google in <a href="/wiki/Susan_Wojcicki" title="Susan Wojcicki">Susan Wojcicki</a>'s garage in <a href="/wiki/Menlo_Park,_California" title="Menlo Park, California">Menlo Park</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergey_Brin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brin was born on August 21, 1973, in Moscow in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> to <a href="/wiki/Jewish" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish">Jewish</a> parents,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Mikhail and Eugenia Brin, both graduates of <a href="/wiki/Moscow_State_University" title="Moscow State University">Moscow State University</a> (MSU).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> His father is a retired mathematics professor at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Maryland" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Maryland">University of Maryland</a>, and his mother a researcher at <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Goddard_Space_Flight_Center" title="Goddard Space Flight Center">Goddard Space Flight Center</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Moment_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moment-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Brin family lived in a three-room apartment in central Moscow, which they also shared with Sergey's paternal grandmother.<sup id="cite_ref-Moment_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moment-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 1977, after his father returned from a mathematics conference in <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a>, Poland, Mikhail Brin announced that it was time for the family to emigrate.<sup id="cite_ref-Moment_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moment-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> They formally applied for their <a href="/wiki/Exit_visa" class="mw-redirect" title="Exit visa">exit visa</a> in September 1978, and as a result, his father was "promptly fired". For related reasons, his mother had to leave her job. For the next eight months, without any steady income, they were forced to take on temporary jobs as they waited, afraid their request would be denied as it was for many <a href="/wiki/Refuseniks" class="mw-redirect" title="Refuseniks">refuseniks</a>. In May 1979 they were granted their official exit visas and were allowed to leave the country.<sup id="cite_ref-Moment_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moment-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Brin family lived in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> and Paris while Mikhail Brin secured a teaching position at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Maryland" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Maryland">University of Maryland</a> with help from <a href="/wiki/Anatole_Katok" title="Anatole Katok">Anatole Katok</a>. During this time, the Brin family received support and assistance from the <a href="/wiki/HIAS" title="HIAS">Hebrew Immigrant Aid Society</a>. They arrived in the United States on October 25, 1979.<sup id="cite_ref-Moment_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moment-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hias_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hias-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Brin attended elementary school at Paint Branch <a href="/wiki/Montessori_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Montessori School">Montessori School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Adelphi,_Maryland" title="Adelphi, Maryland">Adelphi, Maryland</a>, but he received further education at home; his father, a professor in the department of mathematics at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Maryland" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Maryland">University of Maryland</a>, encouraged him to learn mathematics and his family helped him retain his Russian-language skills. He attended <a href="/wiki/Eleanor_Roosevelt_High_School_(Greenbelt,_Maryland)" class="mw-redirect" title="Eleanor Roosevelt High School (Greenbelt, Maryland)">Eleanor Roosevelt High School, Greenbelt, Maryland</a>. In September 1990, Brin enrolled in the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Maryland" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Maryland">University of Maryland</a>, where he received his Bachelor of Science from the Department of Computer Science in 1993 with honors in computer science and mathematics at the age of 19.<sup id="cite_ref-resume_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-resume-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Brin began his graduate study in computer science at <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a> on a <a href="/wiki/NSF-GRF" class="mw-redirect" title="NSF-GRF">graduate fellowship</a> from the <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation" title="National Science Foundation">National Science Foundation</a>. In 1993, he interned at <a href="/wiki/Wolfram_Research" title="Wolfram Research">Wolfram Research</a>, the developers of <a href="/wiki/Mathematica" class="mw-redirect" title="Mathematica">Mathematica</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-resume_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-resume-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> As of 2008<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sergey_Brin&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, he was on leave from his PhD studies at Stanford.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Search_engine_development">Search engine development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergey_Brin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Search engine development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During an orientation for new students at Stanford, he met <a href="/wiki/Larry_Page" title="Larry Page">Larry Page</a>. The two men seemed to disagree on most subjects, but after spending time together they "became intellectual soul-mates and close friends." Brin's focus was on developing data mining systems while Page's was in extending "the concept of inferring the importance of a research paper from its <a href="/wiki/Citations" class="mw-redirect" title="Citations">citations</a> in other papers".<sup id="cite_ref-Economist_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Economist-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Together, they authored a paper titled "The Anatomy of a Large-Scale <a href="/wiki/Hypertext" title="Hypertext">Hypertextual</a> Web Search Engine".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>
To convert the backlink data gathered by BackRub's <a href="/wiki/Web_crawler" title="Web crawler">web crawler</a> into a measure of importance for a given web page, Brin and Page developed the <a href="/wiki/PageRank" title="PageRank">PageRank algorithm</a>, and realized that it could be used to build a <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" title="Search engine">search engine</a> far superior to those existing at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-wiredbirth_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wiredbirth-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The new algorithm relied on a new kind of technology that analyzed the relevance of the <a href="/wiki/Backlink" title="Backlink">backlinks</a> that connected one Web page to another, and allowed the number of links and their rank, to determine the rank of the page.<sup id="cite_ref-Moschovitis_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moschovitis-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></p><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:157px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Page%2Bbrin_by_origa.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Page%2Bbrin_by_origa.jpg/155px-Page%2Bbrin_by_origa.jpg" decoding="async" width="155" height="137" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Page%2Bbrin_by_origa.jpg/233px-Page%2Bbrin_by_origa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Page%2Bbrin_by_origa.jpg/310px-Page%2Bbrin_by_origa.jpg 2x" data-file-width="850" data-file-height="752" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Page%2Bbrin_by_origa.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Page and Brin</div></div></div><p> Combining their ideas, they began utilizing Page's dormitory room as a machine laboratory, and extracted spare parts from inexpensive computers to create a device that they used to connect the nascent search engine with Stanford's broadband campus network.<sup id="cite_ref-wiredbirth_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wiredbirth-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After filling Page's room with equipment, they then converted Brin's dorm room into an office and programming center, where they tested their new search engine designs on the web. The rapid growth of their project caused Stanford's computing infrastructure to experience problems.<sup id="cite_ref-economist.com_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-economist.com-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Page and Brin used the former's basic <a href="/wiki/HTML" title="HTML">HTML</a> programming skills to set up a simple search page for users, as they did not have a web page developer to create anything visually elaborate. They also began using any computer part they could find to assemble the necessary computing power to handle searches by multiple users. As their search engine grew in popularity among Stanford users, it required additional <a href="/wiki/Server_(computing)" title="Server (computing)">servers</a> to process the queries. In August 1996, the initial version of Google was made available on the Stanford Web site.<sup id="cite_ref-wiredbirth_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wiredbirth-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>

And we figured, maybe this is really real."<sup id="cite_ref-economist.com_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-economist.com-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_interests">Other interests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergey_Brin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other interests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brin is working on other, more personal projects that reach beyond Google. For example, he and Page are trying to help solve the world's energy and climate problems at Google's philanthropic arm, <a href="/wiki/Google.org" title="Google.org">Google.org</a>, which invests in the <a href="/wiki/Alternative_energy" class="mw-redirect" title="Alternative energy">alternative energy</a> industry to find wider sources of <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a>. The company acknowledges that its founders want "to solve really big problems using technology".<sup id="cite_ref-windpower_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-windpower-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2010, for example, they invested in a major offshore wind power development to assist the East coast power grid,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> which will eventually become one of about a dozen offshore wind farms that are proposed for the region.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> A week earlier they introduced a car that, with "artificial intelligence", can drive itself using video cameras and radar sensors.<sup id="cite_ref-windpower_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-windpower-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In the future, drivers of cars with similar sensors would have fewer accidents. These safer vehicles could, therefore, be built lighter and require less fuel consumption.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> They are trying to get companies to create innovative solutions to increasing the world's energy supply.<sup id="cite_ref-LATimes_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> He is an investor in <a href="/wiki/Tesla_Motors" class="mw-redirect" title="Tesla Motors">Tesla Motors</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-tesla-motors-investment_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tesla-motors-investment-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> which has developed the <a href="/wiki/Tesla_Roadster_(2008)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tesla Roadster (2008)">Tesla Roadster (2008)</a>, a 244-mile (393&#160;km) range <a href="/wiki/Battery_electric_vehicle" title="Battery electric vehicle">battery electric vehicle</a> as well as the <a href="/wiki/Tesla_Model_S" title="Tesla Model S">Tesla Model S</a>, a 265-mile (426&#160;km) range battery electric vehicle.
</p><p>In 2004, he and Page were named "Persons of the Week" by <i><a href="/wiki/World_News_with_Charles_Gibson" class="mw-redirect" title="World News with Charles Gibson">ABC World News Tonight</a></i>. In January 2005 he was nominated to be one of the <a href="/wiki/World_Economic_Forum" title="World Economic Forum">World Economic Forum</a>'s "Young Global Leaders". In June 2008, Brin invested $4.5&#160;million in <a href="/wiki/Space_Adventures" title="Space Adventures">Space Adventures</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Space_tourism" title="Space tourism">space tourism</a> company. His investment will serve as a deposit for a reservation on one of Space Adventures' proposed flights in 2011. Space Adventures, the only company that sends tourists to space, has sent five of them so far.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Brin and Page jointly own a customized <a href="/wiki/Boeing_767" title="Boeing 767">Boeing 767-200</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Dassault/Dornier_Alpha_Jet" title="Dassault/Dornier Alpha Jet">Dornier Alpha Jet</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-dornierjet_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dornierjet-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> and pay $1.3&#160;million a year to house them and two <a href="/wiki/Gulfstream_V" title="Gulfstream V">Gulfstream V</a> jets owned by Google executives at <a href="/wiki/Moffett_Federal_Airfield" title="Moffett Federal Airfield">Moffett Federal Airfield</a>. The aircraft have had scientific equipment installed by <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> to allow experimental data to be collected in flight.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012 Brin has been involved with the <a href="/wiki/Project_Glass" class="mw-redirect" title="Project Glass">Project Glass</a> program and has demoed eyeglass prototypes. Project Glass is a research and development program by Google to develop an <a href="/wiki/Augmented_reality" title="Augmented reality">augmented reality</a> <a href="/wiki/Head-mounted_display" title="Head-mounted display">head-mounted display</a> (HMD).<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The intended purpose of Project Glass products would be the hands-free displaying of information currently available to most <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">smartphone</a> users,<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> and allowing for interaction with the Internet via <a href="/wiki/Natural_language_processing" title="Natural language processing">natural language</a> voice commands.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Brin was also involved in the <a href="/wiki/Google_driverless_car" class="mw-redirect" title="Google driverless car">Google driverless car</a> project. In September 2012, at the signing of the California Driverless Vehicle Bill,<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Brin predicted that within five years, robotic cars will be available to the general public.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.93present"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–present">2009–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sergey_Brin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2009–present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>In November 2009 <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> decided Brin and Page were the fifth most powerful people in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Earlier that same year, in February, Brin was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/National_Academy_of_Engineering" title="National Academy of Engineering">National Academy of Engineering</a>, which is "among the highest professional distinctions accorded to an engineer ... [and] honors those who have made outstanding contributions to engineering research, practice...". He was selected specifically, "for leadership in development of rapid indexing and retrieval of relevant information from the World Wide Web".<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In their "Profiles" of Fellows, the <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation" title="National Science Foundation">National Science Foundation</a> included a number of earlier awards:<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r996844942"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>he was a featured speaker at the <a href="/wiki/World_Economic_Forum" title="World Economic Forum">World Economic Forum</a> and the Technology, Entertainment and Design Conference. ... <i><a href="/wiki/PC_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="PC Magazine">PC Magazine</a></i> has praised Google in the Top 100 Web Sites and Search Engines (1998) and awarded Google the Technical Excellence Award, for Innovation in Web Application Development in 1999. In 2000, Google earned a <a href="/wiki/Webby_Award" title="Webby Award">Webby Award</a>, a People's Voice Award for technical achievement, and in 2001, was awarded Outstanding Search Service, Best Image Search Engine, Best Design, Most Webmaster Friendly Search Engine, and Best Search Feature at the Search Engine Watch Awards.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote></li>
<li>As of January 2021, Brin is the 9th-richest person in the world according to Forbes, with an estimated net worth of US$75&#160;billion.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
