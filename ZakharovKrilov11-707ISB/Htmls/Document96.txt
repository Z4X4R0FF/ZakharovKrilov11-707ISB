</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Website</th><td><span class="url"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.jgc.org">www<wbr />.jgc<wbr />.org</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table><p><b>John Graham-Cumming</b> is a British <a href="/wiki/Software_engineer" title="Software engineer">software engineer</a> and writer<sup id="cite_ref-twitter_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twitter-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-grauniad_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grauniad-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> best known for starting a successful petition to the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Government of the United Kingdom">Government of the United Kingdom</a> asking for an apology for its persecution of <a href="/wiki/Alan_Turing" title="Alan Turing">Alan Turing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> As of 2020<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=John_Graham-Cumming&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, he serves as <a href="/wiki/Chief_Technology_Officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Chief Technology Officer">Chief Technology Officer</a> (CTO) at <a href="/wiki/Cloudflare" title="Cloudflare">Cloudflare</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> previously he worked at <a href="/wiki/Electric_Cloud" title="Electric Cloud">Electric Cloud</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ec_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ec-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Graham-Cumming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Graham-Cumming was educated at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a> where he was awarded a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Doctor of Philosophy</a> degree in <a href="/wiki/Computer_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer Science">Computer Science</a> in 1992 for research on <a href="/wiki/Communicating_sequential_processes" title="Communicating sequential processes">communicating sequential processes</a> (CSPs) supervised by Jeff W. Sanders.<sup id="cite_ref-jgcdphil_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jgcdphil-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He was a postgraduate student at <a href="/wiki/Lady_Margaret_Hall,_Oxford" title="Lady Margaret Hall, Oxford">Lady Margaret Hall, Oxford</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jgcdphil_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jgcdphil-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Graham-Cumming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Graham-Cumming is the original writer of <a href="/wiki/POPFile" title="POPFile">POPFile</a>, an open-source, cross-platform <a href="/wiki/Email_spam" title="Email spam">email spam</a> filtering program.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He is the author of <i>The Geek Atlas</i>, a travel book,<sup id="cite_ref-tga_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tga-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and <i>The GNU Make book</i>, a how-to technical manual for the <a href="/wiki/GNU" title="GNU">GNU</a> <a href="/wiki/Make_(software)" title="Make (software)">make software</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In October 2010, he started an organization whose aim is to build <a href="/wiki/Charles_Babbage" title="Charles Babbage">Charles Babbage</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Analytical_Engine" title="Analytical Engine">Analytical Engine</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tedx_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tedx-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ted_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ted-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> known as Plan 28.<sup id="cite_ref-plan28_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-plan28-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He has also campaigned for <a href="/wiki/Open-source_software" title="Open-source software">open-source software</a> in science.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, he launched his <a href="/wiki/MovieCode" title="MovieCode">MovieCode</a> site on <a href="/wiki/Tumblr" title="Tumblr">Tumblr</a>, which aims to connect <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">film</a> screenshots to specific extracts of <a href="/wiki/Source_code" title="Source code">source code</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-itworld_johnson_20140108_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itworld_johnson_20140108-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
