<p><b>Martin Fowler</b> (18 December 1963) is a naturalized American <a href="/wiki/Software_developer" class="mw-redirect" title="Software developer">software developer</a>, author and international public speaker on software development, specialising in <a href="/wiki/Object-oriented_analysis_and_design" title="Object-oriented analysis and design">object-oriented analysis and design</a>, <a href="/wiki/Unified_Modeling_Language" title="Unified Modeling Language">UML</a>, <a href="/wiki/Design_pattern_(computer_science)" class="mw-redirect" title="Design pattern (computer science)">patterns</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Agile_software_development" title="Agile software development">agile software development</a> methodologies, including <a href="/wiki/Extreme_programming" title="Extreme programming">extreme programming</a>.
</p><p>His 1999 book <i>Refactoring</i> popularised the practice of <a href="/wiki/Code_refactoring" title="Code refactoring">code refactoring</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 2004 he introduced Presentation Model (PM), an <a href="/wiki/Architectural_pattern" title="Architectural pattern">architectural pattern</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Fowler_(software_engineer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fowler was born and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Walsall" title="Walsall">Walsall</a>, England, where he went to <a href="/wiki/Queen_Mary%27s_Grammar_School" title="Queen Mary&#39;s Grammar School">Queen Mary's Grammar School</a> for his secondary education. He graduated at <a href="/wiki/University_College_London" title="University College London">University College London</a> in 1986. In 1994 he moved to the United States, where he lives near <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> in the suburb of <a href="/wiki/Melrose,_Massachusetts" title="Melrose, Massachusetts">Melrose</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mfbio_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mfbio-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fowler started working with software in the early 1980s. Out of university in 1986 he started working in software development for <a href="/wiki/Coopers_%26_Lybrand" class="mw-redirect" title="Coopers &amp; Lybrand">Coopers &amp; Lybrand</a> until 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2000 he joined <a href="/wiki/ThoughtWorks" title="ThoughtWorks">ThoughtWorks</a>, a systems integration and consulting company,<sup id="cite_ref-mfbio_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mfbio-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> where he serves as Chief Scientist.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fowler has written nine books on the topic of software development (see <i><a href="#Publications">Publications</a></i>). He is a member of the <i><a href="/wiki/Agile_Alliance" class="mw-redirect" title="Agile Alliance">Agile Alliance</a></i> and helped create the <a href="/wiki/Agile_software_development#The_Agile_Manifesto" title="Agile software development">Manifesto for Agile Software Development</a> in 2001, along with 16 fellow signatories.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He maintains a <i><a href="/wiki/Bliki" class="mw-redirect" title="Bliki">bliki</a></i>, a mix of <a href="/wiki/Blog" title="Blog">blog</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wiki" title="Wiki">wiki</a>. He popularised the term <a href="/wiki/Dependency_Injection" class="mw-redirect" title="Dependency Injection">Dependency Injection</a> as a form of <a href="/wiki/Inversion_of_Control" class="mw-redirect" title="Inversion of Control">Inversion of Control</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
