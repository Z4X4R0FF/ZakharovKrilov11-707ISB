<p><b>Ralph William Gosper Jr.</b> (born April 26, 1943), known as <b>Bill Gosper</b>, is an American mathematician and programmer.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>   Along with <a href="/wiki/Richard_Greenblatt_(programmer)" title="Richard Greenblatt (programmer)">Richard Greenblatt</a>, he may be considered to have founded the <a href="/wiki/Hacker_(programmer_subculture)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hacker (programmer subculture)">hacker</a> community, and he holds a place of pride in the <a href="/wiki/Lisp_programming_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Lisp programming language">Lisp</a> community.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Gosper_curve" title="Gosper curve">Gosper curve</a> is named after him.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Becoming_a_hacker">Becoming a hacker</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bill_Gosper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Becoming a hacker">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In high school, Gosper was interested in <a href="/wiki/Model_rocket" title="Model rocket">model rockets</a> until one of his friends was injured in a rocketry accident and contracted a fatal brain infection.<sup id="cite_ref-mmp_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mmp-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  Gosper enrolled in <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">MIT</a> in 1961, and he received his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor&#39;s degree">bachelor's degree</a> in mathematics from MIT in 1965 despite becoming disaffected with the mathematics department because of their anti-computer attitude.<sup id="cite_ref-mmp_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mmp-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In his second year at MIT, Gosper took a programming course from <a href="/wiki/John_McCarthy_(computer_scientist)" title="John McCarthy (computer scientist)">John McCarthy</a> and became affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/MIT_AI_Lab" class="mw-redirect" title="MIT AI Lab">MIT AI Lab</a>.
</p><p>His contributions to computational mathematics include <a href="/wiki/HAKMEM" title="HAKMEM">HAKMEM</a> and the MIT <a href="/wiki/Maclisp" title="Maclisp">Maclisp</a> system. He made major contributions to <a href="/wiki/Macsyma" title="Macsyma">Macsyma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Project_MAC" class="mw-redirect" title="Project MAC">Project MAC</a>'s computer algebra system. Gosper later worked with <a href="/wiki/Symbolics" title="Symbolics">Symbolics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Macsyma,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Macsyma, Inc.">Macsyma, Inc.</a> on commercial versions of Macsyma.
</p><p>In 1974, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a>, where he lectured and worked with <a href="/wiki/Donald_Knuth" title="Donald Knuth">Donald Knuth</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mmp_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mmp-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since that time, he has worked at or consulted for <a href="/wiki/Xerox_PARC" class="mw-redirect" title="Xerox PARC">Xerox PARC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Symbolics" title="Symbolics">Symbolics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wolfram_Research" title="Wolfram Research">Wolfram Research</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Livermore_Laboratory" class="mw-redirect" title="Lawrence Livermore Laboratory">Lawrence Livermore Laboratory</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Macsyma_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Macsyma Inc.">Macsyma Inc.</a>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Key_contributions">Key contributions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bill_Gosper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Key contributions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Conway.27s_Game_of_Life"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Conway's_Game_of_Life">Conway's Game of Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bill_Gosper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Conway&#039;s Game of Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<table class="box-More_citations_needed_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" decoding="async" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs additional citations for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verification</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Bill_Gosper&amp;action=edit">improve this article</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Referencing_for_beginners" title="Help:Referencing for beginners">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Unsourced material may be challenged and removed.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">July 2019</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>He became intensely interested in the <a href="/wiki/Conway%27s_Game_of_Life" title="Conway&#39;s Game of Life">Game of Life</a> shortly after <a href="/wiki/John_Horton_Conway" title="John Horton Conway">John Horton Conway</a> had proposed it.  Conway conjectured the existence of infinitely growing patterns, and offered a reward for an example. Gosper was the first to find such a pattern, the <a href="/wiki/Gun_(cellular_automaton)" title="Gun (cellular automaton)">glider gun</a>, and won the prize.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Gosper was also the originator of the <a href="/wiki/Hashlife" title="Hashlife">Hashlife</a> algorithm that can speed up the computation of Life patterns by many orders of magnitude.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Packing_problems">Packing problems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bill_Gosper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Packing problems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gosper has created numerous <a href="/wiki/Packing_problem" class="mw-redirect" title="Packing problem">packing problem</a> puzzles, such as "Twubblesome Twelve".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbolic_computation">Symbolic computation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bill_Gosper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Symbolic computation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gosper was the first person to realize the possibilities of <a href="/wiki/Symbolic_computation" class="mw-redirect" title="Symbolic computation">symbolic computation</a> on a computer as a mathematics research tool,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> whereas computer methods were previously limited to purely numerical methods. In particular, this research resulted in his work on <a href="/wiki/Continued_fraction" title="Continued fraction">continued fraction</a><sup id="cite_ref-contfrac_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-contfrac-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> representations of real numbers and <a href="/wiki/Gosper%27s_algorithm" title="Gosper&#39;s algorithm">Gosper's algorithm</a> for finding closed form <a href="/wiki/Hypergeometric_series" class="mw-redirect" title="Hypergeometric series">hypergeometric</a> identities.
</p><p>In 1985, Gosper briefly held the world record for computing the most digits of <a href="/wiki/Pi#Rapidly_convergent_series" title="Pi">pi</a> with 17 million digits.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> See <a href="/wiki/Chronology_of_computation_of_%CF%80" title="Chronology of computation of π">chronology of computation of π</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Space-filling_curves">Space-filling curves</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bill_Gosper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Space-filling curves">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the continuity of early 20th century examples of <a href="/wiki/Space-filling_curve" title="Space-filling curve">space-filling curves</a>—the Koch-Peano curve, Cesàro and <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A9vy_C_curve" title="Lévy C curve">Lévy C curve</a>, all special cases of the general <a href="/wiki/De_Rham_curve" title="De Rham curve">de Rham curve</a>—and following the path of <a href="/wiki/Benoit_Mandelbrot" title="Benoit Mandelbrot">Benoit Mandelbrot</a>, Gosper discovered the <a href="/wiki/Peano-Gosper_curve" class="mw-redirect" title="Peano-Gosper curve">Peano-Gosper curve</a>, before engaging with variations on the <a href="/wiki/Dragon_curve" title="Dragon curve">Harter-Heighway dragon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In the late 80s, Gosper independently discovered the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gosper-Lafitte_triangle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gosper-Lafitte triangle (page does not exist)">Gosper-Lafitte triangle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
