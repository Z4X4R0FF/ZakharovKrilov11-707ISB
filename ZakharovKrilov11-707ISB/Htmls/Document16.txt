<p><b>Kent Beck</b> (born 1961) is an American <a href="/wiki/Software_engineer" title="Software engineer">software engineer</a> and the creator of <a href="/wiki/Extreme_programming" title="Extreme programming">extreme programming</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Cworld92_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cworld92-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> a <a href="/wiki/Software_development_process" title="Software development process">software development methodology</a> that eschews rigid formal specification for a collaborative and iterative design process. Beck was one of the 17 original signatories of the <a href="/wiki/Agile_software_development#The_Agile_Manifesto" title="Agile software development">Agile Manifesto</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Cworld92_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cworld92-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> the founding document for <a href="/wiki/Agile_software_development" title="Agile software development">agile software development</a>. Extreme and Agile methods are closely associated with <a href="/wiki/Test-driven_development" title="Test-driven development">Test-Driven Development</a> (TDD), of which Beck is perhaps the leading proponent.
</p><p>Beck pioneered <a href="/wiki/Software_design_pattern" title="Software design pattern">software design patterns</a>, as well as the commercial application of <a href="/wiki/Smalltalk" title="Smalltalk">Smalltalk</a>. He wrote the <a href="/wiki/SUnit" title="SUnit">SUnit</a> <a href="/wiki/Unit_testing" title="Unit testing">unit testing</a> framework for Smalltalk, which spawned the <a href="/wiki/XUnit" title="XUnit">xUnit</a> series of frameworks, notably <a href="/wiki/JUnit" title="JUnit">JUnit</a> for Java, which Beck wrote with <a href="/wiki/Erich_Gamma" title="Erich Gamma">Erich Gamma</a>. Beck popularized <a href="/wiki/Class-responsibility-collaboration_card" title="Class-responsibility-collaboration card">CRC cards</a> with <a href="/wiki/Ward_Cunningham" title="Ward Cunningham">Ward Cunningham</a>, the inventor of the wiki.
</p><p>He lives in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a> and worked at social media company <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Kent has worked at <a href="/wiki/Gusto_(company)" title="Gusto (company)">Gusto</a> since April 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kent_Beck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beck attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oregon" title="University of Oregon">University of Oregon</a> between 1979 and 1987, receiving <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">B.S.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Science" title="Master of Science">M.S.</a> degrees in <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oregon_Department_of_Computer_and_Information_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Oregon Department of Computer and Information Science">computer and information science</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1996 Beck was hired to work on the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Comprehensive_Compensation_System" title="Chrysler Comprehensive Compensation System">Chrysler Comprehensive Compensation System</a>. Beck in turn brought in <a href="/wiki/Ron_Jeffries" title="Ron Jeffries">Ron Jeffries</a>. In March 1996 the development team estimated the system would be ready to go into production around one year later.  In 1997 the development team adopted a way of working which is now formalized as <a href="/wiki/Extreme_programming" title="Extreme programming">extreme programming</a>.<sup class="reference plainlinks nourlexpansion" id="ref_dateOfXpAdoption"><a class="external autonumber" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kent_Beck#endnote_dateOfXpAdoption">[1]</a></sup> The one-year delivery target was nearly achieved, with actual delivery being only a couple of months late.
</p>
