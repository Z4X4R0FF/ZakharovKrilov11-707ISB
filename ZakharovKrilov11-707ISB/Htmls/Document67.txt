<p><b>Moodle</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Free_and_open-source" class="mw-redirect" title="Free and open-source">free and open-source</a> <a href="/wiki/Learning_management_system" title="Learning management system">learning management system</a> (LMS) written in <a href="/wiki/PHP" title="PHP">PHP</a> and distributed under the <a href="/wiki/GNU_General_Public_License" title="GNU General Public License">GNU General Public License</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Developed on <a href="/wiki/Pedagogy" title="Pedagogy">pedagogical</a> principles,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Moodle is used for <a href="/wiki/Blended_learning" title="Blended learning">blended learning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Distance_education" title="Distance education">distance education</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flipped_classroom" title="Flipped classroom">flipped classroom</a> and other <a href="/wiki/E-learning" class="mw-redirect" title="E-learning">e-learning</a> projects in schools, universities, workplaces and other sectors.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With customizable management features, it is used to create private <a href="/wiki/Websites" class="mw-redirect" title="Websites">websites</a> with online courses for educators and trainers to achieve learning goals.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Moodle allows for extending and tailoring learning environments using community-sourced plugins.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moodle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moodle was originally developed by Martin Dougiamas to help educators create online courses with a focus on interaction and collaborative construction of content, and it is in continual evolution. The first version of Moodle was released on 20&#160;August 2002<span class="noprint">&#32;(18 years ago)</span><span style="display:none">&#160;(<span class="bday dtstart published updated">2002-08-20</span>)</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Moodle Project is led and coordinated by Moodle HQ, an Australian company of 50 developers which is financially supported by a network of eighty-four Moodle Partner service companies worldwide. Moodle's development has also been assisted by the work of open-source programmers.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Moodle is a learning platform used to augment and move existing learning environments online.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> As an <a href="/wiki/E-learning" class="mw-redirect" title="E-learning">E-learning</a> tool, Moodle developed a number of features<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> now considered standard for learning management systems, such as a calendar and a gradebook.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Moodle is a <a href="/wiki/Virtual_learning_environment" title="Virtual learning environment">virtual learning environment</a><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and can be used in many types of environments such as education, training, and development<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and in <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">business</a> settings.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Plugins">Plugins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moodle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plugins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Plug-in_(computing)" title="Plug-in (computing)">Plugins</a> are a flexible tool set, allowing Moodle users to extend the features of the site. Moodle has hundreds of available plugins which extend the features of Moodle's core functionality.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Each plugin is maintained in the Moodle plugins directory. As of October 2019, 1,619 plugins were available for Moodle, with over 380,400 recent downloads.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Themes">Themes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moodle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Themes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Graphical themes for Moodle can be installed to change the look and functionality of a Moodle site or of an individual course.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mobile">Mobile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moodle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mobile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Many Moodle themes, based on <a href="/wiki/Responsive_web_design" title="Responsive web design">responsive web design</a>, allow Moodle to be used on <a href="/wiki/Mobile_device" title="Mobile device">mobile devices</a>. Also, a Moodle <a href="/wiki/Mobile_app" title="Mobile app">mobile app</a> is available in <a href="/wiki/Google_Play" title="Google Play">Google Play</a>, <a href="/wiki/App_Store_(iOS)" class="mw-redirect" title="App Store (iOS)">App Store (iOS)</a>, <a href="/wiki/F-Droid" title="F-Droid">F-Droid</a> (Android <a href="/wiki/Free/Libre_Open_Source_Software" class="mw-redirect" title="Free/Libre Open Source Software">FLOSS</a> repository), and the <a href="/wiki/Windows_Phone_Store" title="Windows Phone Store">Windows Phone Store</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="E-learning_standards_support">E-learning standards support</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moodle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: E-learning standards support">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moodle has adopted the following e-learning standards:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Sharable_Content_Object_Reference_Model" title="Sharable Content Object Reference Model">Sharable Content Object Reference Model (SCORM)</a> is a collection of E-learning standards and specifications that define communications between client side content and a server side learning management system, as well as how externally authored content should be packaged in order to integrate with the LMS effectively. There are two versions: SCORM 1.2 and SCORM 2004. Moodle is SCORM 1.2 compliant, and passes all the tests in the ADL Conformance test suite 1.2.7 for SCORM 1.2. SCORM 2004 is not supported in Moodle, however Rustici Software have a Moodle plugin which can turn any Moodle site into a fully compliant SCORM 2004 LMS.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The AICC HACP standard for CMI was developed by the <a href="/wiki/Aviation_Industry_Computer-Based_Training_Committee" title="Aviation Industry Computer-Based Training Committee">Aviation Industry Computer-Based Training Committee (AICC)</a> and is used to call externally authored content and assessment packages. AICC content packages are supported in Moodle 2.1 and later versions.</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=IMS_Global&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="IMS Global (page does not exist)">IMS</a> Common Cartridge packages can also be imported into Moodle. In addition, Moodle Book activities can be exported as IMS Content Packages.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Learning_Tools_Interoperability" title="Learning Tools Interoperability">Learning Tools Interoperability</a> (LTI) is a standard way of integrating rich learning applications (often remotely hosted and provided through third-party services) with educational platforms. Moodle uses the External Tool activity to act as an 'LTI consumer' as standard, and will act as an 'LTI provider' using a plugin.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Deployment">Deployment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moodle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Deployment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Users can download and install Moodle on a <a href="/wiki/Web_server" title="Web server">Web server</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Apache_HTTP_Server" title="Apache HTTP Server">Apache HTTP Server</a>, and a number of database management systems, such as <a href="/wiki/MySQL" title="MySQL">MySQL</a>, are supported. Pre-built combinations of Moodle with a Web server and database are available for <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a> and <a href="/wiki/Macintosh" title="Macintosh">Macintosh</a>. Other automated installation approaches exist, such as installing  a <a href="/wiki/Debian" title="Debian">Debian</a> package, deploying a ready-to-use <a href="/wiki/TurnKey_Linux_Virtual_Appliance_Library" title="TurnKey Linux Virtual Appliance Library">TurnKey</a> Moodle appliance, using the <a href="/wiki/Bitnami" title="Bitnami">Bitnami</a> installer, or using a "one-click install" service such as <a href="/wiki/Installatron" title="Installatron">Installatron</a>.
</p><p>Certified Moodle Partners provide other Moodle services, including hosting, training, customization and content development.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> This network of providers support development of the Moodle project through royalties.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Needs reliable, third-party published sources (not from Moodle, press releases, blogs, etc.) in order to keep this claim in the article. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Interoperability">Interoperability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moodle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Interoperability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moodle runs without modification on <a href="/wiki/Unix" title="Unix">Unix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a>, <a href="/wiki/FreeBSD" title="FreeBSD">FreeBSD</a>, <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Windows</a>, <a href="/wiki/OS_X" class="mw-redirect" title="OS X">OS X</a>, <a href="/wiki/NetWare" title="NetWare">NetWare</a> and any other systems that support <a href="/wiki/PHP" title="PHP">PHP</a> and a database, including webhost providers.
</p><p>Moodle also has import features for use with other specific systems, such as importing quizzes or entire courses from <a href="/wiki/Blackboard_Learning_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Blackboard Learning System">Blackboard</a> or WebCT. As of February&#160;2010<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Moodle&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, Moodle did not import Blackboard courses, apparently due to changes in php code-releases.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2012 Blackboard acquired two companies based on Moodle's software including Baltimore-based <a href="/wiki/Moodlerooms" title="Moodlerooms">Moodlerooms</a> Inc. and NetSpot of Adelaide, Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In August 2015, Blackboard acquired Colombia-based Nivel7, possibly the largest Moodle services provider in Latin America.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Red_Hat" title="Red Hat">Red Hat</a> site, Opensource.com, reported that Moodle will always be an <a href="/wiki/Open-source_software" title="Open-source software">open-source</a> project, with clear delineation between Blackboard and Moodle.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moodle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moodle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Martin Dougiamas, who has graduate degrees in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a>, wrote the first version of Moodle. Dougiamas started a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Ph.D.</a> to examine "the use of <a href="/wiki/Open_source_software" class="mw-redirect" title="Open source software">open source software</a> to support a <a href="/wiki/Social_constructionist" class="mw-redirect" title="Social constructionist">social constructionist</a> <a href="/wiki/Epistemology" title="Epistemology">epistemology</a> of teaching and learning within Internet-based communities of reflective inquiry." Although how exactly social constructivism makes Moodle different from other eLearning platforms is difficult to show, it has been cited as an important factor by Moodle adopters.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Other Moodle adopters, such as the <a href="/wiki/Open_University" title="Open University">Open University</a> in the UK, have pointed out that Learning Management Systems can equally be seen as "relatively pedagogy-neutral".<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pedagogical_approach">Pedagogical approach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moodle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Pedagogical approach">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The stated philosophy of Moodle includes a <a href="/wiki/Constructivism_(pedagogical)" class="mw-redirect" title="Constructivism (pedagogical)">constructivist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Social_constructionism" title="Social constructionism">social constructionist</a> approach to education, emphasizing that learners (and not just teachers) can contribute to the educational experience.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Using these <a href="/wiki/Pedagogy" title="Pedagogy">pedagogical</a> principles, Moodle provides an environment for learning communities.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_of_name">Origin of name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moodle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Origin of name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The acronym <i>Moodle</i> officially stands for <i>modular object-oriented dynamic learning environment</i>. When the project was first started, the "m" instead stood for "Martin's", after original developer Martin Dougiamas. Besides being an acronym, the name was also chosen because of the dictionary definition of Moodle, with connotations such as "tinkering", "insight", and "creativity",<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> as well as to correspond to an available domain name.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moodle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moodle has continued to evolve since 1999 (since 2001 with the current architecture). It has been translated into over 100 different languages and is accessible in many countries worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Institutions can add as many Moodle servers as needed without having to pay license fees. The <a href="/wiki/Open_University" title="Open University">Open University</a> of the UK currently uses a Moodle installation for their 200,000 users<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> while the UK government uses a Moodle installation for their Civil Service Learning platform serving half a million employees.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Moodle_conferences">Moodle conferences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moodle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Moodle conferences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A MoodleMoot is a conference for Moodle community members, held to learn about Moodle, share experiences of the learning platform, discuss research in related educational technologies and contribute ideas to future Moodle development.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Held around the world, MoodleMoots are organised by universities or other large organizations using Moodle, Moodle Partners, Moodle associations or Moodle HQ. 2020 saw the #Moot move to a virtual conference. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Adoption">Adoption</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moodle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Adoption">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moodle has over 50% of market share in Europe, Latin America, and Oceania.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the U.S. higher education market as of fall 2018, the top three learning management systems (LMS) by number of institutions were Blackboard (31%), Canvas (30%), and Moodle (18%). --- In 2013, Moodle was for a time the second largest provider with 23% <a href="/wiki/Market_share" title="Market share">market share</a> (following <a href="/wiki/Blackboard_Learning_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Blackboard Learning System">Blackboard</a> (41%).<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> However, by 2017, Moodle had dropped to the third largest provider, due in part to increased adoption of <a href="/wiki/Instructure" title="Instructure">Instructure's</a> semi-opensource<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Canvas platform.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> In March 2016, Blackboard became an official Moodle partner, although this was later completely revoked.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
