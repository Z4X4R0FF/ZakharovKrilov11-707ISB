<p><b>Bradley Joseph Fitzpatrick</b> (born February 5, 1980) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Programmer" title="Programmer">programmer</a>. He is best known as the creator of <a href="/wiki/LiveJournal" title="LiveJournal">LiveJournal</a> and is the author of a variety of <a href="/wiki/Free_software" title="Free software">free software</a> projects such as <a href="/wiki/Memcached" title="Memcached">memcached</a>, <a href="/wiki/PubSubHubbub" class="mw-redirect" title="PubSubHubbub">PubSubHubbub</a>, <a href="/wiki/OpenID" title="OpenID">OpenID</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Perkeep" title="Perkeep">Perkeep</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Fitzpatrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">Iowa</a>, Fitzpatrick grew up in <a href="/wiki/Beaverton,_Oregon" title="Beaverton, Oregon">Beaverton, Oregon</a>, and majored in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Washington" title="University of Washington">University of Washington</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>. He started his first company, FreeVote.com, while in high school.<sup id="cite_ref-Chron_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chron-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Fitzpatrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>LiveJournal grew out of a journaling program Fitzpatrick wrote for himself as a college freshman.<sup id="cite_ref-BW_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Chron_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chron-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It eventually became a full-time job and then a company; in January 2005 he sold it and its parent company, Danga Interactive, to <a href="/wiki/Six_Apart" title="Six Apart">Six Apart</a>, for an undisclosed sum of cash and <a href="/wiki/Stock" title="Stock">stock</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BW_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Chron_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chron-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He was named chief architect of <a href="/wiki/Six_Apart" title="Six Apart">Six Apart</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He left Six Apart in August 2007, moving to <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and in 2008, after the sale of LiveJournal to <a href="/wiki/SUP_Media" title="SUP Media">SUP Media</a>, joined the LiveJournal Advisory Board.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In June 2010 the board was dissolved,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> ending his involvement with LiveJournal. At Google he was a Staff Software Engineer and was part of the <a href="/wiki/Go_(programming_language)" title="Go (programming language)">Go programming language</a> team.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2020, Brad announced<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> he was leaving Google. Three days later he started working<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> for <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tailscale&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tailscale (page does not exist)">Tailscale</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honors">Honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Fitzpatrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In June 2014, the University of Washington School of Computer Science and Engineering gave Fitzpatrick an award for Early Career Achievement.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
