<p><b>Richard Allan Bartle</b> <a href="/wiki/FBCS" class="mw-redirect" title="FBCS">FBCS</a> <a href="/wiki/FRSA" class="mw-redirect" title="FRSA">FRSA</a> (born 10 January 1960) is a British writer, professor and game researcher<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/Massively_multiplayer_online_game" title="Massively multiplayer online game">massively multiplayer online game</a> industry.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He co-created <i><a href="/wiki/MUD1" title="MUD1">MUD1</a></i> (the first <a href="/wiki/MUD" title="MUD">MUD</a>) in 1978, and is the author of the 2003 book <i><a href="/wiki/Designing_Virtual_Worlds" title="Designing Virtual Worlds">Designing Virtual Worlds</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Bartle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1988, Bartle received a PhD in <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Essex" title="University of Essex">University of Essex</a>, where as an undergraduate, he created <i><a href="/wiki/MUD1" title="MUD1">MUD1</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Roy_Trubshaw" class="mw-redirect" title="Roy Trubshaw">Roy Trubshaw</a> in 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He lectured at Essex until 1987, when he left to work full-time on <i>MUD</i> (known as <i><a href="/wiki/MUD2" title="MUD2">MUD2</a></i> in its present version). Recently he has returned to the university as a part-time professor and principal teaching fellow in the Department of Computing and Electronic Systems, supervising courses on computer <a href="/wiki/Game_design" title="Game design">game design</a> as part of the department's degree course on computer <a href="/wiki/Game_development" class="mw-redirect" title="Game development">game development</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_of_Arts" title="Royal Society of Arts">Royal Society of Arts</a>.
</p><p>In 2003, he wrote <i><a href="/wiki/Designing_Virtual_Worlds" title="Designing Virtual Worlds">Designing Virtual Worlds</a></i>, a book about the history, ethics, structure, and technology of massively multiplayer games.
</p><p>Bartle is also a contributing editor to <i><a href="/wiki/Terra_Nova_(blog)" title="Terra Nova (blog)">Terra Nova</a></i>, a collaborative blog that deals with <a href="/wiki/Virtual_world" title="Virtual world">virtual world</a> issues.
</p><p>Bartle did research on player personality types in virtual worlds. In Bartle's analysis, players of virtual worlds can be divided into four types: achievers, explorers, socializers and killers.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This idea has been adapted into an online test generally referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Bartle_Test" class="mw-redirect" title="Bartle Test">Bartle Test</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> which is quite popular, with scores often exchanged on <a href="/wiki/Massively_multiplayer_online_game" title="Massively multiplayer online game">massively multiplayer online games</a> forums and networking sites.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Circa 2003, Bartle was reported as living in a village near <a href="/wiki/Colchester" title="Colchester">Colchester</a>, England, with his wife Gail and their two children Jennifer and Madeleine.<sup id="cite_ref-dog_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dog-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bartle is an atheist and a patron of <a href="/wiki/Humanists_UK" title="Humanists UK">Humanists UK</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
