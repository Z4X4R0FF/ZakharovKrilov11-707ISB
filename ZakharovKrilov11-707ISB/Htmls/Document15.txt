<p><b>Friedrich Ludwig</b> "<b>Fritz</b>" <b>Bauer</b> (10 June 1924 – 26 March 2015) was a German pioneer of <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> and professor at the <a href="/wiki/Technical_University_of_Munich" title="Technical University of Munich">Technical University of Munich</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friedrich_L._Bauer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bauer earned his <a href="/wiki/Abitur" title="Abitur">Abitur</a> in 1942 and served in the <a href="/wiki/Wehrmacht" title="Wehrmacht">Wehrmacht</a> during World War II, from 1943 to 1945. From 1946 to 1950, he studied mathematics and <a href="/wiki/Theoretical_physics" title="Theoretical physics">theoretical physics</a> at <a href="/wiki/Ludwig-Maximilians-Universit%C3%A4t" class="mw-redirect" title="Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität">Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität</a> in <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>. Bauer received his <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Doctor of Philosophy</a> (Ph.D.) under the supervision of <a href="/wiki/Fritz_Bopp" class="mw-redirect" title="Fritz Bopp">Fritz Bopp</a> for his thesis <i>Gruppentheoretische Untersuchungen zur Theorie der Spinwellengleichungen</i> ("Group-theoretic investigations of the theory of spin wave equations") in 1952. He completed his <a href="/wiki/Habilitation" title="Habilitation">habilitation</a> thesis <i>Über quadratisch konvergente Iterationsverfahren zur Lösung von algebraischen Gleichungen und Eigenwertproblemen</i> ("On quadratically convergent iteration methods for solving algebraic equations and eigenvalue problems") in 1954 at the <a href="/wiki/Technical_University_of_Munich" title="Technical University of Munich">Technical University of Munich</a>. After teaching as a <i><a href="/wiki/Privatdozent" title="Privatdozent">privatdozent</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_Maximilian_University_of_Munich" title="Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich">Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich</a> from 1954 to 1958, he became extraordinary professor for applied mathematics at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Mainz" title="University of Mainz">University of Mainz</a>. Since 1963, he worked as a professor of mathematics and (since 1972) computer science at the Technical University of Munich. He retired in 1989.<sup id="cite_ref-GutenbergBiographics2016_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GutenbergBiographics2016-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work">Work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friedrich_L._Bauer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:FriedrichLBauer29-05-1995.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/FriedrichLBauer29-05-1995.jpg/220px-FriedrichLBauer29-05-1995.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="169" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/FriedrichLBauer29-05-1995.jpg/330px-FriedrichLBauer29-05-1995.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/FriedrichLBauer29-05-1995.jpg/440px-FriedrichLBauer29-05-1995.jpg 2x" data-file-width="700" data-file-height="537" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:FriedrichLBauer29-05-1995.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Friedrich L. Bauer at the editors' meeting of Informatik Spektrum on 29 May 1995.</div></div></div>
<p>Bauer's early work involved constructing computing machinery (e.g. the logical relay computer STANISLAUS from 1951–1955<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>). In this context, he was the first to propose the widely used <a href="/wiki/Stack_(data_structure)" class="mw-redirect" title="Stack (data structure)">stack method</a> of expression evaluation.
</p><p>Bauer was a member of the committees that developed the <a href="/wiki/Imperative_programming" title="Imperative programming">imperative</a> computer <a href="/wiki/Programming_language" title="Programming language">programming languages</a> <a href="/wiki/ALGOL_58" title="ALGOL 58">ALGOL 58</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-GAMM_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GAMM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and its successor <a href="/wiki/ALGOL_60" title="ALGOL 60">ALGOL 60</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-IFIP_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IFIP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> important predecessors to all modern imperative programming languages. For ALGOL 58, Bauer was with the German <i><a href="/wiki/Gesellschaft_f%C3%BCr_Angewandte_Mathematik_und_Mechanik" title="Gesellschaft für Angewandte Mathematik und Mechanik">Gesellschaft für Angewandte Mathematik und Mechanik</a></i> (GAMM, Society of Applied Mathematics and Mechanics) which worked with the American <i><a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a></i> (ACM).<sup id="cite_ref-GAMM_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GAMM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> For ALGOL 60, Bauer was with the <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_for_Information_Processing" title="International Federation for Information Processing">International Federation for Information Processing</a> (IFIP) <a href="/wiki/IFIP_Working_Group_2.1" title="IFIP Working Group 2.1">IFIP Working Group 2.1</a> on Algorithmic Languages and Calculi,<sup id="cite_ref-IFIP_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IFIP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> which <a href="/wiki/Specification_(technical_standard)" title="Specification (technical standard)">specified</a>, maintains, and supports the languages ALGOL 60 and <a href="/wiki/ALGOL_68" title="ALGOL 68">ALGOL 68</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bauer was an influential figure in establishing <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> as an independent subject in German universities, which until then was usually considered part of <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>. In 1967, he held the first lecture in computer science at a German university at the <a href="/wiki/Technical_University_of_Munich" title="Technical University of Munich">Technical University of Munich</a>, titled <i>Information Processing</i>. By 1992, computer science had separated from the <a href="/wiki/TUM_Department_of_Mathematics" title="TUM Department of Mathematics">Department of Mathematics</a> to form an independent <a href="/wiki/TUM_Department_of_Informatics" title="TUM Department of Informatics">Department of Informatics</a>, where he would hold a chair until his retirement in 1989.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1968, he coined the term <i><a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">software engineering</a></i> which has been in widespread use since, and has become a discipline in computer science.
</p><p>His scientific contributions spread from <a href="/wiki/Numerical_analysis" title="Numerical analysis">numerical analysis</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bauer%E2%80%93Fike_theorem" title="Bauer–Fike theorem">Bauer–Fike theorem</a>) and fundamentals of interpretation and translation of programming languages, to his later works on systematics of program development, especially <a href="/wiki/Program_transformation" title="Program transformation">program transformation</a> methods and systems (CIP-S) and the associated <a href="/wiki/Wide-spectrum_language" title="Wide-spectrum language">wide-spectrum language</a> system CIP-L. He also wrote a well-respected book on <a href="/wiki/Cryptology" class="mw-redirect" title="Cryptology">cryptology</a>, <i>Decrypted secrets</i>, now in its fourth edition.
</p><p>He was the <a href="/wiki/Doctoral_advisor" title="Doctoral advisor">doctoral advisor</a> of 39 students, including <a href="/wiki/Rudolf_Berghammer" title="Rudolf Berghammer">Rudolf Berghammer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manfred_Broy" title="Manfred Broy">Manfred Broy</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Gries" title="David Gries">David Gries</a>, Manfred Paul, Gerhard Seegmüller, <a href="/wiki/Josef_Stoer" title="Josef Stoer">Josef Stoer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Wynn_(mathematician)" title="Peter Wynn (mathematician)">Peter Wynn</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Christoph_Zenger" title="Christoph Zenger">Christoph Zenger</a>.
</p><p>Friedrich Bauer was one of the 19 founding members of the <a href="/wiki/German_Informatics_Society" title="German Informatics Society">German Informatics Society</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He was editor of the <a href="/wiki/Informatik_Spektrum" class="mw-redirect" title="Informatik Spektrum">Informatik Spektrum</a> from its founding in 1978, and held that position until his death.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Friedrich Bauer was married to Hildegard Bauer-Vogg. He was the father of three sons and two daughters.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition_of_software_engineering">Definition of software engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friedrich_L._Bauer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Definition of software engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bauer was a colleague of the German Representative the NATO Science Committee. In 1967, NATO had been discussing 'The Software Crisis' and Bauer had suggested the term 'Software Engineering' as a way to conceive of both the problem and the solution.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1972, Bauer published the following definition of software engineering:
</p><p>"Establishment and use of sound engineering principles to economically obtain software that is reliable and works on real machines efficiently."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friedrich_L._Bauer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 1992, the <a href="/wiki/Technical_University_of_Munich" title="Technical University of Munich">Technical University of Munich</a> has awarded the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Friedrich_L._Bauer_Prize&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Friedrich L. Bauer Prize (page does not exist)">Friedrich L. Bauer Prize</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/F.L.-Bauer-Preis" class="extiw" title="de:F.L.-Bauer-Preis">de</a>&#93;</span> in computer science.
