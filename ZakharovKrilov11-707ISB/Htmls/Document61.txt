<p><b>Jeffrey Adgate "Jeff" Dean</b> (born July 1968) is an American <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Software_engineer" title="Software engineer">software engineer</a>. He is currently the lead of <a href="/wiki/Google_AI" title="Google AI">Google AI</a>, Google's AI division.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeff_Dean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dean received a B.S., summa cum laude, from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Minnesota" title="University of Minnesota">University of Minnesota</a> in Computer Science &amp; Economics in 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He received a Ph.D. in <a href="/wiki/Computer_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer Science">Computer Science</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Washington" title="University of Washington">University of Washington</a>, working under <a href="/wiki/Craig_Chambers" title="Craig Chambers">Craig Chambers</a> on <a href="/wiki/Compiler" title="Compiler">compilers</a><sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and whole-program optimization techniques for <a href="/wiki/Object-oriented_programming_languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Object-oriented programming languages">object-oriented programming languages</a> in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He was elected to the <a href="/wiki/National_Academy_of_Engineering" title="National Academy of Engineering">National Academy of Engineering</a> in 2009, which recognized his work on "the science and engineering of large-scale <a href="/wiki/Distributed_computing" title="Distributed computing">distributed computer systems</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeff_Dean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before joining <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, Dean worked at <a href="/wiki/Digital_Equipment_Corporation" title="Digital Equipment Corporation">DEC</a>/<a href="/wiki/Compaq" title="Compaq">Compaq</a>'s Western Research Laboratory,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> where he worked on profiling tools, microprocessor architecture, and information retrieval.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Much of his work was completed in close collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Sanjay_Ghemawat" title="Sanjay Ghemawat">Sanjay Ghemawat</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to graduate school, he worked at the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a>'s Global Programme on <a href="/wiki/AIDS" class="mw-redirect" title="AIDS">AIDS</a>, developing software for <a href="/wiki/Statistical_model" title="Statistical model">statistical modeling</a> and forecasting of the <a href="/wiki/HIV" title="HIV">HIV</a>/<a href="/wiki/AIDS" class="mw-redirect" title="AIDS">AIDS</a> <a href="/wiki/Pandemic" title="Pandemic">pandemic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dean joined Google in mid-1999, and is currently the head of its <a href="/wiki/Artificial_Intelligence" class="mw-redirect" title="Artificial Intelligence">Artificial Intelligence</a> division. While at Google, he designed and implemented large portions of the company's advertising, <a href="/wiki/Web_crawler" title="Web crawler">crawling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Web_indexing" title="Web indexing">indexing</a> and query serving systems, along with various pieces of the distributed computing infrastructure that underlies most of Google's products.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At various times, he has also worked on improving search quality, <a href="/wiki/Statistical_machine_translation" title="Statistical machine translation">statistical machine translation</a>, and various internal software development tools and has had significant involvement in the engineering hiring process.
</p><p>The projects Dean has worked on include:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Spanner_(distributed_database_technology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanner (distributed database technology)">Spanner</a>, a scalable, multi-version, globally distributed, and synchronously replicated database</li>
<li>Some of the production system design and statistical machine translation system for <a href="/wiki/Google_Translate" title="Google Translate">Google Translate</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bigtable" title="Bigtable">BigTable</a>, a large-scale semi-structured storage system<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/MapReduce" title="MapReduce">MapReduce</a>, a system for large-scale data processing applications<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/LevelDB" title="LevelDB">LevelDB</a>, an open-source on-disk key-value store</li>
<li>DistBelief, a proprietary machine-learning system for deep neural networks that was eventually refactored into TensorFlow</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/TensorFlow" title="TensorFlow">TensorFlow</a>, an open-source machine-learning software library<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>He was an early member of <a href="/wiki/Google_Brain" title="Google Brain">Google Brain</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  a team that studies large-scale artificial neural networks, and he has headed Artificial Intelligence efforts since they were split from Google Search.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  
</p><p>Dean was the subject of controversy when ethics in AI researcher <a href="/wiki/Timnit_Gebru" title="Timnit Gebru">Timnit Gebru</a> challenged Google's research review process, ultimately leading to her departure from the company. Dean responded by publishing a letter on Google's approach to the research process<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> that was the subject of further criticism and controversy.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeff_Dean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Philanthropy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dean and his wife, Heidi Hopper, started the Hopper-Dean Foundation and began making philanthropic grants in 2011. In 2016, the foundation gave $1 million each to <a href="/wiki/UC_Berkeley" class="mw-redirect" title="UC Berkeley">UC Berkeley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a> to support programs that promote diversity in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (<a href="/wiki/Science,_technology,_engineering,_and_mathematics" title="Science, technology, engineering, and mathematics">STEM</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeff_Dean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dean is married and has two daughters.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_honors">Awards and honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeff_Dean&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Awards and honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Elected to the <a href="/wiki/National_Academy_of_Engineering" title="National Academy of Engineering">National Academy of Engineering</a> (2009)</li>
<li>Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a> (2009)</li>
<li>ACM-Infosys Foundation Award<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> (2012)</li>
<li>ACM <a href="/wiki/SIGOPS_Mark_Weiser_Award" title="SIGOPS Mark Weiser Award">SIGOPS Mark Weiser Award</a> (2012)<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/American_Academy_of_Arts_and_Sciences" title="American Academy of Arts and Sciences">American Academy of Arts and Sciences</a> (2016)<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>He is widely credited within the Google corporation and in the general field of <a href="/wiki/Computer_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer Science">Computer Science</a> for his numerous contributions to the field.
</p>
