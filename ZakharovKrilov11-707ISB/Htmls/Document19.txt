<p><b>Douglas Andrew Bell</b> (born February 24, 1961<sup id="cite_ref-Amazon_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Amazon-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>)
is a <a href="/wiki/Video_game_developer" title="Video game developer">computer game developer</a>, best known for his role as the <a href="/wiki/Game_designer" class="mw-redirect" title="Game designer">lead designer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Game_programmer" class="mw-redirect" title="Game programmer">programmer</a> for the <i><a href="/wiki/Dungeon_Master_(video_game)" title="Dungeon Master (video game)">Dungeon Master</a></i> series of computer games, which met with critical success, from San Diego studio <a href="/wiki/FTL_Games" title="FTL Games">FTL Games</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-exploring_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-exploring-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work">Work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doug_Bell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Doug Bell worked as director, lead designer and developer for <i>Dungeon Master</i>. But before he joined in 1983 FTL Games, the game was titled <i>Crystal Dragon</i>, and developed together with Andy Jaros (Artwork) in their development studio PVC Dragon for the 8-bit <a href="/wiki/Apple_II" title="Apple II">Apple II</a> computer. Then after the merger, the game was rescheduled to be launched after the release, and for the target platform of the 16-bit <a href="/wiki/Atari_ST" title="Atari ST">Atari ST</a> computer, which offered more possibilities.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Bell was the <a href="/wiki/Lead_programmer" title="Lead programmer">lead developer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Technical_director" title="Technical director">technical director</a> of FTL from 1986 until 1995, the company ceased operations in 1996.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Game_credits">Game credits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Doug_Bell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Game credits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Lead programmer for the <a href="/wiki/Atari_ST" title="Atari ST">Atari ST</a> version of <i><a href="/wiki/SunDog:_Frozen_Legacy" title="SunDog: Frozen Legacy">SunDog: Frozen Legacy</a></i> (1985)</li>
<li>Lead developer for <i><a href="/wiki/Dungeon_Master_(video_game)" title="Dungeon Master (video game)">Dungeon Master</a></i> (1987) (also did the <a href="/wiki/X68000" title="X68000">X68000</a> <a href="/wiki/Porting" title="Porting">port</a> of <i>Dungeon Master</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Game_producer" class="mw-redirect" title="Game producer">Project manager</a> and developer for <i><a href="/wiki/Chaos_Strikes_Back" title="Chaos Strikes Back">Chaos Strikes Back</a></i> (1989)</li>
<li>Lead developer for <i><a href="/wiki/Dungeon_Master_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Dungeon Master II">Dungeon Master II: The Legend of Skullkeep</a></i> (1993)</li>
<li>Trion Network Platform for the 2013 title <a href="/wiki/Defiance_(video_game)" title="Defiance (video game)">Defiance</a> (Trion).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
