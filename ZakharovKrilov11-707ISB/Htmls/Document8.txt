<p><b>Marc Lowell Andreessen</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/æ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;bad&#39;">æ</span><span title="&#39;n&#39; in &#39;nigh&#39;">n</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;d&#39; in &#39;dye&#39;">d</span><span title="&#39;r&#39; in &#39;rye&#39;">r</span><span title="/iː/: &#39;ee&#39; in &#39;fleece&#39;">iː</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span><span title="/ən/: &#39;on&#39; in &#39;button&#39;">ən</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling">ann-<span style="font-size:90%">DREE</span>-sən</i></a>; born July 9, 1971) is an American <a href="/wiki/Entrepreneur" class="mw-redirect" title="Entrepreneur">entrepreneur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Investor" title="Investor">investor</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Software_engineer" title="Software engineer">software engineer</a>. He is the co-author of <a href="/wiki/Mosaic_(web_browser)" title="Mosaic (web browser)">Mosaic</a>, the first widely used <a href="/wiki/Web_browser" title="Web browser">web browser</a>; co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Netscape" title="Netscape">Netscape</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-co-founder_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-co-founder-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and co-founder and general partner of <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley" title="Silicon Valley">Silicon Valley</a> <a href="/wiki/Venture_capital" title="Venture capital">venture capital</a> firm <a href="/wiki/Andreessen_Horowitz" title="Andreessen Horowitz">Andreessen Horowitz</a>. He co-founded and later sold the <a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a> company <a href="/wiki/Opsware" title="Opsware">Opsware</a> to <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a>. Andreessen is also a co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Ning_(website)" title="Ning (website)">Ning</a>, a company that provides a platform for social networking websites.  He sits on the board of directors of Facebook. Andreessen was one of six inductees in the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web_Hall_of_Fame" class="mw-redirect" title="World Wide Web Hall of Fame">World Wide Web Hall of Fame</a> announced at the <a href="/wiki/First_International_Conference_on_the_World-Wide_Web" title="First International Conference on the World-Wide Web">First International Conference on the World-Wide Web</a> in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Andreessen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andreessen was born in <a href="/wiki/Cedar_Falls,_Iowa" title="Cedar Falls, Iowa">Cedar Falls, Iowa</a>, and raised in <a href="/wiki/New_Lisbon,_Wisconsin" title="New Lisbon, Wisconsin">New Lisbon, Wisconsin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-simonebio_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-simonebio-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He is the son of Patricia and Lowell Andreessen, who worked for a seed company.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In December 1993,<sup id="cite_ref-simonebio_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-simonebio-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> he received his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor&#39;s degree">bachelor's degree</a> in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Illinois_at_Urbana%E2%80%93Champaign" title="University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign">University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  As an undergraduate, he interned twice at <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> in <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He worked in the AIX graphics software development group which was responsible for the MIT <a href="/wiki/X_Window_System" title="X Window System">X Window</a> implementation and ports of the 3D language APIs:  SGI's Graphics Language (GL) and PHIGS.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  He also worked at the <a href="/wiki/National_Center_for_Supercomputing_Applications" title="National Center for Supercomputing Applications">National Center for Supercomputing Applications</a> (NCSA) at the University of Illinois, where he became familiar with <a href="/wiki/Tim_Berners-Lee" title="Tim Berners-Lee">Tim Berners-Lee</a>'s open standards for the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web" title="World Wide Web">World Wide Web</a>.  Andreessen and full-time salaried co-worker <a href="/wiki/Eric_Bina" title="Eric Bina">Eric Bina</a> worked on creating a user-friendly browser with integrated graphics that would work on a wide range of computers. The resulting code was the Mosaic Web browser.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r996844942">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>In the Web's first generation, Tim Berners-Lee launched the Uniform Resource Locator (URL), Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP), and HTML standards with prototype Unix-based servers and browsers. A few people noticed that the Web might be better than <a href="/wiki/Gopher_(protocol)" title="Gopher (protocol)">Gopher</a>. In the second generation, Marc Andreessen and Eric Bina developed NCSA Mosaic at the University of Illinois. Several million then suddenly noticed that the Web might be better than sex.</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8201;<cite>Bob Metcalfe, <i>InfoWorld</i>, August 21, 1995, Vol. 17, Issue 34.<sup id="cite_ref-Web_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Web-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></cite></div></blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Netscape">Netscape</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Andreessen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Netscape">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After his graduation from UIUC in 1993, Andreessen moved to <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> to work at Enterprise Integration Technologies. Andreessen then met with <a href="/wiki/James_H._Clark" title="James H. Clark">Jim Clark</a>, the founder of <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Graphics" title="Silicon Graphics">Silicon Graphics</a>, who had recently exited the firm.  Clark believed the Mosaic browser had great commercial possibilities and suggested starting an Internet software company.  Soon, <a href="/wiki/Mosaic_Communications_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Mosaic Communications Corporation">Mosaic Communications Corporation</a> was in business in <a href="/wiki/Mountain_View,_California" title="Mountain View, California">Mountain View, California</a>, with Andreessen as co-founder and vice president of technology. The University of Illinois was unhappy with the company's use of the Mosaic name, so Mosaic Communications changed its name to Netscape Communications, and its flagship Web browser was the <a href="/wiki/Netscape_Navigator" title="Netscape Navigator">Netscape Navigator</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Netscape's <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">IPO</a> in 1995 put Andreessen into the public eye. He was featured on the cover of <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and other publications.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Netscape was acquired in 1999 for $4.3 billion by <a href="/wiki/America_Online" class="mw-redirect" title="America Online">AOL</a>. Andreessen's hiring as its <a href="/wiki/Chief_technical_officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Chief technical officer">Chief Technology Officer</a> was contingent on the completion of the acquisition.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The same year, he was named to the <i><a href="/wiki/MIT_Technology_Review" title="MIT Technology Review">MIT Technology Review</a></i> <a href="/wiki/TR35" class="mw-redirect" title="TR35">TR100</a> as one of the top 100 innovators in the world under the age of 35.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Loudcloud">Loudcloud</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Andreessen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Loudcloud">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After AOL acquired Netscape in late 1998, Andreessen went on to found Opsware with <a href="/wiki/Ben_Horowitz" title="Ben Horowitz">Ben Horowitz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tim_Howes" title="Tim Howes">Tim Howes</a>, and In Sik Rhee,.<sup id="cite_ref-CNET_Loudcloud_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNET_Loudcloud-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Originally named Loudcloud, the company provided computing, hosting and software services to consumer facing internet and e-commerce companies. Loudcloud sold its hosting business to <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Data_Systems" title="Electronic Data Systems">EDS</a> and changed its name to <a href="/wiki/Opsware" title="Opsware">Opsware</a> in 2003, with Andreessen serving as chairman. Acquired by Hewlett-Packard for $1.6 billion in 2007, it was one of the first companies to offer <a href="/wiki/Software_as_a_service" title="Software as a service">software as a service</a> and to attempt cloud hosting.<sup id="cite_ref-pcworld_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcworld-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Andreessen_Horowitz">Andreessen Horowitz</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Andreessen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Andreessen Horowitz">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_founding">History and founding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Andreessen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History and founding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Between 2005 and 2009, Andreessen and longtime business partner <a href="/wiki/Ben_Horowitz" title="Ben Horowitz">Ben Horowitz</a> separately invested a total of $80 million in 45 start-ups that included <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Qik" class="mw-redirect" title="Qik">Qik</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-UCLA_Engineering_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UCLA_Engineering-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>  The two became well known as <a href="/wiki/Super_angel" title="Super angel">super angel</a> investors.<sup id="cite_ref-UCLA_Engineering_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UCLA_Engineering-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On July 6, 2009, Andreessen and Horowitz announced their Silicon Valley venture-capital firm Andreessen Horowitz.<sup id="cite_ref-CNNMoney_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNNMoney-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The firm had been scrutinized among several other venture capital firms for lack of diversity in its workforce.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with New York Magazine, Andreessen stated the diversity discussion was valid, however, he believed the firm, as well as other venture capital firms of Silicon Valley, had been wrongly accused of intentionally discriminating against women and people of color.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> When asked specifically about the critique of ethnic and gender diversity in Silicon Valley, Andreessen responded that the issues were the "same thing."<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Investments">Investments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Andreessen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Investments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Begun with an initial capitalization of $300 million,<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_2.1.2012_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ_2.1.2012-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> within three years the firm grew to $2.7 billion under management across three funds.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_4.6.11_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ_4.6.11-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Andreessen Horowitz's portfolio holdings include <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foursquare_(company)" title="Foursquare (company)">Foursquare</a>, <a href="/wiki/GitHub" title="GitHub">GitHub</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pinterest" title="Pinterest">Pinterest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>, and Honor, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-MarketWatch_Release_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MarketWatch_Release-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 1, 2009, an investor group that included Andreessen Horowitz acquired a majority stake in <a href="/wiki/Skype" title="Skype">Skype</a> for $2.75 billion,<sup id="cite_ref-TechCrunch_11.19.09_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TechCrunch_11.19.09-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> which was considered risky.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_Deal_Journal_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ_Deal_Journal-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The deal paid off in May 2011 when <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> bought Skype for $8.5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_Deal_Journal_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ_Deal_Journal-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, Andreessen and Horowitz made personal investments in headset maker <a href="/wiki/Jawbone_(headset)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jawbone (headset)">Jawbone</a> in 2006. In 2010, the firm assisted Silicon Valley attorney <a href="/wiki/Ted_Wang" title="Ted Wang">Ted Wang</a> in creating the first free standardized seed round financing documents, the Series Seed Documents.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The firm announced a $49 million investment in Jawbone in March 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_Dealbook_3-16-11_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_Dealbook_3-16-11-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="eBay">eBay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Andreessen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: eBay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andreessen first joined the eBay board of directors in 2008, and served on it for six years.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> In October 2014, Andreessen announced his resignation from the board due to the company's decision to break off its online payments unit PayPal. The decision to cut ties with PayPal was a point of contention between Andreessen and investor <a href="/wiki/Carl_Icahn" title="Carl Icahn">Carl Icahn</a>. Icahn advocated for the PayPal split while Andreessen opposed the spin off, resulting in public disputes. Andreessen was accused by Icahn of putting his own interests in front of what was best for shareholders. Icahn published his argument in an open letter that detailed alleged conflicts of interest in eBay's 2009 sale of Skype to a group of private investors, which included Andreessen's own firm.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry_influence">Industry influence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Andreessen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Industry influence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andreessen advises the leaders of companies in which Andreessen Horowitz invests, including <a href="/wiki/Mark_Zuckerberg" title="Mark Zuckerberg">Mark Zuckerberg</a> of Facebook and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Pincus" title="Mark Pincus">Mark Pincus</a> of Zynga.<sup id="cite_ref-Economist_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Economist-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Andreessen and Horowitz were ranked no. six on <i><a href="/wiki/Vanity_Fair_(magazine)" title="Vanity Fair (magazine)">Vanity Fair</a>'s</i> 2011 New Establishment List,<sup id="cite_ref-Vanity_Fair_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vanity_Fair-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> no. 1 on <a href="/wiki/CNET" title="CNET">CNET</a>'s 2011 most influential investors list<sup id="cite_ref-CNET_Investors_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNET_Investors-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> and no. 2 and 21, respectively, on the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a> <a href="/wiki/Midas_List" title="Midas List">Midas List</a> of Tech's Top Investors.<sup id="cite_ref-Midas_List_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Midas_List-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2012, Andreessen and Andreessen Horowitz General Partners Ben Horowitz, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Levine_(venture_capitalist)" class="mw-redirect" title="Peter Levine (venture capitalist)">Peter Levine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Jordan_(venture_capitalist)" title="Jeff Jordan (venture capitalist)">Jeff Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_O%27Farrell_(venture_capitalist)" title="John O&#39;Farrell (venture capitalist)">John O'Farrell</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Scott_Weiss" title="Scott Weiss">Scott Weiss</a> pledged to donate half of their lifetime incomes from venture capital to charitable organizations.<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Andreessen was named in the <a href="/wiki/Time_100" title="Time 100">Time 100</a>, an annual list of the 100 most influential people in the world assembled by <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Time2012_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Time2012-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Andreessen was one of five Internet and Web pioneers awarded the inaugural <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_Prize_for_Engineering" title="Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering">Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2020, early in the COVID-19 pandemic, Andreessen published an opinion article, "IT’S TIME TO BUILD," describing America's terrible coronavirus response and suggesting technological and cultural solutions to the problem.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ventures">Ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Andreessen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andreessen cofounded and was chairman of Ning, the third company he established after Netscape and Loudcloud.<sup id="cite_ref-Marc_Blog_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marc_Blog-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> In September 2011, it was announced that Ning had been sold to <a href="/wiki/Mode_Media" title="Mode Media">Mode Media</a> for a reported price of $150 million. Andreessen joined Glam Media's board of directors following the sale.<sup id="cite_ref-Biz_Insider_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biz_Insider-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is a personal investor in companies including <a href="/wiki/LinkedIn" title="LinkedIn">LinkedIn</a><sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_Software_Eating_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ_Software_Eating-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> and boutique bank Raine.<sup id="cite_ref-Biz_Insider_Raine_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Biz_Insider_Raine-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Andreessen serves on the board of <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-co-founder_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-co-founder-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hewlett_Packard_Enterprise" title="Hewlett Packard Enterprise">Hewlett Packard Enterprise</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kno" title="Kno">Kno</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-BizWeek_Profile_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BizWeek_Profile-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Stanford_Hospital_and_Clinics" class="mw-redirect" title="Stanford Hospital and Clinics">Stanford Hospital</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Stanford_Hospital_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanford_Hospital-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bump_(application)" title="Bump (application)">Bump Technologies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anki_(company)" title="Anki (company)">Anki</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-TC:_Debuts_at_WWDC_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TC:_Debuts_at_WWDC-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Oculus_VR" class="mw-redirect" title="Oculus VR">Oculus VR</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Dialpad, and <a href="/wiki/TinyCo" title="TinyCo">TinyCo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hewlett Packard Enterprise announced in February 2018 that board member Andreessen would not seek reelection at the 2018 Annual Meeting of Stockholders on April 4.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> In his time at Hewlett Packard, Andreessen had been partially blamed for some of the company's failures, including the recruiting of <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A9o_Apotheker" title="Léo Apotheker">Leo Apotheker</a> as well as the acquisitions of <a href="/wiki/HP_Autonomy" title="HP Autonomy">Autonomy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Palm,_Inc." title="Palm, Inc.">Palm</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He serves as advisor to <a href="/wiki/Asana_(web_application)" class="mw-redirect" title="Asana (web application)">Asana</a> and director of <a href="/wiki/CollabNet" title="CollabNet">CollabNet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dialpad_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dialpad-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Andreessen is a proponent of <a href="/wiki/Bitcoin" title="Bitcoin">Bitcoin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cryptocurrency" title="Cryptocurrency">cryptocurrency</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Andreessen serves on an advisory board for <a href="/wiki/Neom" title="Neom">Neom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>’s plan to build a futuristic "mega city" in the desert.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Andreessen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andreessen married <a href="/wiki/Laura_Arrillaga-Andreessen" title="Laura Arrillaga-Andreessen">Laura Arrillaga</a> in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> She is the founder of the Silicon Valley Social Venture Fund and daughter of Silicon Valley real estate billionaire <a href="/wiki/John_Arrillaga" title="John Arrillaga">John Arrillaga</a>. They have one son together.
</p><p>In 2012, Andreessen expressed some support for Republican candidate <a href="/wiki/Mitt_Romney" title="Mitt Romney">Mitt Romney</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> During the 2016 primary season, he endorsed Republican candidate <a href="/wiki/Carly_Fiorina" title="Carly Fiorina">Carly Fiorina</a>, but after Fiorina dropped out of the race, Andreessen switched his endorsement to the Democratic nominee <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">Hillary Clinton</a>, citing the Republican nominee <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a>'s anti-immigration stance.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Andreessen has a chapter giving advice in <a href="/wiki/Tim_Ferriss" title="Tim Ferriss">Tim Ferriss</a>' book <a href="/wiki/Tools_of_Titans" title="Tools of Titans">Tools of Titans</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Andreessen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Twitter">Twitter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Andreessen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Twitter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2016, Marc Andreessen posted a tweet in response to India's decision to apply <a href="/wiki/Net_neutrality" title="Net neutrality">net neutrality</a> to Facebook's proposed project <a href="/wiki/Free_Basics" class="mw-redirect" title="Free Basics">Free Basics</a>. The tweet suggested that anti-colonialism had been catastrophic for the Indian people. Andreessen later deleted the tweet following criticism from Indians and non-Indians alike (including Facebook founder <a href="/wiki/Mark_Zuckerberg" title="Mark Zuckerberg">Mark Zuckerberg</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-dc_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dc-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> Facebook spent millions advertising Free Basics to the Indian public.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> The project failed due to violations, setting preferential tariffs in accessing content and setting up a <a href="/wiki/Closed_platform" title="Closed platform">"walled garden"</a> on the internet.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Andreessen was a supporter for the controversial British journalist <a href="/wiki/Milo_Yiannopoulos" title="Milo Yiannopoulos">Milo Yiannopoulos</a>, whose verified status was revoked by <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> in 2016 for abusive behavior and hateful conduct, growing into an ongoing controversy. Marc Andreessen tweeted in support of Yiannopoulos.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="referenced article doesn&#39;t say Andreessen supported Milo, just that he tweeted about it. It links to some random Medium blog that is not an RS (April 2019)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Conflict_of_interest">Conflict of interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Andreessen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Conflict of interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2016, Facebook shareholders filed a class action lawsuit to block Zuckerberg's plan to create a new class of non-voting shares. The lawsuit alleges Andreessen secretly coached Zuckerberg through a process to win board approval for the stock change, while Andreessen served as an independent board member representing stockholders.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to court documents, Andreessen shared information with Zuckerberg regarding their progress and concerns as well as helping Zuckerberg negotiate against shareholders. Court documents included transcripts of private texts between Zuckerberg and Andreessen.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
