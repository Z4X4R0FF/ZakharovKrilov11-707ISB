<p><b>Scott Adams</b> (born July 10, 1952) is an American entrepreneur, computer programmer, and video game designer. He co-founded, with ex-wife Alexis, <a href="/wiki/Adventure_International" title="Adventure International">Adventure International</a> in 1979. The company developed and published <a href="/wiki/Video_games" class="mw-redirect" title="Video games">video games</a> for <a href="/wiki/Home_computers" class="mw-redirect" title="Home computers">home computers</a>. The cornerstone products of Adventure International in its early years were the <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_Scott_Adams_Adventure_video_games" title="List of Scott Adams Adventure video games">Adventure</a></i> series of <a href="/wiki/Text_adventures" class="mw-redirect" title="Text adventures">text adventures</a> written by Adams.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Adventure_International">Adventure International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Adams_(game_designer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Adventure International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, Adams had access to an advanced 16-bit computer at home, built by his brother <a href="/wiki/Richard_Adams_(inventor)" title="Richard Adams (inventor)">Richard Adams</a>, that gave him a jump on game programming in his leisure time.<sup id="cite_ref-16bit_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16bit-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Adams wrote a graphical action game similar to <i><a href="/wiki/Spacewar!" title="Spacewar!">Spacewar!</a></i> on this system in 1975.
</p><p>Scott Adams was the first person known to create an <a href="/wiki/Adventure_game" title="Adventure game">adventure-style game</a> for personal computers,<sup id="cite_ref-adventure_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adventure-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> in 1978 on a 16 <a href="/wiki/Kilobyte" title="Kilobyte">KB</a> <a href="/wiki/RadioShack" title="RadioShack">Radio Shack</a> <a href="/wiki/TRS-80" title="TRS-80">TRS-80</a> Model I, written in  <a href="/wiki/BASIC" title="BASIC">BASIC</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/Colossal_Cave_Adventure" title="Colossal Cave Adventure">Colossal Cave</a></i> was written two years earlier by <a href="/wiki/William_Crowther_(programmer)" title="William Crowther (programmer)">Will Crowther</a>, but on a mainframe computer (the <a href="/wiki/PDP-10" title="PDP-10">PDP-10</a>). These early <a href="/wiki/Text_adventure" class="mw-redirect" title="Text adventure">text adventures</a> recognize two-word commands of the form VERB NOUN. The parser only scans the first three letters of each command, so SCREAM BEAR, SCRATCH BEAR, or SCREW BEAR are treated identically.<sup id="cite_ref-adventure_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adventure-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The games from his company, <a href="/wiki/Adventure_International" title="Adventure International">Adventure International</a>, were subsequently released on most of the major home computers of the day, including <a href="/wiki/TRS-80" title="TRS-80">TRS-80</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apple_II" title="Apple II">Apple II</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atari_8-bit_family" title="Atari 8-bit family">Atari 8-bit family</a>, <a href="/wiki/Commodore_PET" title="Commodore PET">Commodore PET</a>, <a href="/wiki/Commodore_VIC-20" title="Commodore VIC-20">VIC-20</a>, and <a href="/wiki/ZX_Spectrum" title="ZX Spectrum">ZX Spectrum</a>. Later adventure games added graphics, with the text entry window below an image illustrating the scene.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Adams's work was  influential in adventure gaming. In 1990 <i><a href="/wiki/Computer_Gaming_World" title="Computer Gaming World">Computer Gaming World</a></i> reported a statement  by a "respected designer" that it was impossible to design new and more difficult adventure puzzles, because Adams had already created them all in his early games.<sup id="cite_ref-cgw199010_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cgw199010-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_work">Later work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Adams_(game_designer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Later work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since the late 1980s, Scott Adams has worked as a senior programmer for AVISTA in <a href="/wiki/Platteville,_Wisconsin" title="Platteville, Wisconsin">Platteville, Wisconsin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Scott Adams released the <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a>-based <i>The Inheritance</i>, his first game in over ten years. As in most of his other games, the player is  the protagonist of a novel-like story, and helps events unfold in a text adventure setting.  However, this new game also includes sound.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In a 2018 interview Adams said he had not been happy with the game as it was released so had withdrawn it from the market whilst it was rewritten.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July, 2016 Adams created a new company called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Clopas_(video_game_studio)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Clopas (video game studio) (page does not exist)">Clopas</a> to begin publishing games in a new genre he called "Conversational Adventure Games".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> These games were similar to his early text adventure games, except that they now supported full natural language sentences.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The first game he put out under this company was <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lost_Legends_of_Redwall#Episodes" title="The Lost Legends of Redwall">Escape The Gloomer</a></i>, a game published in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Soma_Games" title="Soma Games">Soma Games</a> for their <a href="/wiki/The_Lost_Legends_of_Redwall" title="The Lost Legends of Redwall"><i>Lost Legends of Redwall</i></a> series.
</p>
