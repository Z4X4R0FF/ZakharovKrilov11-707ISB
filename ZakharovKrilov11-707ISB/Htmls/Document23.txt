<p><b>Eric J. Bina</b> (born October 1964) is an American software programmer who is the co-creator of <a href="/wiki/Mosaic_(web_browser)" title="Mosaic (web browser)">Mosaic</a> and the co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Netscape_Communications_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Netscape Communications Corporation">Netscape</a>. In 1993, Bina along with <a href="/wiki/Marc_Andreessen" title="Marc Andreessen">Marc Andreessen</a> authored the first version of Mosaic while working as a <a href="/wiki/Programmer" title="Programmer">programmer</a> at <a href="/wiki/National_Center_for_Supercomputing_Applications" title="National Center for Supercomputing Applications">National Center for Supercomputing Applications</a> (NCSA) at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Illinois_at_Urbana-Champaign" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign">University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign</a>.
</p><p>Bina attended the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, graduating from there with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">Bachelor of Science</a> degree in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">Computer Science</a> in 1986 and a <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master&#39;s degree">master's degree</a> in 1988. He joined NCSA in 1991 as a programmer. There, Bina and Andreessen started working on Mosaic in December 1992 and had a working version by March 1993. Mosaic was posted to the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a> and is famed as the first <a href="/wiki/Killer_application" title="Killer application">killer application</a> that popularized the Internet. He is one of the five inaugural inductees to the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web_Hall_of_Fame" class="mw-redirect" title="World Wide Web Hall of Fame">World Wide Web Hall of Fame</a> announced at the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web_Conference_1" class="mw-redirect" title="World Wide Web Conference 1">first international conference on the World Wide Web</a> in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1995, Bina and <a href="/wiki/Marc_Andreessen" title="Marc Andreessen">Andreessen</a> were awarded the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">ACM</a> <a href="/wiki/Software_System_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Software System Award">Software System Award</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-acmaward_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acmaward-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, Bina and Andreessen were inducted into the University of Illinois Engineering Hall of Fame.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
